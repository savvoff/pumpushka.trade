## Overview

Ethereum is on the brink of a significant transformation with the anticipated introduction of native smart accounts. This feature, which has been under development for over a decade, is set to be part of the network's forthcoming Hegota upgrade. Co-founder Vitalik Buterin has emphasized the importance of this upgrade, particularly for privacy-focused users.

## Upcoming Changes in Ethereum's Functionality

The introduction of native smart accounts represents a pivotal shift in how Ethereum users will interact with the blockchain. Vitalik Buterin has pointed out that this feature will eliminate the need for intermediaries, known as "public broadcasters," which have historically facilitated transactions. This change aims to enhance user control by replacing the intermediary system with a general-purpose public memory pool. 

Buterin's commitment to minimizing intermediaries reflects a deeper ethos within the Ethereum community, emphasizing self-sufficiency and resilience. He articulated that the goal is to maximize user capabilities, even in scenarios where external infrastructure may fail.

The concept of account abstraction has been a topic of discussion since early 2016, with the original proposal outlined in EIP-86. Now, with the introduction of EIP-8141 as part of the Hegota upgrade, the Ethereum development team aims to resolve the remaining issues related to account abstraction. 

## Technical Innovations

The Hegota upgrade will incorporate a modular design called "frame transactions." In this innovative approach, a transaction will no longer be a singular action but rather a series of interconnected frames. Each frame can reference the data of another, allowing for multiple authorizations, including those for senders and gas payers. This structure will enable users to pay transaction fees using tokens other than ETH, facilitated through a paymaster contract or a specialized exchange that provides ETH when needed.

Additionally, the Hegota upgrade will not solely focus on smart accounts. Buterin has also introduced a quantum resistance roadmap, identifying critical areas such as validator signatures, data storage, user account signatures, and zero-knowledge proofs. The upgrade aims to ensure that existing accounts will transition smoothly into the new framework, gaining enhanced functionalities like batch operations and transaction sponsorship.

## From Author

The developments surrounding the Hegota upgrade signify a concerted effort by the Ethereum team to enhance user experience and broaden the functionality of the network. By prioritizing self-sufficiency and reducing reliance on intermediaries, Ethereum aims to empower its users, particularly those who value privacy. The introduction of frame transactions and the quantum resistance roadmap reflects a forward-thinking approach that addresses both current and future challenges in the blockchain space.

## Impact on the Crypto Market

- The elimination of intermediaries could lead to greater user autonomy and security within the Ethereum ecosystem.
- Enhanced privacy features may attract more users to Ethereum, particularly those focused on confidentiality in transactions.
- The ability to pay transaction fees in various tokens could broaden the appeal of Ethereum to a wider audience, facilitating more diverse use cases.
- A successful implementation of quantum resistance may bolster confidence in Ethereum's long-term viability and security.
- The modular design of frame transactions could inspire other blockchain projects to adopt similar structures, potentially influencing industry standards.
