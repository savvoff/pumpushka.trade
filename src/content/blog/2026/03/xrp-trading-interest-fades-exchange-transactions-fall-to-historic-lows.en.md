## Overview

Recent developments indicate a significant drop in XRP trading interest, with exchange transaction counts hitting historic lows. While XRP's price has stabilized around $1.40, on-chain data suggests a notable decrease in market participation, raising concerns about the asset's future performance.

## XRP Trading Activity Declines

XRP is currently trading near $1.40 following a week characterized by volatility among major cryptocurrencies. Despite this stabilization in price, data from a CryptoQuant analyst highlights a significant decline in trading activity across centralized exchanges. Specifically, the number of XRP deposits and withdrawals has fallen to the lowest levels since the metric began tracking exchange behavior. This metric, known as the Multi Exchanges Daily Depositing and Withdrawing Transactions Delta, measures the net number of XRP transfer transactions across 15 major cryptocurrency exchanges.

Unlike traditional metrics that consider the volume of coins moving on and off exchanges, this dataset focuses on the sheer number of transactions. This distinction is crucial as it provides insights into user behavior and engagement rather than just the capital size involved. The recent downturn in transaction counts suggests a slowdown in user-driven exchange activity, indicating that traders are stepping back from short-term speculation while awaiting clearer price direction.

## Understanding the Market Context

The decline in XRP exchange activity should be interpreted within a broader market framework. The metric's movements indicate shifts in investor behavior. A sharp rise in transaction counts typically signals that more users are sending XRP to exchanges than withdrawing it, often leading to increased selling pressure as traders prepare for potential liquidation. Conversely, a decline in transactions suggests that investors are withdrawing XRP into private wallets, which aligns with accumulation phases, indicating reduced selling intentions.

Recent data underscores a pronounced decline in the number of XRP deposit and withdrawal transactions, leading to a quieter market environment. This trend is further complicated by the fact that XRP has decreased significantly from its previous highs, which has likely diminished retail participation in the market.

The last major spike in XRP exchange deposits occurred in January 2025, when the asset approached the $3 level. Since then, Binance has remained the primary exchange driving transaction activity.

## Technical Analysis of XRP

XRP's price continues to hover around the $1.40 level, following a prolonged correction that has defined its price structure. The daily chart indicates an attempt at stabilization after a significant sell-off that saw prices drop from above $2.30 to the $1.20–$1.30 range earlier this year. The overall technical structure remains bearish, with XRP consistently trading below its major moving averages, including the 50-day, 100-day, and 200-day trends, all of which are currently sloping downward.

Recent price movements suggest that the $1.30–$1.35 zone is acting as short-term support. After a capitulation event in February that briefly drove XRP near the $1.20 area, buyers have stepped in to push the asset back toward the $1.40 region. However, attempts for upward movement remain limited, with the declining 50-day moving average near $1.60 acting as a key resistance level.
