## Overview

X, the social media platform, has issued a warning to its creators regarding the posting of AI-generated war videos. The head of product at X, Nikita Bier, emphasized that any creators who fail to disclose the nature of such content will face consequences, specifically the loss of access to the platform's revenue-sharing program.

## What Happened

The announcement from X comes in response to concerns about the ethical implications and potential misinformation associated with AI-generated content, particularly in sensitive areas such as war. Nikita Bier's statement highlights a proactive approach by X to maintain the integrity of its platform and protect its users from misleading information.

By enforcing a policy that requires creators to disclose whether their videos are AI-generated, X aims to promote transparency and accountability. The decision underscores the platform's commitment to ensuring that its revenue-sharing program is not exploited by creators who may produce content that could mislead viewers or distort reality.

This warning serves as a reminder of the growing complexities that social media platforms face in managing user-generated content, especially as advancements in AI technology continue to evolve. The ability to create realistic-looking videos using AI raises significant ethical questions, prompting platforms like X to take a stand.
