## Overview

Ethereum is currently trading just above the critical psychological level of $2,000. However, recent technical analysis suggests that this apparent stabilization may be misleading, as it could indicate a counter-trend correction rather than a genuine recovery.

## Analysis of Current Market Conditions

According to a technical analysis by a crypto analyst, the ongoing crypto winter is still a significant factor affecting Ethereum's price movements. The analysis highlights that the price of Ethereum has recently printed a series of lower highs and lower lows, indicating a bearish trend. Following a rejection around a previous high, the price dropped to a zone between $1,960 and $1,990, which shows that sellers remain in control of the market. This pattern has led to what is characterized as a counter-trend correction, a phenomenon where prices may appear to recover temporarily while still being trapped in a broader bearish structure.

The current situation is further complicated by the performance of Bitcoin. Bitcoin appeared to be staging a recovery attempt but has since reversed its gains, impacting altcoins like Ethereum. The weakness in Bitcoin is anticipated to exert additional pressure on Ethereum's price, suggesting that the altcoin may experience heightened selling pressure in the upcoming days.

### Price Levels and Resistance

On the 2-hour chart, there is a notable resistance cluster between $2,024 and $2,062. This resistance zone is supported by various technical indicators, including previous support levels that have turned into resistance, Fibonacci retracement levels, and a descending trendline that has been pressing down on lower highs throughout March. 

The analysis indicates that Ethereum may test liquidity zones ranging from $2,025 to $2,062. A potential short squeeze could provide a signal for a further decline. The critical resistance levels to monitor include $2,025, $2,037, and $2,062. The most significant point of interest is at $2,062.50. If Ethereum retests this resistance zone and experiences a false breakout followed by consolidation, it could confirm the dominance of bearish sentiment in the market.

Should this bearish confirmation occur, it may lead to increased selling pressure, pushing Ethereum's price down to a support level around $1,900. At the time of the analysis, Ethereum was trading at $2,050.
