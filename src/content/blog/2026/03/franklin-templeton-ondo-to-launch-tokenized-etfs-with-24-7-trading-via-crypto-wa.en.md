## Overview

Franklin Templeton and Ondo are set to introduce tokenized exchange-traded funds (ETFs) that will enable 24/7 trading via crypto wallets. Initially, these funds will be available to non-US investors, providing a novel way to gain onchain exposure to various asset classes, including equities, bonds, and gold.

## What Happened

Franklin Templeton, a prominent investment firm, is collaborating with Ondo, a financial technology company, to create tokenized ETFs. These ETFs are designed to facilitate trading around the clock, leveraging the capabilities of crypto wallets. This initiative allows investors to engage with traditional asset classes in a digital format, enhancing accessibility and liquidity.

The tokenized ETFs will offer exposure to a diverse range of assets, including equities, bonds, and gold. By utilizing blockchain technology, these funds aim to streamline the investment process, making it more efficient and transparent. The decision to initially target non-US investors may be influenced by regulatory considerations, as the landscape for cryptocurrency and digital assets continues to evolve in various jurisdictions.

This move by Franklin Templeton and Ondo is significant as it represents a growing trend of traditional financial institutions exploring the integration of blockchain technology and digital assets into their offerings. The use of crypto wallets for trading provides a modern approach to investing, aligning with the increasing demand for flexibility and accessibility in the financial markets.
