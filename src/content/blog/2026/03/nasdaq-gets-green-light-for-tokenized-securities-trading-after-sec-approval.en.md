## Overview

The US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has approved a significant rule change that allows Nasdaq, one of the largest stock exchanges globally, to engage in trading tokenized securities. This decision marks a pivotal moment in the integration of blockchain technology within mainstream financial markets.

## Nasdaq's Rule Amendments Approved

On Wednesday, the SEC announced its approval of Nasdaq's modified regulations, which followed a thorough seven-month evaluation process initiated in September 2025. This assessment aimed to ensure that Nasdaq's operations would comply with federal securities laws and meet investor protection standards. Tokenized securities are essentially blockchain-based representations of traditional financial instruments, such as stocks, bonds, or funds. The ownership rights for these instruments are recorded as digital tokens on a distributed ledger, facilitating a more modern approach to trading.

Proponents of tokenization assert that this technology can revolutionize trading by enabling 24/7 trading hours, expediting settlement processes, and allowing for fractional ownership. These attributes could significantly modernize aspects of market infrastructure that have traditionally depended on outdated systems.

According to the SEC's filing, the approved pilot program for Nasdaq will work in conjunction with the Depository Trust Company (DTC). This collaboration is designed to provide a regulated framework for market participants, allowing them to trade these digital representations of securities in a compliant manner.

## Cross-Border Rails For Tokenized Securities

The SEC's approval is a crucial step forward for various industry initiatives already underway. Earlier this month, Payward, the parent company of the cryptocurrency exchange Kraken, announced a partnership with Nasdaq to develop an equities transformation gateway. This project combines Nasdaq's regulated market infrastructure with Kraken's xStocks framework. The goal is to enable the seamless movement of tokenized equities between controlled institutional environments and decentralized finance (DeFi) networks.

Additionally, Nasdaq revealed a partnership with Boerse Stuttgart Group’s tokenized settlement platform, Seturion. This alliance aims to connect Nasdaq's European trading venues with a settlement infrastructure specifically tailored for tokenized securities.
