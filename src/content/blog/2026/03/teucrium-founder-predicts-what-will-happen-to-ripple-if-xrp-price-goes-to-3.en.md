## Overview

The conversation around Ripple's potential transition into a fully licensed bank has intensified, especially following remarks from Teucrium CEO Sal Gilbertie. He discussed the implications of this shift on Ripple's holdings and the price of XRP, suggesting a significant transformation in the crypto landscape if Ripple secures a banking license.

## Ripple's Potential Transformation

In a recent discussion, Sal Gilbertie highlighted the substantial holdings of Ripple and the potential impact on the XRP price if the company were to obtain a full banking license. Analysts are now evaluating what a rise in XRP to $3 could mean for Ripple’s positioning in the global banking sector. According to Gilbertie, if Ripple secures this license, it could ascend to the ranks of the top 20 banks globally by market capitalization.

Gilbertie elaborated on a scenario where Ripple’s existing holdings of 40 million XRP, currently held in escrow, could significantly enhance its balance sheet value. This strategic asset could provide Ripple with a competitive advantage in a regulated banking environment. The potential rise in XRP price, as suggested by Gilbertie, could not only elevate Ripple’s market position but also transform it into a leading financial institution.

During the interview, the discussion also touched on the broader implications of digital assets in traditional financial systems. Gilbertie expressed confidence that all financial instruments would eventually operate on-chain, indicating a future where traditional finance is fully integrated with blockchain technology.

## Ripple's Banking License Status

Ripple is actively pursuing regulatory pathways to achieve a banking-like authority in the United States. The company has already received conditional preliminary approval from the United States Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) for a national trust bank charter. This development aligns Ripple with a select group of crypto firms that are also working towards becoming regulated banks under U.S. law.

Although Ripple has not yet received full approval, the company is making strides in enhancing its payment infrastructure through various acquisitions, partnerships, and share buybacks. In the meantime, XRP, Ripple's primary token, has experienced fluctuations in price, recently trading at $1.43 after a rally earlier in the week.
