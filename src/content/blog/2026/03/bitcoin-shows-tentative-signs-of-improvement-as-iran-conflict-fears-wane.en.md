## What Happened

The relationship between oil and Bitcoin is often scrutinized, as fluctuations in oil prices can impact investor sentiment across various asset classes, including cryptocurrencies.

Renewed inflows into Bitcoin ETFs signal a growing interest from institutional investors and retail participants alike. This trend may suggest that confidence in Bitcoin is slowly returning, as these financial products provide a regulated avenue for investing in the cryptocurrency.

On-chain data, which refers to information recorded on the blockchain, indicates that the overall stress in the crypto market might be easing. This data can include various metrics such as transaction volumes, active addresses, and network activity, all of which can provide insights into market health.

The combination of these factors—oil's volatility, increased Bitcoin ETF inflows, and improving on-chain metrics—paints a picture of a market in recovery. However, it is essential to note that this rebound remains tentative and could be influenced by various external factors moving forward.
