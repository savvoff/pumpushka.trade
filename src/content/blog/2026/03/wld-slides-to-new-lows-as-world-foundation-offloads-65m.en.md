## Overview

The World Foundation, the organization behind the biometric identity platform World, has recently disclosed the completion of a significant over-the-counter sale of its WLD tokens. This sale, valued at $65 million, has resulted in a notable decline in the token's market value, raising concerns among investors.

## Sale Details and Market Reaction

On Saturday, the World Foundation announced that its token issuance arm, World Assets, finalized a series of over-the-counter sales involving WLD tokens. The sales were distributed among four buyers throughout the past week, with the first settlement taking place on March 20. The tokens changed hands at an average price of approximately $0.27 each, totaling around 239 million WLD tokens.

The sale's timing is critical, as it represents a substantial 76% decrease from the previous fundraising round in May 2024, when the foundation raised $135 million at an average price of $1.13 per token. The current transaction has negatively impacted the token's market performance, with WLD briefly hitting an all-time low of $0.24 before recovering slightly to around $0.27. This current valuation reflects a dramatic 97% decline from the peak price of $11.82 recorded in March 2024.

The World Foundation stated that the proceeds from this sale would fund essential operations, research and development, orb manufacturing, and ecosystem initiatives. However, not all tokens sold are locked up; while $25 million of the tokens are subject to a six-month lockup period, the remainder is immediately tradable, which could lead to further market volatility.

## Regulatory Challenges

The World project has faced multiple regulatory challenges across various countries. In October, Thai authorities raided an iris-scanning site associated with the project due to compliance issues. Additionally, regulatory scrutiny has intensified in other regions, with Indonesia suspending World ID registration, Brazil banning the eye-scanning operation, and Germany launching an inquiry. Kenya has also raised concerns regarding data privacy.

These regulatory hurdles have compounded the issues surrounding the WLD token, as they not only affect the project's reputation but also contribute to investor uncertainty.
