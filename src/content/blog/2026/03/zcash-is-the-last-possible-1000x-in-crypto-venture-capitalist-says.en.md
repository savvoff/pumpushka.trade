## Overview

Qiao Wang, co-founder of Alliance DAO, has put forth a compelling argument suggesting that Zcash may represent the last viable opportunity for a 1000x return in the cryptocurrency market. His perspective hinges on the belief that privacy remains the final significant gap in the digital asset landscape, which could lead to substantial long-term growth.

## The Argument for Zcash

In a post shared on X, Wang articulated his viewpoint on March 15, emphasizing that Zcash could be the last token capable of achieving a 1000x return. He cites various macroeconomic factors contributing to this belief, including government overreach, rampant money printing, a rise in socialism, and the implications of quantum computing. Wang's stance is not merely speculative; he suggests a long-term investment strategy akin to that of Bitcoin, advising investors to "accumulate during periods of apathy and hold it for 10-20 years."

Wang's reasoning centers on the concept of scale. He noted that while there are potential opportunities for 10x or even 100x returns, a 1000x return necessitates a significantly large total addressable market (TAM). This implies that achieving such remarkable returns is not solely dependent on innovative technology or strong narratives but also requires a market capable of sustaining a multi-decade re-rating.

This notion of privacy as a critical component of the crypto ecosystem was echoed by Mert Mumtaz, CEO of Helius Labs. He referenced a privacy thesis he published previously, suggesting that the original Bitcoin design had three fundamental issues: legitimacy, programmability and scale, and privacy. According to Mumtaz, Bitcoin has already addressed the first two issues, making privacy the last remaining challenge that could yield substantial returns.

## Credibility and Market Perception

The conversation around Zcash's potential also touches on its credibility compared to other privacy-focused cryptocurrencies. Awa Sun Yin, co-founder of Anoma and a board member at Shielded Labs, shared an anecdote about a rumor implying that an influential figure had been advocating for Zcash in political circles, highlighting concerns about the visibility of holdings and balances in Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. This rumor underscores the perception that Zcash possesses a level of credibility that other privacy coins may lack, making it a more favorable option in discussions about privacy in the cryptocurrency space.
