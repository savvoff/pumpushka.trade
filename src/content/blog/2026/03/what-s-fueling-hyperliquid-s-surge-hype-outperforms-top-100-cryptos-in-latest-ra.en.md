## Overview

Hyperliquid, a decentralized exchange, has recently witnessed a remarkable surge in trading volume related to oil-linked contracts, surpassing $1 billion within a 24-hour period. This spike has resulted in a notable rally of 10% in the platform's native token, HYPE, allowing it to outperform many of the top 100 cryptocurrencies by market capitalization.

## Recent Developments

The key driver behind this performance is the CL-USDC perpetual contract on Hyperliquid, which tracks the prices of West Texas Intermediate crude oil. This specific contract has seen trading volumes that have overtaken those of Ethereum on the platform. The rise in activity aligns with a significant increase in oil futures, which have jumped substantially due to escalating tensions in the Middle East. These geopolitical events have disrupted global supply chains, contributing to a surge in oil prices.

Before the recent geopolitical developments, the daily trading volume for the CL-USDC contract was around $21 million. However, following these events, this figure has skyrocketed to over $1.2 billion. Additionally, open interest in this contract has risen to $183 million, indicating a growing interest among traders.

## Market Sentiment

The excitement surrounding the HYPE rally is also bolstered by a bullish outlook from Arthur Hayes, co-founder of the cryptocurrency platform BitMEX. In a recent essay, Hayes expressed optimism regarding Hyperliquid's potential, suggesting that the platform can continue to grow its revenue streams despite potential challenges in the broader cryptocurrency market. 

While HYPE has been experiencing a positive trend, major cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum have shown modest recoveries during the same timeframe. Bitcoin has increased by approximately 2.5%, while Ethereum has seen a slightly higher rise of 3.4%. 

An analysis of HYPE’s daily trading chart reveals essential support levels that investors should monitor closely. Key support zones are expected around $32, $29, and $28, with the lowest level acting as a significant accumulation point over the past two weeks.
