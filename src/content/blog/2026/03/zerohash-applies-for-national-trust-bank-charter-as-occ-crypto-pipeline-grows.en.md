## Overview

ZeroHash has taken a significant step by applying for a national trust bank charter with the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC). This move signals a growing interest in the regulatory framework for cryptocurrencies and digital assets. In a related development, Revolut is also pursuing a full banking license in the United States, highlighting the increasing convergence of traditional banking and cryptocurrency services.

## ZeroHash's Application for Trust Bank Charter

ZeroHash’s application for a national trust bank charter is a noteworthy development in the cryptocurrency sphere. By seeking this status, ZeroHash aims to enhance its capabilities in providing crypto custody services. The OCC's recognition of trust banks allows them to operate under a federal charter, which could streamline operations and regulatory compliance for companies involved in digital asset custody.

The significance of this application lies in the broader context of regulatory acceptance of cryptocurrencies. As the OCC continues to develop its framework for digital assets, ZeroHash's move could pave the way for other companies seeking similar charters. This could lead to increased legitimacy for cryptocurrency custodians and foster consumer trust.

## Revolut's Pursuit of a U.S. Banking License

In parallel, Revolut is actively seeking a full banking license in the United States. This pursuit indicates a strategic shift for the company as it aims to broaden its service offerings. By obtaining a U.S. banking license, Revolut would be able to provide a wider range of financial services, including traditional banking operations alongside its existing cryptocurrency services.

Revolut's application reflects a growing trend among fintech companies to integrate cryptocurrency offerings with traditional banking services. As more firms explore these avenues, the landscape of financial services is likely to evolve, further blurring the lines between traditional finance and the digital asset space.
