## Overview

Recently, a significant event unfolded within the XRP Ledger, where a decentralized exchange founder successfully recovered a substantial amount of forgotten funds that had been previously abandoned by users. This recovery not only highlights the potential of the XRP Ledger but also indicates a surge in network activity, suggesting growing interest and participation in the ecosystem.

## Fund Recovery and Its Implications

Adam, the founder of First Ledger, a decentralized exchange operating on the XRP Ledger, undertook a comprehensive scan of the entire network to identify expired escrows that had been overlooked by users. Reports indicate that the total amount recovered was 750,218 XRP, valued at just over $1 million. This recovery is noteworthy because it primarily involved ordinary community members rather than institutional investors. Many of these holders had established time-based conditions on their XRP years ago, only to forget about them or remain unaware of the necessary steps to reclaim their funds.

The process of unlocking these funds is not automatic. The XRP Ledger's escrow system requires that once a timelock expires, recipients must execute a specific transaction known as an EscrowFinish to collect their funds. If this action is not taken within a designated timeframe, the escrow may expire entirely, making the funds unclaimable unless a cancellation transaction is initiated by the original sender. Furthermore, in some cases where no expiration date was set, the funds could remain locked indefinitely.

In response to this issue, First Ledger has implemented regular scans of the network to catch eligible escrows promptly, ensuring that users can reclaim their funds before they are lost indefinitely. This initiative not only assists users in retrieving their assets but also enhances the overall efficiency of the XRP Ledger.

## Increased Activity on the XRP Ledger

The recovery of these funds coincides with a broader surge in activity on the XRP Ledger. Data indicates that deposits into automated market makers reached an all-time high of 70,735 on February 28. Additionally, AccountSet transactions, which are utilized to modify account settings without transferring funds, reached a record single-day count of 114,690 on March 20. This uptick in transactions suggests that more users are engaging with the network.

Moreover, there has been a notable increase in error messages related to insufficient XRP reserves, which surpassed 370,000 on March 18. This figure marks the highest reading in three years and indicates that new users are entering the ecosystem but are encountering challenges due to the network's minimum balance requirements.
