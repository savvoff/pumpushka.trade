## Overview

A recent analysis from the crypto analytics platform CoinCodex presents a discouraging outlook for Shiba Inu (SHIB) investors. The analysis indicates that SHIB is unlikely to reach its all-time high from 2021 in the near future, particularly in 2026. This projection is significant for investors who may be hoping for a resurgence of the popular meme coin.

## Analysis of Current Market Conditions

According to CoinCodex, Shiba Inu has demonstrated considerable volatility since reaching its all-time high of approximately $0.000088 in 2021. As of March 12, 2026, SHIB is trading at around $0.0000058, which is over 93% lower than its historic peak. The analysis suggests that achieving the previous high would require an extraordinary price increase of roughly 1,400%, highlighting the significant challenges ahead for SHIB holders.

CoinCodex's forecast reflects a broadly negative sentiment surrounding SHIB, with a current sentiment breakdown showing 71% bearish and 29% bullish perspectives. Additionally, the Fear and Greed Index currently sits at 15, indicating that the market is in a state of extreme fear. Over the past 30 days, SHIB has only posted gains on 11 trading days, equating to a mere 37% of the time, further emphasizing the prevailing bearish trend.

Volatility for SHIB is reported at 6.8%, signaling considerable price fluctuations without a consistent upward trajectory. Technical indicators are heavily weighted toward bearish signals, as CoinCodex lists 20 sell signals against just 8 buy signals for Shiba Inu. The 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) stands at $0.0000065, while the 200-day SMA is at $0.0000093. Both averages are above the current trading price, suggesting ongoing selling pressure.

The 14-day Relative Strength Index (RSI) for SHIB is 42.89, indicating a neutral position but trending toward the lower end of the scale. This reading, combined with the moving averages, points to a lack of momentum and a weak buying interest that could hinder any potential price recovery.
