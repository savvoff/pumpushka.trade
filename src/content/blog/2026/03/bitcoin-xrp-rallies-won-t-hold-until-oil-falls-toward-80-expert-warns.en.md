## Overview

Recent market analysis indicates that the potential for sustainable rallies in Bitcoin and XRP is closely tied to fluctuations in oil prices. Market expert Sam Daodu emphasizes that for these cryptocurrencies to maintain upward momentum, oil prices need to decline further, ideally toward the $85–$80 range.

## Current Market Conditions

On Monday, Brent crude oil prices experienced a significant drop of nearly 12%, trading around $94. Despite this decrease, Daodu warns that this level is not sufficient for Bitcoin and XRP to achieve lasting gains. The connection between energy prices and the cryptocurrency market is underscored by ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have contributed to inflationary pressures. These pressures, combined with concerns over interest rates, are limiting the potential for risk assets like cryptocurrencies to thrive.

As of now, Bitcoin is hovering just above the $70,000 mark, while XRP is consolidating around $1.44. Both cryptocurrencies have seen modest retracements from their recent highs, with Bitcoin down approximately 4% and XRP down about 5% over the past week. These pullbacks are attributed to the same macroeconomic factors that have influenced oil prices, particularly the impact of escalating tensions since the closures in the Strait of Hormuz began.

Daodu highlights that elevated oil prices contribute to ongoing inflationary concerns, which in turn hinder the Federal Reserve's ability to ease monetary policy. The Fed's recent communications have extended expectations for any potential rate cuts, leading to a capital rotation away from risk-on assets. Cryptocurrencies, which still exhibit characteristics of high-risk investments, are particularly vulnerable in this environment.

## Market Sensitivity and Technical Patterns

One notable aspect of the cryptocurrency market is its 24/7 trading nature, which allows it to react swiftly to geopolitical events. This immediate response can lead to sharper price movements for Bitcoin and XRP, especially following significant news over weekends or during off-hours when traditional markets are closed.

Despite these challenges, Daodu identifies some encouraging technical patterns. Bitcoin has shown resilience by forming higher lows during sell-offs since late February, indicating that buyers are stepping in during dips. Meanwhile, XRP has maintained a stable holding zone between $1.35 and $1.45, demonstrating its ability to withstand recent market volatility.

Daodu asserts that the price of oil is a crucial variable that could alter the current dynamics of the cryptocurrency market. A retreat in Brent prices toward the $80–$85 range, potentially driven by signs of a ceasefire or diplomatic progress, could alleviate inflationary pressures. This easing may provide the Federal Reserve with the opportunity to consider rate cuts, which would likely encourage a resurgence of risk capital into the cryptocurrency market, offering Bitcoin and XRP the necessary momentum for sustained gains.
