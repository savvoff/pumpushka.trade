## Overview

Cryptocurrency exchange Coinbase has expressed its inability to support the revised provisions of the CLARITY Act, which has created a new hurdle in the ongoing negotiations surrounding this crucial crypto market-structure bill. The revisions primarily focus on the governance of stablecoin yield arrangements, a significant area of discussion in recent months.

## Details of the Situation

Coinbase's rejection stems from the updated language included in the Senate's version of the CLARITY Act, which aims to regulate how stablecoin yield programs operate. The proposed changes would impose restrictions on structures that attempt to replicate bank deposit products and would also narrow the scope of other permissible activities related to stablecoins. Furthermore, the draft raises unresolved questions about the classification of activity-based stablecoins and the treatment of transaction-reward programs.

These uncertainties, coupled with what many in the industry perceive as a more restrictive approach, have led Coinbase to communicate its opposition to lawmakers. This decision represents a notable shift from the more vigorous stance taken by Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong earlier in the year, which had previously stalled the bill’s markup process. 

The industry response to the CLARITY Act's latest draft has been varied. One trade association indicated that the new language diverges significantly from prior discussions with the White House, characterizing it as more restrictive for the cryptocurrency sector. Conversely, another trade group leader viewed the provisions as largely aligned with expectations, suggesting that they strike an appropriate balance by maintaining rewards while curtailing interest-like offerings associated with stablecoins.
