## Overview

Recent events in Tehran have triggered a significant surge in cryptocurrency outflows from Iran’s largest exchange, Nobitex. Following reports of airstrikes by the US and Israel, digital asset withdrawals skyrocketed, highlighting the role of cryptocurrencies as a means of value transfer amidst geopolitical tensions.

## Significant Increase in Crypto Withdrawals

Hours after explosions were reported in Tehran, cryptocurrency activity surged dramatically. Reports indicate that withdrawals from Nobitex, the largest crypto trading platform in Iran, increased sharply as news of military actions spread throughout the country. Blockchain analytics firms observed that outflows from wallets linked to Nobitex rose approximately 700% within a short timeframe, marking a stark contrast to the typical daily activity.

The uptick in withdrawals began almost immediately after confirmation of the airstrikes. Within minutes of the first strike, the volume of transfers escalated into the millions of dollars. This rapid response underscores the urgency felt by many Iranians, who often rely on cryptocurrencies to move value across borders due to existing sanctions and banking limitations.

## Context of Crypto Use in Iran

Iran has a complex relationship with cryptocurrencies, having seen their use increase in response to tightening access to global finance. The country has faced years of economic strain due to sanctions related to its nuclear program and regional policies, leading many citizens and businesses to turn to crypto mining and trading as alternative financial pathways. Nobitex has consistently operated in a gray area, adapting to the evolving landscape of sanctions and capital controls.

While there have been previous instances of increased crypto activity during periods of unrest, the scale of this particular surge is noteworthy. However, it is essential to recognize that the spike in outflows may reflect a short-term panic response rather than a coordinated effort to transfer significant amounts of capital.

## Internet Connectivity Issues

The initial rush of cryptocurrency transactions was short-lived, as internet connectivity in Iran reportedly dropped by around 99% shortly after the airstrikes. This drastic reduction in connectivity significantly hampered further transfers, causing the flow of outgoing crypto transactions to dwindle. While some transactions were completed before the internet blackout, many others were left pending. This situation illustrates the dependence of cryptocurrency transactions on stable internet access and functioning platforms.

This recent incident in Iran serves as an illustration of how quickly digital currencies can react to geopolitical events. However, the limitations imposed by internet connectivity highlight the vulnerabilities within this system, suggesting that while cryptocurrencies may offer a degree of financial freedom, they are not without their challenges.
