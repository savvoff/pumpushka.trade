## Overview

Bitcoin has experienced a notable performance recently, influenced by geopolitical events. However, its price has retreated from significant highs, raising concerns about potential further declines, particularly towards a critical level.

## Recent Price Movements

In the past week, Bitcoin's price surged to a peak of $74,000, driven by escalating geopolitical tensions between the United States, Israel, and Iran. This surge marked one of Bitcoin's strongest performances in the early part of 2026. However, this rally was short-lived, as the price quickly fell back below the critical psychological threshold of $70,000. This decline indicates that the recent price increase was primarily a relief rally, rather than a sustainable upward trend.

The current market structure remains bearish, leading to uncertainty about how low Bitcoin's price may drop in this phase. 

## Risks of Liquidation

Crypto analyst Ali Martinez has highlighted a significant risk concerning Bitcoin's price potentially dropping to around $54,000. This level has been identified as a crucial point for the cryptocurrency during this bearish market phase. Martinez's analysis is based on the Aggregated Liquidation Levels Heatmap metric, which illustrates areas where high concentrations of long or short liquidations exist.

According to the analysis, a decline to $54,000 could result in the liquidation of over $70 million in long positions in Bitcoin. The heatmap indicates that this level is a "hot" zone, marked by a concentration of high-leverage positions. Such zones often exert a magnetic pull on prices, leading to increased activity around these levels.

If Bitcoin's price were to fall to this critical point, it could exacerbate the already low market sentiment. The potential for a liquidation cascade at this level could trigger a "Long Squeeze." This phenomenon occurs when a declining price forces bullish traders to sell their positions to limit losses or achieve breakeven, further adding downward pressure on the price. 

The $54,000 mark is not only significant due to the potential liquidation risks but also aligns with the realized price, making it a pivotal threshold for Bitcoin's trajectory in the coming months.
