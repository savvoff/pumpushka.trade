## Overview

A coalition of advocacy groups has voiced strong opposition to a ballot measure supported by OpenAI, citing concerns regarding child safety. The groups argue that the measure would restrict legal accountability and establish limited protections for children, prompting a significant debate over the implications of artificial intelligence in society.

## Opposition to OpenAI's Ballot Measure

The advocacy groups have raised alarms over the potential consequences of the OpenAI-backed measure, which they believe would undermine existing legal frameworks designed to protect children. Their primary concern revolves around the assertion that the measure could lead to reduced accountability for AI developers and operators, thereby increasing the risk of harm to minors.

The coalition emphasizes that the proposed measure could create a legal environment that favors AI companies at the expense of child safety. By locking in narrow protections, the groups fear that it might hinder efforts to establish more robust safeguards against the misuse of AI technologies. This situation is particularly alarming given the rapid advancement of AI and its growing presence in various aspects of everyday life, including education, entertainment, and social interactions.

Advocacy groups are advocating for a more comprehensive approach that prioritizes child safety and enhances legal protections. They believe that any measures related to AI should not only address the interests of technology developers but also consider the potential risks posed to vulnerable populations, especially children. The coalition argues that a lack of accountability could lead to unforeseen consequences, leaving children exposed to harmful content or interactions facilitated by AI systems.
