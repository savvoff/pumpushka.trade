## Overview

XRP is at a pivotal moment as technical indicators suggest the early stages of a potential bottoming phase. Following a prolonged pullback, analysts are noting increasing price compression and historically oversold conditions, which could set the stage for a significant market movement.

## Market Dynamics and Analysis

XRP is currently trading near a price zone that many market participants perceive as weak. Despite this perception, crypto analyst Diana argues that the situation may not be as negative as it seems. She interprets the monthly chart as indicating a high-timeframe reset rather than a collapse in the market. This perspective is important as it challenges the prevailing bearish sentiment surrounding XRP.

The $1.30 to $1.35 range is identified as a crucial support zone where XRP's price has started to stabilize. While momentum has diminished, the selling pressure appears to be easing, suggesting that the ongoing price compression could lead to either a breakout or a breakdown. Diana highlights that traders often focus on XRP's large total supply, leading to assumptions about its inability to move significantly. However, she points out that a significant portion of XRP's supply is not actively available for trading. Much of it is either locked, stored off exchanges, or held by long-term investors who are not inclined to sell. Consequently, a surge in demand could potentially drive prices higher more rapidly than expected.

If XRP maintains its position within this support zone and can reclaim higher resistance levels, the market might start aiming for a move back toward higher price levels. Conversely, a decisive breakdown below this support area could indicate the need for a more profound reset before any bullish momentum can materialize.

In another analysis, crypto analyst EGRAG CRYPTO has noted that XRP's weekly Relative Strength Index (RSI) is nearing historically oversold territory. This condition has often preceded significant turning points in XRP's history, occurring in years such as 2014, 2015, 2018, 2020, and 2022. Each time the RSI reached these extreme levels, the market approached a major macro low before reversing direction. EGRAG emphasizes that while entering this oversold zone does not guarantee an immediate bottom, it often signals the market is transitioning into a bottoming phase. This phase typically involves a final liquidity sweep and sideways accumulation before a gradual recovery begins.

As XRP's weekly RSI approaches this historically significant level once again, investors are left to ponder whether this moment represents a risky entry point or a valuable long-term accumulation opportunity.
