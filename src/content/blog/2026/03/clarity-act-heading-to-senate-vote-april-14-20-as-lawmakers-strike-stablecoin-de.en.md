## Overview

Lawmakers are making significant progress toward the passage of the CLARITY Act, a bill designed to establish a clearer regulatory framework for cryptocurrencies in the United States. A tentative agreement has been reached between key senators and the White House, particularly focusing on stablecoin regulations, which could pave the way for the legislation's advancement.

## Tentative Stablecoin Deal

On Wednesday, it was reported that key senators have come to a tentative agreement with the White House regarding the language of the CLARITY Act. This agreement aims to resolve a major dispute between banks and cryptocurrency firms concerning stablecoin yields. The development is seen as a crucial step that could facilitate the progress of the legislation in the near future.

Senator Thom Tillis and Senator Angela Alsobrooks are leading the negotiations and have been credited with crafting this agreement alongside White House officials. Alsobrooks expressed optimism about the deal, stating that it represents significant progress in their efforts. She noted that the agreement would not only foster innovation but would also help mitigate the risk of widespread deposit flight from traditional banks to crypto platforms.

Despite the positive developments, Senator Tillis emphasized that the agreement is still tentative and not yet finalized. He indicated that he intends to discuss the details of the agreement with industry stakeholders before proceeding further. This cautious approach underscores the complexities involved in crafting legislation that balances the interests of both traditional financial institutions and the rapidly evolving crypto industry.

## CLARITY Act Markup Scheduled

The timeline for further action on the CLARITY Act is beginning to take shape, with indications that the Senate Banking Committee plans to conduct a markup in the latter half of April. Market expert MartyParty noted that Senator Cynthia Lummis has mentioned that this markup could take place during the weeks following the Easter recess, specifically around April 13 or April 20. 

This markup is a critical phase in the legislative process, as it will allow for potential revisions and political maneuvering regarding the draft of the CLARITY Act. The outcome of this markup could significantly influence the bill's progression toward a vote on the Senate floor. However, details surrounding the negotiations and the final language of the bill remain uncertain at this stage.
