## Overview

Recent reports indicate a significant surge in cryptocurrency outflows from Iran, with a notable increase of 700% following airstrikes conducted by the US and Israel. This spike in outflows may reflect a broader trend of capital flight from the country amid escalating regional tensions and uncertainty.

## What Happened

Elliptic, a blockchain analytics firm, conducted an initial tracing of the cryptocurrency transactions originating from Iran. Their findings revealed that a substantial portion of the outflows was directed to foreign cryptocurrency exchanges. This pattern suggests that individuals or entities within Iran are seeking to move their capital out of the country, possibly as a response to the heightened geopolitical risks associated with the recent military actions.

The airstrikes by the US and Israel have contributed to an environment of instability, prompting many to consider alternative avenues for safeguarding their assets. Cryptocurrency, known for its decentralized nature, often serves as a preferred option for individuals looking to protect their wealth during times of crisis. The substantial increase in outflows indicates that many Iranians may be utilizing digital currencies to escape the uncertainties tied to their national situation.
