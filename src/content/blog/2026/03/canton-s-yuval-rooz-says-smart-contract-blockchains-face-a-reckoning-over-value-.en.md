## Overview

Yuval Rooz, co-founder of Canton, has expressed concerns regarding the current state of smart contract blockchains, particularly emphasizing the disconnect between their valuations and actual usage. He argues that many platforms promoting themselves as financial solutions do not have the necessary activity to support their market valuations. Additionally, Rooz points out that stablecoins have yet to achieve a genuine product-market fit.

## The Current State of Smart Contract Blockchains

According to Rooz, there is a significant issue within the blockchain ecosystem where numerous projects are marketing themselves as financial infrastructures. However, he suggests that these blockchains are falling short in terms of real-world activity. This lack of engagement raises questions about their valuations, as it is critical for any financial platform to demonstrate substantial usage to justify its worth.

Rooz's comments highlight a broader concern in the blockchain space: the sustainability of projects that do not have a robust user base or practical applications. The discrepancy between perceived value and actual utility could lead to a reevaluation of many blockchain projects, especially those that rely heavily on speculative investment rather than solid operational metrics.

Furthermore, Rooz addresses the issue of stablecoins, which are designed to maintain a stable value and facilitate transactions. He notes that these digital assets still struggle to find a true product-market fit. This situation is significant because stablecoins are often viewed as essential components of the cryptocurrency ecosystem, providing a bridge between volatile cryptocurrencies and traditional fiat currencies. Without a clear market demand or effective use cases, the future of stablecoins could be jeopardized.

## From author

The insights shared by Rooz reflect a critical examination of the blockchain landscape, particularly concerning the sustainability and viability of smart contract platforms and stablecoins. His observations prompt a necessary dialogue regarding the importance of real-world applications and user engagement in the blockchain space. As the market continues to evolve, the emphasis on actual usage over speculative valuations may become increasingly crucial for the survival of various projects.

## Impact on the crypto market

- The discussion raises awareness about the potential overvaluation of blockchain projects lacking real activity, which could lead to market corrections.
- A focus on user engagement may shift investment strategies, prioritizing projects with proven functionality and user bases.
- The challenges faced by stablecoins could hinder their adoption, affecting liquidity in the cryptocurrency market.
- If the gap between valuation and activity continues to widen, it may lead to increased scrutiny from regulators and investors.
- Overall market sentiment may become more cautious as stakeholders seek to differentiate between genuine innovation and speculative ventures.
