## Overview

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is advocating for a strategic initiative to establish a connection between the central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) of BRICS nations. This proposal aims to be discussed at the upcoming 2026 summit that India will host. The move signifies a growing interest among BRICS countries in the development and integration of digital currencies.

## Proposal for CBDC Link

The RBI's call to action emphasizes the importance of linking the CBDCs of BRICS nations—Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa. This initiative aims to enhance cooperation and facilitate transactions between these countries through their respective digital currencies. The proposed agenda item for the 2026 summit reflects a broader trend among emerging economies to explore the potential of digital currencies as a means of fostering economic collaboration and reducing reliance on traditional financial systems.

The RBI's initiative is rooted in the increasing global interest in digital currencies and the need for efficient cross-border transactions. By establishing a framework for CBDC interoperability, BRICS nations could streamline trade, improve payment efficiencies, and potentially bolster their economic ties. The emphasis on digital currencies also aligns with efforts to modernize financial systems and adapt to the evolving demands of the global economy.

The proposal to link CBDCs among BRICS countries comes at a time when various nations are exploring the implementation of their own digital currencies. This collective approach could position BRICS nations as significant players in the global digital currency landscape, promoting their currencies as viable alternatives in international trade.

## From author

The RBI's proposal highlights a significant shift in how countries perceive the potential of digital currencies. By advocating for a collective approach among BRICS nations, India is positioning itself as a leader in the digital currency space. The initiative could pave the way for increased economic collaboration among these nations, which historically have sought to enhance their influence in global markets. 

Moreover, this proposal reflects a growing recognition of the importance of digital currencies in the modern economy. As governments and central banks around the world continue to explore the implications of digital currencies, initiatives like the one proposed by the RBI could play a crucial role in shaping the future of financial systems.
