## Overview

The Federal Reserve recently marked its 112th anniversary, prompting a reflection on its historical impact on purchasing power and money supply. As the central banking system of the United States, the Federal Reserve has overseen significant changes in the economy, particularly noted by the drastic loss of purchasing power and the substantial increase in the money supply. In contrast, Bitcoin is presented as an alternative that fundamentally diverges from the practices of traditional monetary systems.

## The Federal Reserve's Historical Impact

The Federal Reserve's establishment has been a pivotal moment in the history of the U.S. economy. Over the years, it has played a crucial role in managing the monetary system, yet its track record reveals a stark reality: the purchasing power of the U.S. dollar has diminished significantly since the Fed's inception. The report indicates that a staggering 97% of purchasing power has been lost, highlighting the challenges faced by consumers and the economy due to inflationary pressures.

In conjunction with the loss of purchasing power, the M2 money supply, which includes cash, checking deposits, and easily convertible near money, has surged dramatically by 140,000%. This exponential increase in the money supply raises questions about the long-term implications for economic stability and the value of currency. Critics argue that such expansionary monetary policies can lead to inflation and erode the savings of individuals, ultimately impacting the overall economy.

## Bitcoin as an Alternative

In light of the Federal Reserve's monetary policies and their consequences, Bitcoin emerges as a fundamentally different path. Unlike traditional fiat currencies that are subject to inflationary pressures due to central bank policies, Bitcoin operates on a decentralized network with a capped supply. This characteristic positions Bitcoin as a potential hedge against inflation and a store of value, appealing to those who seek financial alternatives to conventional banking systems.

The contrasting narratives of the Federal Reserve and Bitcoin underscore a broader debate about the future of money, value preservation, and economic freedom. As the Fed continues to navigate monetary policy in an ever-changing economic landscape, Bitcoin advocates argue for its role as a solution to the challenges posed by traditional financial systems.
