## Overview

The recent hack of Trust Wallet, resulting in a loss of $7 million, underscores significant vulnerabilities faced by crypto-friendly small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs). This incident sheds light on critical weaknesses in verification processes, browser extensions, and internal controls that can expose these businesses to cybersecurity threats.

## What Happened

Trust Wallet experienced a significant breach, leading to the theft of $7 million. This incident serves as a stark reminder of the inherent risks associated with the cryptocurrency space, particularly for SMEs that often lack the robust security measures seen in larger organizations. The hack has raised alarms about the effectiveness of verification systems that are essential for safeguarding digital assets.

One of the key vulnerabilities highlighted by this incident is the reliance on browser extensions. Browser extensions can be a convenient tool for managing digital wallets and conducting transactions; however, they can also serve as a gateway for cybercriminals if not properly secured. The Trust Wallet hack exemplifies how these extensions can be exploited, putting users' funds at risk.

Moreover, the internal controls within crypto-friendly SMEs are under scrutiny following the hack. Effective internal control systems are vital for preventing unauthorized access and ensuring that only legitimate transactions are processed. The weaknesses revealed in Trust Wallet's internal controls may prompt other businesses in the sector to reevaluate their security measures to avoid similar breaches.
