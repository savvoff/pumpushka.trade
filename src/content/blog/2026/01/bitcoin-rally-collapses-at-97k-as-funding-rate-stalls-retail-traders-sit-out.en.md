## Overview

Bitcoin's recent rally has faced a significant setback as it struggled to maintain the $97,000 mark. The funding rate for Bitcoin has stalled, leading to a noticeable retreat in the market. Meanwhile, retail traders appear to be sitting on the sidelines, raising questions about the future momentum of Bitcoin's price movement.

## Bitcoin's Struggles at $97,000

Despite Bitcoin's ascent toward the $97,000 threshold, it ultimately failed to maintain this level. This inability to hold the price point is critical as it reflects a lack of sustained buying pressure in the market. The funding rate, which is a crucial indicator of market sentiment and trader confidence, has stalled. A stagnant funding rate often signals that traders are not heavily leveraging their positions, indicating a potential lack of enthusiasm or conviction in the current market environment.

The situation becomes even more complex when considering the role of retail traders. The current market dynamics suggest that retail investors are largely absent from the trading scene. Their reluctance to engage in the market could be attributed to several factors, including uncertainty around Bitcoin's price stability and broader market conditions. Retail participation is often a key driver of momentum in cryptocurrency markets, so their absence could hinder any potential recovery or further price increases. 

The question now arises: will traditional finance (TradFi) play a role in reigniting the rally towards the coveted $100,000 mark? The intersection of traditional financial systems and the cryptocurrency market is an area of increasing interest. As institutional interest in Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies continues to grow, the potential for TradFi to influence price movements becomes more pronounced. However, the current market scenario, characterized by stalled funding rates and a lack of retail engagement, suggests that any significant rally may remain elusive without a shift in trader sentiment.
