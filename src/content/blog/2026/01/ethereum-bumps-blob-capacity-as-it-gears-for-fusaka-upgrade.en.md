## Overview

Ethereum has made a significant update to its data capacity, increasing the blob target and maximum blob limit. This adjustment is part of the preparations for the upcoming Fusaka upgrade, which aims to enhance the network’s efficiency and scalability.

## What Happened

Ethereum has raised its data capacity per block by increasing the blob target to 14 and setting the maximum blob limit to 21. This change reflects Ethereum's ongoing commitment to improving its infrastructure and accommodating the growing demands of its user base. The increase in blob capacity is particularly relevant as it aligns with the network's goals for better performance and scalability.

The decision to increase the blob capacity comes in anticipation of the Fusaka upgrade, an important milestone for Ethereum that aims to introduce various enhancements to the network. By raising the blob target and limit, Ethereum is taking proactive steps to ensure that it can handle more data efficiently, which is crucial for maintaining the network's performance as it continues to grow.
