## Overview

Bitcoin's price has experienced a significant pullback, dipping below critical support levels. The cryptocurrency is currently facing consolidation of losses, with concerns mounting about its ability to maintain stability above the $92,000 mark. This situation raises questions regarding the future trajectory of Bitcoin's price.

## Bitcoin Price Decline

Bitcoin began its latest decline after failing to maintain support above the $94,500 level. The price dropped sharply below both the $94,000 and $93,500 thresholds. Currently, Bitcoin is trading under $93,500 and has also fallen below the 100 hourly Simple Moving Average. Notably, a break occurred below a declining channel, which had support at $93,550 on the hourly chart of the BTC/USD pair.

As the price continued to slide, it tested the 76.4% Fibonacci retracement level of the recent wave, which ranged from a swing low to a swing high. The price even dipped below the $92,000 level, indicating a significant moment of volatility. If Bitcoin can stabilize above this level, there may be a potential for a rebound. The immediate resistance is positioned around the $93,000 level, with the first key resistance at $93,500. Should Bitcoin manage to close above the $94,000 resistance, it might push the price higher, potentially testing the $95,000 mark and beyond.

Conversely, if Bitcoin struggles to rise above the $93,500 resistance zone, it risks entering a further decline. Immediate support is now near the $92,000 level, with the first major support at $91,800 and the next at $91,300. If losses continue, the price could fall toward the $90,500 support, with a critical support level identified at $90,000, below which Bitcoin might see accelerated downward momentum.
