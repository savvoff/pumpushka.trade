## Overview

F/m Investments is actively pursuing approval from the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) to record ownership of its $6 billion Treasury Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) on a permissioned blockchain. This move is part of a broader trend of tokenization gaining traction on Wall Street, signaling a potential shift in how financial assets are managed and traded.

## What Happened

F/m Investments, an asset manager managing approximately $18 billion in assets, has submitted a request to the SEC. The firm aims to utilize blockchain technology to enhance the efficiency and transparency of its Treasury ETF, which currently has a valuation of $6 billion. By recording ownership on a permissioned blockchain, F/m Investments seeks to streamline the process of asset management and improve the overall experience for investors.

The initiative comes at a time when the concept of tokenization is increasingly being embraced by financial institutions. Tokenization refers to the process of converting rights to an asset into a digital token on a blockchain, which can provide numerous benefits including increased liquidity, reduced settlement times, and enhanced security. As more firms explore the advantages of blockchain technology, F/m Investments’ application could pave the way for similar efforts within the industry.

This effort to tokenize a Treasury ETF is particularly significant as U.S. Treasury securities are among the most widely held and traded financial instruments. The move could not only modernize the management of these assets but also attract a new wave of investors who are interested in the benefits offered by blockchain technology.
