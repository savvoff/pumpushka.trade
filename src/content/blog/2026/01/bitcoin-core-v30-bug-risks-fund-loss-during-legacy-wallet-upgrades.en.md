## Overview

Bitcoin Core has issued a warning to users of versions 30.0 and 30.1 regarding a critical bug that poses a significant risk during the migration of legacy wallets. This issue can lead to the complete deletion of all files in a wallet directory if the migration process does not succeed. The announcement highlights the potential for substantial fund loss, urging users to exercise caution.

## What Happened

The Bitcoin Core team has identified a serious vulnerability in its software versions 30.0 and 30.1. Users attempting to upgrade their legacy wallets may encounter a situation where, if the migration fails, all files within the wallet directory could be deleted. This bug raises urgent concerns about the safety of users' funds, as it could lead to irreversible loss of assets stored in these wallets.

Due to the nature of this issue, it is imperative that users refrain from attempting to migrate their legacy wallets until a resolution is provided. The warning underscores the importance of ensuring that software updates do not inadvertently compromise the security of users' holdings.

The Bitcoin Core team's prompt communication regarding this vulnerability serves as a reminder of the complexities involved in software upgrades within the cryptocurrency ecosystem. Users must remain vigilant and informed about potential risks associated with such updates, particularly when handling sensitive financial data.
