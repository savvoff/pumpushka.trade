## Overview

In a recent inquiry, six US senators have expressed their concerns regarding the Department of Justice's decision to disband its cryptocurrency enforcement team. They highlighted a significant increase in illicit cryptocurrency activity, which they argue underscores the necessity of maintaining a dedicated unit to address these challenges.

The six senators pointed to a notable 162% increase in illicit cryptocurrency activity in 2025 as a critical reason for their inquiry. This alarming statistic raises questions about the effectiveness of current regulatory measures and the ability of law enforcement to keep pace with the evolving landscape of digital currencies.

The senators believe that the increase in illicit activities linked to cryptocurrencies signals a growing threat that warrants a focused response from the DOJ. By disbanding the enforcement team, they argue that the DOJ may have diminished its capacity to combat these illegal operations effectively. 

The inquiry reflects a broader concern among lawmakers about the implications of cryptocurrency on financial crime and regulatory oversight. The senators' appeal indicates a recognition that as the popularity and acceptance of cryptocurrencies rise, so too do the risks associated with their misuse.
