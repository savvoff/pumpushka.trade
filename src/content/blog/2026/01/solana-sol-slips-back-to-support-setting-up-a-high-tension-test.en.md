## Recent Developments

Solana's price correction began after it was unable to surpass the $150 mark. This failure led to a decline below the $146 and $145 levels, which has placed SOL in a short-term bearish zone. The price decline is notable, as it has dropped below the 100-hourly simple moving average, a key technical indicator for many traders.

Currently, Solana's price is hovering below $145, with a potential support level forming at $141, which is supported by a bullish trend line visible on the hourly chart of the SOL/USD pair. This trend line suggests that there may still be some buying interest in the market, particularly around the $140 zone, where bids could materialize.

Despite the current bearish sentiment, bulls remain active above the $140 threshold. However, the price faces immediate resistance around the $144 level, with significant resistance levels identified at $146 and $148. A successful close above the $148 level could potentially pave the way for a more substantial upward movement.

On the downside, if Solana fails to break above the $146 resistance, it may initiate another decline. Initial support is established at the $141 level, while the first major support lies at $140. A breach below the $140 level could lead to further declines towards the $132 support zone, and closing below this level might trigger a drop toward the $124 support.
