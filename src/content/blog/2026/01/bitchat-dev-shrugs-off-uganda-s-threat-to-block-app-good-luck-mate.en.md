## Overview

The developer of Bitchat, Calle, has expressed confidence in the face of Uganda's threat to block the messaging app ahead of the country's upcoming election. This situation raises important questions about the intersection of technology, governance, and free speech in the context of political events.

## What Happened

As Uganda gears up for its election next week, government officials have indicated plans to potentially block the Bitchat app. This action is aimed at curbing communication through the platform during a politically sensitive period. In response to this threat, Bitchat's developer Calle stated that the app's operations would continue regardless of any government efforts to impede its use. His declaration, “You can’t stop us,” suggests a firm stance against governmental censorship.

The Ugandan government has been known for its strict control over digital communications, especially during elections. Blocking apps like Bitchat could be seen as part of a broader strategy to limit dissent and manage the flow of information. The implications of such actions could resonate beyond Uganda, highlighting ongoing tensions between state authority and digital freedom.
