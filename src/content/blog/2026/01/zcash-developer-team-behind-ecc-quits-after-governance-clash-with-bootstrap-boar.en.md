## Overview

The developer team behind the cryptocurrency Zcash, known as the Electric Coin Company (ECC), has departed following a governance dispute with the Bootstrap board. This exit highlights underlying tensions in the management and direction of the Zcash project and raises questions about its future development.

## What Happened

The Electric Coin Company (ECC) has experienced a significant leadership shift as its developer team has chosen to leave the organization. This decision comes in the wake of what ECC's CEO, Josh Swihart, describes as changes that rendered the team's work untenable. These changes were initiated by the Bootstrap board, which has a role in overseeing the project's governance.

The conflict appears to stem from differing views on the direction of the Zcash project and how it should be managed. The ECC has been pivotal in the development of Zcash, a cryptocurrency known for its privacy features. The departure of the developer team raises concerns about the continuity of the project and the potential impact on its roadmap.

Swihart's statement suggests that the governance changes imposed by the Bootstrap board were substantial enough to disrupt the operational dynamics within the ECC. Although specific details of the governance changes have not been disclosed, the implications of this rift could be profound for both the developers and the broader Zcash community.
