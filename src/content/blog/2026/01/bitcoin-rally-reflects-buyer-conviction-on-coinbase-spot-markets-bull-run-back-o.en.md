## Overview

The recent rally in Bitcoin appears to be fueled by genuine spot demand on Coinbase, indicating a shift in market dynamics. This rise is characterized by direct purchases rather than speculative positions in derivatives, highlighting a significant commitment from buyers in the cryptocurrency space.

## Bitcoin Rally Driven by Spot Demand

Data from Coinbase reveals a surge in spot activity that supports the ongoing Bitcoin rally. This movement is significant as it suggests that the price increase is not merely a result of leveraged trading but reflects real capital commitment from investors. Spot buying is crucial in establishing a solid foundation for price movements, distinguishing it from transient bets often seen in derivatives markets.

The rally gained momentum following news related to a subpoena of Powell, which has been linked to increased buying activity on Coinbase. Crypto trader Alex Krüger noted on X that metrics such as the Adjusted Coinbase Premium and Cumulative Volume Delta (CVD) indicate a steady accumulation of Bitcoin through spot purchases. This accumulation is notable as it has occurred despite a prevailing sentiment among many Bitcoin enthusiasts that the cryptocurrency has been underperforming compared to equities and commodities.

Interestingly, the narrative surrounding the performance of equities may not be entirely accurate. Data indicates that a significant portion of the S&P 500 stocks have closed in the red during 2025, contradicting the perception of robust performance in traditional markets. The recent actions by the United States Department of Justice regarding Powell represent a critical macroeconomic test for Bitcoin, further influencing market sentiment.

Krüger emphasizes that the long-term value proposition of Bitcoin lies in its potential to serve as a hedge against the risks associated with central bank monetary policies. On Monday, Bitcoin experienced a slight upward movement, with the trader suggesting that the key battleground for Bitcoin remains around the 50-week moving average.

## Institutional Interest and Regulatory Developments

A significant development on the horizon is the markup of the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act, scheduled for discussion in the Senate Banking Committee. This act aims to clarify regulatory uncertainties between the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) and the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC). Such regulatory clarity is essential for institutional players who have been hesitant to engage with Bitcoin and other digital assets due to the existing ambiguity in the regulatory landscape.

The passage of the Clarity Act could mark a pivotal moment for the cryptocurrency market, potentially restoring confidence among banks, pension funds, and large investors. As regulatory uncertainties diminish, the influx of institutional capital could lead to increased demand for Bitcoin, thereby driving its price momentum higher.
