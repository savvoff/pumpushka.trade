## Overview

The ongoing debate in the US Senate regarding the Clarity Act has brought renewed attention to XRP and its treatment under US regulations, particularly concerning exchange-traded funds (ETFs). This discussion is significant as it may redefine how certain cryptocurrencies are classified and managed within financial markets.

## What Happened

The Clarity Act aims to provide clearer guidelines for cryptocurrencies that support US-listed ETFs, potentially leading to a classification similar to commodities. Such a shift could have important implications for the regulatory landscape surrounding digital assets. Notably, XRP spot ETFs have attracted substantial capital, with reported inflows of approximately $1.37 billion since their inception in November 2025. This figure underscores the interest from lawmakers and market observers in the evolving role of these financial instruments.

The operational mechanics of ETFs allow for the creation and redemption of shares “in kind,” meaning that funds can accept actual assets instead of cash. However, this process is not accessible to everyday investors since only authorized participants, such as large broker-dealers and market makers, can exchange tokens for ETF shares. This distinction is crucial to the ongoing debate about whether ETFs could operate similarly to banks.

XRP ETFs, characterized as In-Kind Funds, enable investors to deposit XRP directly into the fund in exchange for shares that reflect the value of the deposited tokens. This mechanism may encourage more investors to choose this option once the Clarity Act is enacted, as it allows them to utilize the ETF in a bank-like manner.

## Community Perspectives

Voices from the XRP community have begun to articulate a vision where ETFs serve as a regulated storage solution for token holders. Chad Steingraber, a prominent figure within this community, has emphasized the in-kind mechanics of ETFs, suggesting that investors could exchange XRP for corresponding ETF shares. This arrangement could provide a safer method for individuals to hold their assets until they are ready to utilize them again.

Steingraber's comments have contributed to the growing perception that ETFs could function as a secure alternative for managing crypto holdings. This has the potential to shift investor attitudes towards ETFs as a reliable means of safeguarding their digital assets.

## Tax Considerations

The structure of ETFs is also relevant to tax implications for investors. The in-kind creation and redemption process can help ETFs avoid regular capital gains distributions, which enhances their tax efficiency. However, tax consequences for token holders depend on how transactions are executed and the legal framework governing the products.

Under existing US regulations, transferring assets in a way that alters their form can trigger taxable events for the individuals involved. Furthermore, fund-level distributions may still result in tax liabilities for investors.
