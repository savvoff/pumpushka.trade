## Overview

Aero DEX is set to launch in the second quarter of 2026, with the ambitious goal of addressing the issue of liquidity fragmentation in decentralized finance (DeFi). By focusing on this critical aspect, Aero aims to compete directly with established platforms such as Uniswap and Curve.

## What Happened

The development team behind Aero DEX has announced their plans to introduce the platform, which is designed to tackle one of the significant challenges in the DeFi space: liquidity fragmentation. This issue has persisted as various decentralized exchanges (DEXs) attract liquidity to their platforms, leading to inefficiencies and higher slippage for traders. The team emphasized that their approach will be different from existing solutions, which have not fully resolved the liquidity fragmentation problem.

Aero's planned launch in 2026 puts it in a competitive position against incumbents in the DeFi sector. Uniswap and Curve have established themselves as leaders in the DEX space, with their unique liquidity pools and automated market-making capabilities. By targeting these platforms, Aero aims to offer users a more streamlined and efficient trading experience, potentially reshaping the competitive landscape of decentralized exchanges.

The decision to focus on liquidity fragmentation is significant, as it could lead to better price discovery and reduced transaction costs for users. The team believes that by addressing this issue, Aero can attract liquidity providers and traders who are currently spread across multiple platforms.
