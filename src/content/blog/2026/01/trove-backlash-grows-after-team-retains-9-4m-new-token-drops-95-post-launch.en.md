## Overview

Trove has recently faced significant backlash from its community following a controversial decision to change its decentralized exchange (DEX) platform. The team announced a shift from Hyperliquid to Solana just before launching their token, which resulted in a dramatic drop in the token's value.

## Shift in DEX Platform

Last week, Trove made headlines when it unexpectedly decided to pivot its perpetual decentralized exchange (DEX) from Hyperliquid to Solana. This last-minute change has raised eyebrows among its community and initial backers, many of whom expressed their dissatisfaction with the decision. The switch to Solana has been met with criticism, as it appears to diverge from the expectations set during the project's earlier phases.

The team’s decision to retain a substantial sum of $9.4 million has further fueled the discontent among supporters. Many community members feel that this retention raises questions about the team's commitment to its initial vision and the trust placed in them by their backers. The abrupt transition and the financial decisions made by the team have created a rift between Trove and its supporters.

## Community Reaction

The reaction from the community has been swift and severe. Early supporters who invested in the project based on its original plans expressed feelings of betrayal and disappointment. The new token, which launched concurrently with the shift to Solana, saw a staggering decline in value, dropping by 95% shortly after its introduction. This decline has led to further scrutiny of the team's decisions and has amplified calls for accountability.

Community feedback has highlighted concerns regarding transparency and communication from the Trove team. Many backers are questioning the rationale behind the pivot and seeking clarity on how it aligns with the project's long-term goals. The drastic change in direction has left many feeling uncertain about the future of the project and its viability in the competitive landscape of decentralized finance.
