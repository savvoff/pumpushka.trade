## Overview

Newrez, a US-based lender, has announced a significant policy change that will enable borrowers to utilize various cryptocurrency holdings in the mortgage approval process. This innovative approach allows for the inclusion of digital assets such as Bitcoin, Ether, crypto exchange-traded funds (ETFs), and US dollar-backed stablecoins in the verification of assets and estimation of income, without necessitating liquidation of these assets.

## Policy Details

The newly implemented policy by Newrez marks a pivotal shift in how lenders view cryptocurrency as part of the financial landscape. Traditionally, potential homebuyers have faced challenges in using their digital currencies for securing loans, primarily due to the volatility and regulatory uncertainties surrounding cryptocurrencies. However, Newrez's decision to accept crypto holdings represents a progressive step toward mainstream acceptance of digital assets in the real estate market.

Under this policy, borrowers can present their cryptocurrency holdings as part of their financial portfolio. This includes popular cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ether, as well as crypto ETFs, which offer exposure to a basket of cryptocurrencies without the need to hold them directly. Additionally, US dollar-backed stablecoins provide a stable alternative that can help mitigate the risks associated with cryptocurrency volatility. By allowing these assets to be used for asset verification and income estimates, Newrez is addressing a significant barrier that many potential borrowers face.

The policy is particularly important as it opens up new avenues for individuals who may have substantial assets in digital currencies but have been unable to leverage these for traditional financing options. This move could attract a new demographic of tech-savvy borrowers who are increasingly integrating digital assets into their financial strategies.
