## Overview

Bitcoin is currently experiencing significant price movements as it approaches a crucial technical area. Analysts suggest that this could lead to higher price levels in the near future, particularly focusing on the $93,500 resistance level. The recent performance indicates a structural change in Bitcoin's price action, which could have implications for its future trajectory.

## Bitcoin's Recent Price Movements

Bitcoin has surged 2.5% recently, retesting the $93,500 resistance level for the first time in a week. This resistance level has been a point of contention, with Bitcoin fluctuating between the $84,000 and $93,500 range over the past three months. Despite multiple attempts, Bitcoin has not successfully turned this resistance into support, which is critical for sustaining upward momentum.

Analyst Rekt Capital has pointed out that Bitcoin is approaching a “historic” test, forming what he describes as “another technically decisive region” above its current price levels. This situation arises as Bitcoin approaches its dynamic Bull Market Exponential Moving Average (EMA) cluster, which consists of the 50-week EMA and the 21-week EMA. The cluster is currently located between the $96,000 and $97,500 levels, a region that has historically been tested before significant price movements.

The analyst elaborates that typically, when Bitcoin overextends beyond this EMA cluster, it often fails to confirm this area as support. Instead, the crossover tends to follow a bearish price event, flipping the EMA cluster into resistance and leading to further downside continuation. Historical trends show that the 50-week and 21-week EMAs have the potential to come very close together, even overlapping for extended periods before a decisive crossover occurs.

As of now, Bitcoin has not yet retested or overextended beyond the two EMAs, but its historical performance suggests a likelihood of this happening. The current positioning of Bitcoin could create a “springboard higher,” potentially allowing for a test of this EMA cluster in the weeks ahead.

## Structural Changes in Bitcoin's Price Action

In the analysis of Bitcoin's recent performance, significant structural changes have been noted despite the sideways price action. Last week, Bitcoin closed above its multi-week downtrend, which has served as a major resistance point since late November. This achievement marks a small but notable technical milestone, as Bitcoin now holds above the November and December highs on a weekly timeframe, treating previous resistance as support.

Additionally, the mid-zone of its local range, around the $90,500 level, is now closely aligned with the former downtrend. This shift indicates that the downtrend that previously rejected price is beginning to act as layered support. If Bitcoin can maintain this mid-range region, it could challenge higher levels and potentially aim for $100,000.

Rekt Capital also noted that the latest rejection from the crucial $93,500 resistance was shallower and shorter than previous attempts, suggesting a weakening of resistance. Bitcoin has successfully retested the downtrend breakout area as support, momentarily reclaiming the $93,500 resistance and surging above the $94,000 area once again. 

Ultimately, Bitcoin needs to hold this area and close the week above $93,500 to initiate a breakout from the weekly range, according to the analyst.
