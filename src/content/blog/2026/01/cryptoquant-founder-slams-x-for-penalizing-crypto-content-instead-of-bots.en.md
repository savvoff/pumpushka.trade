## Overview

The founder of CryptoQuant has voiced strong criticism against X, the platform formerly known as Twitter, for its approach to handling crypto-related content. This criticism comes in response to comments made by X’s head of product, who attributed issues with the reach of crypto-related posts to behavior from users rather than algorithmic suppression of content.

## What Happened

In a recent statement, X's head of product addressed ongoing concerns about the visibility of cryptocurrency content on the platform. The official suggested that the problems faced by users in the crypto community stem from their own actions, specifically referring to "overposting." This indicates a belief that excessive posting by users is the primary reason for diminished reach, rather than any potential issues with X's algorithms that may be suppressing crypto content.

In contrast, the founder of CryptoQuant has taken issue with this viewpoint, asserting that the platform's algorithmic mechanisms are penalizing crypto-related posts more severely than other types of content. This conflict highlights a significant tension between the platform's management and community members who feel marginalized in their efforts to engage audiences.

The founder's remarks underscore a growing frustration within the crypto community regarding the visibility and reach of their content. Many users rely on platforms like X to share insights, news, and updates related to cryptocurrency, and diminished engagement can hinder the community's ability to connect and share information effectively.

The debate surrounding the visibility of crypto content on social media platforms is not just a matter of user experience; it also reflects broader concerns about how algorithms shape discourse and information flow in digital spaces. As the crypto market continues to evolve, the implications of content suppression could have far-reaching effects on community engagement, education, and overall market sentiment.

## From author

This situation raises important questions about the role of social media platforms in facilitating or obstructing communication within niche communities.
