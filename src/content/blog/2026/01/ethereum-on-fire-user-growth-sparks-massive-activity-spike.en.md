## Overview

Ethereum has experienced a significant surge in on-chain activity, primarily driven by an influx of first-time users and increased transaction flow. This trend has been highlighted by notable metrics showing a substantial rise in both new addresses and daily transactions, indicating growing engagement with the Ethereum network.

## Surge in On-Chain Activity

Recent data reveals that Ethereum’s on-chain activity has sharply increased, attributed to a wave of new users entering the ecosystem. According to Glassnode, the number of new activity retention addresses has roughly doubled within a month, escalating from approximately 4 million to around 8 million. This increase signifies that a fresh cohort of wallets is engaging with Ethereum, distinguishing these new users from repeat participants.

In terms of daily transactions, Ethereum reached a record high of 2.8 million, marking a significant increase of 125% when compared to the same period last year. Additionally, Etherscan reports indicate that the number of active addresses on the network has more than doubled year-over-year, rising from about 410,000 accounts to over 1 million. These figures suggest that the engagement with Ethereum is broad-based and not merely a temporary spike in activity.

### Transaction Growth and Layer 2 Networks

The growth in transactions is partially attributed to the rising activity with stablecoins and a decrease in transaction fees. Many users are transitioning their execution to Layer 2 networks, while the settlement processes remain on Ethereum’s main chain. This approach maintains the security of finality and contributes to lower gas costs, enhancing user experience.

Furthermore, staking on Ethereum has seen an increase, reaching nearly 36 million ETH. This uptick in staking contributes to the network's supply dynamics and indicates a growing commitment from users to engage with the platform.

## Market Behavior and Price Dynamics

Despite the positive metrics surrounding user engagement and transaction volume, market behavior continues to exhibit caution. Strength in U.S. equities has provided some stability to crypto prices, yet the flow of capital into Ethereum appears selective rather than widespread. Traders are adopting a conservative approach, opting to wait for clearer signals regarding ETH prices instead of making aggressive predictions about potential breakouts.

As a result, Ethereum has been consolidating within a correction phase, lacking sufficient momentum-driven buying. Even as Ether approached a two-month high, the market remains characterized by uncertainty, with many participants preferring to hold back until key long-term resistance levels are reclaimed.

### Analyst Perspectives

Some analysts have expressed optimism based on improvements in Ethereum’s on-chain fundamentals. Reports from LVRG Research indicate that the rise in transactions and staking activity is fostering a positive network environment. However, despite these strong metrics, technical challenges persist. The market seems to be in a repair phase rather than a confirmed uptrend, with overhead supply limiting sustained price advances.

## From author

The current surge in Ethereum's activity highlights an encouraging trend of increasing user engagement. Traders and investors alike are advised to remain vigilant, monitoring the situation for clearer signals before making significant moves.
