## Overview

Nomura-backed Laser Digital is pursuing a United States bank charter as part of its strategy to expand its operations in the cryptocurrency sector. This development could significantly shape the landscape of crypto banking by allowing the company to operate under a federal framework rather than navigating the complexities of state-by-state licensing.

## What Happened

Laser Digital, a subsidiary of the Japanese financial services group Nomura, is actively seeking to obtain a bank charter in the United States. Should this application be approved, it would enable the company to provide federally regulated cryptocurrency trading services. Notably, the proposed charter would allow Laser Digital to operate without the need to take deposits from customers.

This move is particularly significant given the current regulatory environment surrounding cryptocurrencies and digital assets in the U.S. By securing a federal bank charter, Laser Digital would streamline its operations and potentially offer a more robust and compliant platform for crypto trading. This could attract a wider range of clients who are looking for regulated services in the often-volatile crypto market.

The ability to bypass state-by-state licensing is a crucial factor in this initiative. The current regulatory framework in the U.S. can be cumbersome for financial institutions, especially those dealing with cryptocurrencies. Each state has its own set of rules and regulations that companies must navigate, which can lead to increased costs and operational complexities. By obtaining a federal charter, Laser Digital aims to reduce these barriers and establish a more efficient pathway for its business operations.

The push for a bank charter is part of a broader trend among financial institutions that are recognizing the growing importance of cryptocurrencies and the need for regulation in this space. As more traditional financial entities look to enter the crypto market, the establishment of a federal charter could serve as a model for others to follow.
