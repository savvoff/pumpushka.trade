## Overview

The Ethereum Foundation has announced the formation of a new team dedicated to enhancing post-quantum security. This initiative is being led by researcher Justin Drake, who emphasizes the importance of upgrading wallet safety in response to the rapidly evolving landscape of quantum computing.

## Formation of the New Team

The Ethereum Foundation (EF) is taking proactive measures to address the potential risks posed by quantum computing. With the formation of a new post-quantum team, the EF aims to focus on several key areas crucial for maintaining the security and integrity of Ethereum wallets. 

Justin Drake, a researcher at the Ethereum Foundation, has been vocal about the urgency of this initiative. As quantum technology advances, the timelines for its practical implementation are becoming shorter, which raises concerns about the vulnerabilities of current cryptographic systems. The new team will work on developing upgrades to wallet safety protocols, ensuring that they remain robust against potential quantum threats.

In addition to wallet upgrades, the post-quantum team will also be responsible for managing research prizes. These prizes are designed to incentivize innovative solutions and advancements in the field of post-quantum cryptography. By encouraging research and development, the Ethereum Foundation aims to foster a collaborative environment where new ideas can flourish and contribute to the overall security of the Ethereum ecosystem.

Furthermore, the team will establish test networks to evaluate and implement new security measures. These test networks will serve as experimental platforms where various post-quantum cryptographic techniques can be assessed for their effectiveness and reliability. This hands-on approach allows the Ethereum Foundation to stay ahead of potential threats and ensure that the network remains secure in the face of evolving technology.
