## Overview

XRP has recently attempted a price recovery, climbing above the $1.950 mark but encountering significant resistance near the $2.00 level. As the price struggles to maintain its upward momentum, bearish signals are emerging, leading to speculation about a potential decline below $1.920.

## XRP Price Movement

XRP's price began a recovery wave after finding support above the $1.8650 level. This recovery was mirrored by other cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin and Ethereum, allowing XRP to rise above the $1.90 and $1.920 thresholds. The price even exceeded the 50% Fibonacci retracement level of a previous downward movement, which spanned from a swing high to a low. However, despite these positive movements, the bulls faced a major hurdle at the $2.00 resistance level.

As XRP attempted to break through the $2.00 barrier, it was met with selling pressure, causing the price to retreat. Currently, XRP is trading below the $2.00 level and the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average, indicating a bearish trend. A bearish trend line is also forming with resistance at the $2.00 mark on the hourly chart of the XRP/USD pair. If XRP settles below $1.920, it could signal a continued downward trend.

The potential for further upward movement exists, but it is likely to encounter resistance near the $1.990 level and the 76.4% Fibonacci retracement level of the earlier downward move. The first significant resistance remains at the $2.00 level, and a close above this threshold could lead to a price increase toward $2.0650. If the bulls manage to push the price above $2.10, they could target $2.150 and then $2.20 as the next levels of resistance.

Conversely, if XRP fails to break through the $2.00 resistance zone, it may initiate a fresh decline. The initial support on the downside is at the $1.920 level, followed by the $1.90 mark. A break below $1.90 could lead to further declines toward $1.8650 and potentially down to $1.820. Should the selling pressure persist, XRP could fall below $1.750.
