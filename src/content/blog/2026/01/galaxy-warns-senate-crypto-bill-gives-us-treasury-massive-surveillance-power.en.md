## Overview

Galaxy has raised concerns regarding a draft crypto market structure bill currently under consideration in the Senate. The firm warns that the proposed legislation would significantly enhance the surveillance capabilities of the US Treasury, allowing it to freeze transactions and implement measures similar to those found in the Patriot Act.

## Concerns Raised by Galaxy

In a statement, Galaxy outlined that the draft bill could grant the US Treasury expansive new powers over cryptocurrency transactions. Specifically, the bill would enable the Treasury to freeze transactions, which could have far-reaching implications for users and businesses operating within the crypto space. This move has raised alarm bells among industry stakeholders, who fear that such authority may lead to overreach and a chilling effect on innovation.

The comparison to the Patriot Act is particularly striking, as this legislation was originally enacted in the wake of national security concerns. By likening the proposed crypto legislation to the Patriot Act, Galaxy underscores the potential for extensive surveillance and regulatory oversight that could emerge from this bill. The firm argues that these measures could compromise the foundational principles of privacy and decentralization that many cryptocurrencies are built upon.

The implications of this bill extend beyond the immediate powers it would confer to the Treasury. By establishing a framework that allows for increased transaction oversight, the legislation could alter the relationship between cryptocurrency users and regulatory bodies. This change might lead to a more cautious approach among investors and developers within the crypto market, who may fear repercussions from government actions.
