## Overview

The Truebit token experienced a dramatic decline of 99.9% following a significant security breach that resulted in the theft of $26.6 million in ether. This incident has raised concerns about the vulnerabilities present in older smart contracts and their potential consequences on the market and investors.

## What Happened

The recent attack targeted a flaw within an older smart contract associated with the Truebit token. This vulnerability was exploited by the attacker, who managed to purchase TRU tokens at no cost. By leveraging this loophole, the attacker was able to sell the tokens back, ultimately extracting a substantial amount of ether from the system.

The breach not only affected the value of the Truebit token but also highlighted the risks inherent in using outdated smart contracts. As the crypto market matures, the reliance on legacy systems can lead to severe repercussions, especially when they harbor undiscovered vulnerabilities. The ability of the attacker to manipulate the smart contract underscores the importance of continuous audits and updates to ensure the security of blockchain projects.
