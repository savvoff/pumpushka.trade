## Overview

Tokenization is emerging as a transformative approach that may democratize access to investment opportunities traditionally available only to a select few. This method could potentially allow a broader audience to participate in asset classes that have historically been the domain of pension funds, elite venture firms, family offices, and sovereign wealth funds.

## What Happened?

The concept of tokenization revolves around converting ownership of real-world assets into digital tokens that can be traded on blockchain platforms. This innovative process can unlock access to a wide range of assets, making it feasible for individual investors to engage with investment opportunities that were once out of reach. With the rise of decentralized finance (DeFi) and blockchain technology, tokenization is gaining traction in various sectors, including real estate, art, and even shares in high-profile companies.

One of the significant implications of tokenization is its potential to enhance liquidity in markets that are typically illiquid. By breaking down assets into smaller, tradable tokens, investors can buy and sell fractions of assets rather than needing to purchase entire units. This fractional ownership model can lead to increased market participation, as more individuals can afford to invest in high-value assets.

The historical context highlights that many lucrative investment opportunities have been reserved for institutional investors and wealthy individuals. Tokenization aims to level the playing field by providing retail investors access to these previously inaccessible assets. This shift could lead to a more inclusive financial ecosystem where diverse groups can invest in and benefit from asset appreciation.
