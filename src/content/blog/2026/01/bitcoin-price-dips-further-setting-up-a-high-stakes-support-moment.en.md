## Overview

Bitcoin has recently entered a downside correction after failing to hold above a critical price level. The cryptocurrency is currently facing significant resistance and may be approaching key support levels that could influence its future movement.

## Price Movement and Current Status

Bitcoin's price action shows a clear downside correction that began after it could not maintain levels above $94,000. The cryptocurrency's value fell below $93,200 and $92,500, leading it into a short-term bearish phase. As it stands, Bitcoin is trading below $92,000 and the 100 hourly Simple Moving Average, indicating a weakened position in the market.

The price has formed a bearish trend line with resistance identified at $92,650 on the hourly chart of the BTC/USD pair. If Bitcoin remains below the $92,500 threshold, further declines could be expected. The price has tested a low at $90,666, and while it is currently consolidating losses, it has also touched the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level from its recent decline.

For a potential recovery, Bitcoin needs to stabilize above $90,500. Immediate resistance is noted near the $91,400 mark, with further levels identified at $92,200 and $92,500. A close above the latter could suggest a rise toward $93,050, with additional resistance at $93,800 and $94,000.

Conversely, if Bitcoin fails to rise above the $92,500 resistance zone, it is likely to face more downside pressure. Immediate support is positioned around $90,500, followed by significant support levels at $90,000 and $89,000. A decline below these levels could lead to further drops, with the next major support identified at $87,200 and a critical level at $86,000, below which Bitcoin might see accelerated losses.
