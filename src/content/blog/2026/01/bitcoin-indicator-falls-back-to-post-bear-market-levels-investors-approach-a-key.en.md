## Overview

Bitcoin is currently trading below the $90,000 mark, reflecting a market characterized by indecision and increasing caution among investors. The inability to reclaim this psychological threshold has led to a lack of conviction from both buyers and sellers, heightening uncertainty about Bitcoin's future price movements.

## Current Market Conditions

The ongoing market phase has revealed a struggle among investors, with buyers hesitant to engage aggressively and sellers taking advantage of every price rebound. Despite the broader trend not collapsing entirely, the failure to hold key price levels has raised concerns about Bitcoin's next significant move. 

Top analyst Darkfost has noted that on-chain signals are beginning to reflect conditions typically observed near the conclusion of extended drawdowns. Bitcoin's unrealized profits and losses are approaching levels historically seen at the end of bear markets, suggesting that the market has already experienced a considerable reset in sentiment. This shift indicates potential stress building beneath the surface, even though the price itself has not yet entered a full capitulation phase.

Since Bitcoin's last all-time high, many late-arriving investors find themselves under increasing downside pressure as the market cools. This situation has resulted in shrinking unrealized profits and expanding unrealized losses, creating an environment that often compels traders to make a critical choice between holding through volatility or exiting under stress.

## Decision Point for Bitcoin Investors

Darkfost has emphasized a chart based on an adjusted version of Net Unrealized Profit/Loss (NUPL). This version aims to provide a more accurate representation of investor stress during changing market conditions. By incorporating the realized capitalization of both Short-Term Holders (STHs) and Long-Term Holders (LTHs), the model offers a clearer view of the profit or loss present “on paper” across the market.

To reduce market noise and better identify trend shifts, the metric is smoothed using an average, resulting in what Darkfost refers to as aNUPL. The critical takeaway from this analysis is that Bitcoin is nearing levels that have historically forced investors into a binary decision: either hold and accumulate or capitulate and lock in losses. 

The behavior of investors during this pivotal moment is crucial as it can significantly influence liquidity, sentiment, and the subsequent directional trend. If long-term participants choose to absorb the pressure and continue holding, the market may stabilize and initiate a recovery. Conversely, if selling accelerates among stressed investors, the decline could deepen, leading to a broader bear phase.
