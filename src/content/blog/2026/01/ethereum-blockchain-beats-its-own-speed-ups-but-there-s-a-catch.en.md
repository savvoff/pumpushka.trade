## Overview

In January, the Ethereum blockchain experienced a notable increase in daily active addresses, surpassing several prominent layer-2 networks. This uptick in activity is attributed to a reduction in transaction fees, which has encouraged more users to engage with the network.

## Ethereum’s Performance

Ethereum's daily active addresses have risen significantly, demonstrating the blockchain's resilience and continued appeal among its user base. The increase in activity is linked to a decline in transaction fees, which has made it more cost-effective for users to conduct transactions on the Ethereum network. As fees dropped, more individuals and entities took advantage of the lower costs, leading to a surge in on-chain activity.

The rise in daily active addresses is particularly notable as it has outpaced the activity levels observed on major layer-2 networks. Layer-2 solutions are designed to enhance the scalability of the Ethereum blockchain by processing transactions off the main chain, thereby reducing congestion and costs. However, despite these optimizations, Ethereum's own performance in January indicates that it remains a competitive option for users seeking to engage in blockchain activities.

This development matters for several reasons. Firstly, it highlights Ethereum’s ability to adapt and maintain its position as a leading blockchain platform, even amidst the growing popularity of layer-2 solutions. Secondly, the revival of on-chain activity suggests that users are regaining confidence in the Ethereum network, potentially leading to further developments and innovations within the ecosystem.

## From author

The dynamics observed in January present a fascinating case study of how transaction fees can influence user behavior on blockchain networks. It is essential to recognize that while lower fees can stimulate activity, other factors such as network speed, reliability, and overall user experience also play critical roles in determining a blockchain's success. The fact that Ethereum has managed to increase its daily active addresses despite the competition from layer-2 networks suggests a strong foundation and a loyal user base.

Moreover, this situation raises questions about the sustainability of such growth. Will Ethereum continue to attract users with lower fees, or will layer-2 solutions eventually provide a more compelling alternative? The ongoing evolution of both Ethereum and its layer-2 counterparts will be pivotal in shaping the future landscape of blockchain technology.
