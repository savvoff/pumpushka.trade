## Overview

Paradex, a decentralized exchange (DEX) built on Starknet, recently experienced a significant disruption due to a database migration error. This incident led to an unusual event where the price of Bitcoin was briefly recorded at zero, prompting the DEX to take corrective action by rolling back its blockchain to an earlier state.

## What Happened

The issue arose during a database migration process at Paradex, which is known for its operations on the Starknet blockchain. This migration error caused a temporary malfunction that affected the pricing mechanism of Bitcoin on the platform. As a result, the price of Bitcoin was erroneously displayed as zero, a scenario that is not typical for such a well-established cryptocurrency.

In response to the glitch, Paradex made the decision to reverse its blockchain to an earlier block. This rollback was a necessary step to mitigate the effects of the error and restore normal operations on the exchange. By reverting to a prior state, the team aimed to eliminate the erroneous data generated by the glitch and ensure that the platform could continue to function without further issues.

This incident highlights the vulnerabilities that can arise during technical processes such as database migrations. While such migrations are often essential for improving functionality and performance, they can also lead to significant disruptions if not executed flawlessly. The Paradex team’s quick response in rolling back the blockchain underscores the importance of having robust contingency plans in place for handling unexpected technical failures.
