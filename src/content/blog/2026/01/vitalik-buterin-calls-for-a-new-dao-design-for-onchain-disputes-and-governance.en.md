## Overview

Vitalik Buterin, a co-founder of Ethereum, has put forth a call for a reimagining of Decentralized Autonomous Organizations (DAOs). He emphasizes the necessity for these organizations to evolve beyond traditional token-voting mechanisms and to take on more significant roles in the management of essential blockchain infrastructure.

## The Call for Redesigning DAOs

In his recent statements, Buterin highlighted that the current structure of DAOs primarily relies on simple token-based voting systems, which he believes are insufficient for the complex governance needs of blockchain ecosystems. He argues that to effectively contribute to the development of core infrastructure, DAOs should be redesigned to handle more sophisticated functions such as oracles and on-chain courts.

Oracles serve as critical components in blockchain technology, providing real-world data to smart contracts. By integrating DAOs into the management of oracles, Buterin suggests that these organizations could enhance the reliability and decentralization of data feeds. This integration could lead to more robust and transparent systems, as DAOs can facilitate collective decision-making and governance processes that are essential in maintaining the integrity of oracles.

Additionally, Buterin pointed out the potential for DAOs to be involved in on-chain courts. These courts could serve as a decentralized mechanism for resolving disputes that arise within the blockchain ecosystem. By empowering DAOs to oversee such functions, Buterin believes that the governance structures of blockchain projects can become more democratic and less prone to centralization.

The implications of these changes are profound. With the increasing complexity of blockchain applications, the need for effective governance mechanisms becomes more critical. By advocating for a redesign of DAOs, Buterin is pushing for a system that not only facilitates governance but also enhances the overall functionality and resilience of blockchain networks.

## From author

The evolution of DAOs as proposed by Buterin could signify a pivotal shift in how decentralized governance is perceived and implemented. The current reliance on token voting, while functional, often leads to challenges such as voter apathy and the influence of whale investors. By proposing a model that incorporates more complex governance structures, Buterin opens the door for a more engaged and equitable decision-making process. This could ultimately lead to a more sustainable and effective blockchain ecosystem.

The integration of DAOs into critical infrastructure roles could also encourage innovation and collaboration among different blockchain projects. By establishing a framework where DAOs manage oracles and on-chain courts, a new standard for decentralized governance and operational efficiency may emerge. This could foster an environment where projects can operate more seamlessly and with greater trust.
