This rise in complaints, which has doubled since late 2025, raises serious concerns regarding the impact of artificial intelligence on privacy and consent, particularly in sensitive contexts involving children.

## What Happened

The Commissioner noted a troubling trend in the number of reports regarding non-consensual AI-generated sexual images. Specifically, these reports have doubled since late 2025, indicating a growing issue that demands attention. Among these complaints, some cases involve children, which amplifies the severity and urgency of the situation. 

The increase in complaints highlights the challenges posed by advancements in AI technology, particularly in generating images that may infringe upon personal rights and consent. The ability of AI to create realistic images has outpaced the regulatory frameworks in place, leading to situations where individuals, including minors, may be exploited without their consent. 

This development is alarming as it not only raises ethical questions but also underscores the potential for AI to be misused in ways that can cause significant harm to individuals. The implications of such misuse are profound, affecting not only the victims but also the broader societal norms surrounding consent and privacy.
