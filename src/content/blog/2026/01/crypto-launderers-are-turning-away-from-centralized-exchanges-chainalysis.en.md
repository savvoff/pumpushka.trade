## Overview

Recent findings from Chainalysis reveal a significant shift in the landscape of cryptocurrency laundering. The on-chain money laundering ecosystem processed a staggering amount of funds in 2025, highlighting the evolving tactics used by illicit actors. Notably, Chinese-language networks have emerged as the dominant force in this illicit activity.

## The Shift in Money Laundering Tactics

According to Chainalysis, the on-chain money laundering ecosystem has seen a dramatic increase in the volume of funds processed, amounting to $82 billion in 2025. This figure underscores the growing sophistication and scale of money laundering operations within the cryptocurrency space. 

A key aspect of this trend is the notable shift away from centralized exchanges. Historically, centralized exchanges have been a common target for money laundering activities due to their high liquidity and ease of access. However, the current trend indicates that crypto launderers are increasingly favoring decentralized platforms and networks, particularly those that operate in Chinese languages.

The dominance of Chinese-language networks suggests that these platforms may offer unique advantages or methods that appeal to those engaged in illicit activities. This trend raises concerns about the effectiveness of regulatory measures and the ability to track and combat money laundering in the cryptocurrency ecosystem.

The implications of this shift are significant for law enforcement and regulatory agencies. As launderers adapt to avoid detection, it becomes increasingly challenging to monitor and mitigate the risks associated with cryptocurrency transactions. Furthermore, the rise of decentralized platforms complicates the landscape for compliance efforts, as these networks often operate without the oversight typically associated with centralized exchanges.
