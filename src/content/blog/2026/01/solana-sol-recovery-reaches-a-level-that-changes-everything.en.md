## Overview

Solana (SOL) has initiated a recovery wave, moving above the $125 mark, but is currently encountering resistance around the $128 level. This movement is significant as it indicates a potential shift in market dynamics for Solana, especially in relation to other cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum.

## Solana's Recovery and Current Status

Recently, Solana's price began a recovery wave that saw it rise above the $125 threshold, following an initial bounce from the $118 level. This upward movement is noteworthy as it signifies a break away from recent bearish trends. The price is currently trading above both the $125 mark and the 100-hourly simple moving average, highlighting a shift toward bullish sentiment.

A key event in this recovery was the break above a bearish trend line with resistance at $124 on the hourly chart of the SOL/USD pair. This breakout suggests that buyers are gaining control and are willing to push prices higher. The price has also surpassed the 61.8% Fibonacci retracement level from a recent downward move, indicating that the recovery may have some technical backing.

However, the market is not without its challenges. Solana's price is facing immediate resistance near the $128 level, which is further compounded by the 76.4% Fibonacci retracement level of the downward move. If SOL can successfully navigate past this resistance, the next significant hurdles will be at the $130 and $135 levels. A successful close above the $135 resistance could signal a further bullish trend, potentially leading to prices that reach the $142 and even $145 levels.

Conversely, if Solana fails to break above the $128 resistance, there could be a decline in its price. Initial support is observed around the $124.50 zone, with more substantial support near the $122 level. A drop below this level could lead to further declines toward the $117 support zone. Should the price close below $117, it may indicate a more severe bearish trend, with potential drops to the $105 level.
