## Overview

Bitcoin has recently experienced a significant price increase, surpassing the $92,000 mark and showing signs of bullish momentum. However, it faces potential challenges at the $95,000 resistance level, which could impact its upward trajectory in the near term.

## Recent Price Movements

Bitcoin's price began a fresh increase, moving above the $92,200 zone. Currently, it is trading above $92,500 and the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average. A key bullish trend line is forming, providing support at $92,650 on the hourly chart of the BTC/USD pair. This trend suggests that if Bitcoin maintains its position above the $92,000 zone, it may continue to rise.

After remaining supported above the $91,200 zone, Bitcoin started to gain momentum, breaking through the $92,000 and $92,200 resistance levels. The price even exceeded $94,000, reaching a new multi-week high at $94,783 before entering a phase of consolidation. Following this peak, there was a slight decline below the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level of the recent upward move from a swing low of $90,805 to the high of $94,783.

Bitcoin continues to trade above $92,500, supported by the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average. Immediate resistance levels are identified around $94,200 and $94,500, with a critical barrier at $95,000. If Bitcoin manages to close above this resistance, it could pave the way for further increases, potentially reaching the $95,800 resistance level, followed by targets at $96,500, $97,000, and $97,200.

## Potential Downside Risks

Conversely, if Bitcoin fails to break through the $94,500 resistance zone, it may face another decline. Immediate support is located near the $93,200 level, with the first major support around $92,800, coinciding with the 50% Fibonacci retracement level of the recent upward move. Additional support can be found near the $92,500 zone, and if losses continue, the price could drop toward the $91,200 support level. The main support level is positioned at $90,000, and a drop below this threshold could result in a more significant downward movement.
