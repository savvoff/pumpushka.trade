## Overview

Solana (SOL) has experienced a significant decline, failing to maintain stability above key price levels. The digital asset is currently consolidating losses below $130, with bearish momentum suggesting potential further declines if certain support levels are breached.

## Solana's Recent Price Movement

Solana's price has been on a downward trajectory, starting a fresh decline after failing to settle above $132. The cryptocurrency has now dipped below the $130 mark and is struggling to initiate a recovery wave. This decline mirrors trends seen in other major cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin and Ethereum.

As SOL traded below critical support levels of $130 and $126, the bearish momentum intensified, leading to a low formation at $117. In the current market conditions, Solana is consolidating its losses, with minor recoveries observed as it climbed slightly above the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level of the downward move from the swing high of $132 to the low of $117.

Despite this slight recovery, Solana remains below the $130 threshold and the 100-hourly simple moving average, indicating a continued bearish outlook. Immediate resistance is identified near the $125 level, which corresponds with the 50% Fibonacci retracement level from the recent decline. The next significant resistance point is at $126, where a key bearish trend line is forming on the hourly chart of the SOL/USD pair.

If Solana can close successfully above the $132 resistance zone, it may pave the way for a more stable increase in price. However, this scenario appears contingent on overcoming the immediate resistance levels.
