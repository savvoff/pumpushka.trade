## Overview

Noble, a blockchain entity, is transitioning from the Cosmos ecosystem to establish a standalone Ethereum Virtual Machine (EVM)-compatible blockchain. This move is aimed at enhancing developer access and creating a more robust technological framework for its upcoming stablecoin-focused platform, which is scheduled to launch in March.

## Transitioning to Standalone EVM

Noble's decision to shift from Cosmos to a standalone EVM-compatible blockchain is a strategic initiative intended to improve the experience for developers. By opting for an EVM architecture, Noble aims to leverage the widespread familiarity and established tooling that comes with Ethereum compatibility. This shift is expected to streamline the development process and attract a broader range of developers who are already accustomed to working within the Ethereum ecosystem.

The move to a standalone blockchain signifies Noble's commitment to building a more tailored and efficient platform for its stablecoin-focused ambitions. A dedicated blockchain allows for greater optimization and customization, which could enhance performance and scalability. This is particularly important for stablecoins, which require a stable and reliable infrastructure to maintain their value and facilitate transactions.

The launch of this new blockchain is anticipated to take place in March, positioning Noble to capitalize on the growing demand for stablecoins in the cryptocurrency market. Stablecoins have gained significant traction as they provide a bridge between traditional fiat currencies and the volatile world of cryptocurrencies. By focusing on stablecoin development, Noble is aligning itself with a key trend in the crypto space.
