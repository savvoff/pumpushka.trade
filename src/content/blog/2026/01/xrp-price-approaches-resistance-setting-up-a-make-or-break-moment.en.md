## XRP Price Movement

XRP price has extended its losses, trading below the $2.10 level after a fresh decline that began below the $2.120 zone. The price is currently positioned below the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average, indicating a bearish trend. A key bearish trend line is forming, which presents resistance at the $2.10 mark on the hourly chart of the XRP/USD pair. The ongoing situation suggests that XRP could continue to decline if it remains below the $2.120 level.

The price failed to maintain levels above $2.20, similar to the movements observed in major cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin and Ethereum. Following this failure, XRP dropped below $2.150 and $2.120, entering a short-term bearish zone. During this decline, the price even dipped below $2.050, reaching a low of $2.034. However, it is now attempting to initiate a recovery, having moved above $2.080, although it remains below the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level of the downward movement from a swing high to the low.

Currently, XRP is trading below $2.120 and the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average, which indicates that the price might face resistance near the $2.10 level if there is a fresh upward move. The first major resistance level is identified near $2.120. A close above this level could potentially propel the price toward $2.220 or the 50% Fibonacci retracement level of the recent downward movement. Further resistance levels are noted at $2.320, with an upward breakout above this point possibly leading to $2.350 and beyond.

Should XRP fail to break through the $2.120 resistance zone, it may initiate another decline. The initial support on the downside is located near the $2.050 level, followed by a significant support level at $2.020. If the price closes below the $2.020 level, it might continue to decline toward $2.00, with the next major support situated near the $1.9650 zone.
