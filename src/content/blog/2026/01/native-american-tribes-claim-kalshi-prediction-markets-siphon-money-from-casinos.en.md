## Overview

Native American tribes in Connecticut have expressed their concerns regarding the operations of Kalshi, a prediction market platform. In an amicus brief, these tribes argue that Kalshi's activities divert significant funds from local casinos, impacting their revenue streams.

## The Situation

The Connecticut tribes have taken a stand against Kalshi by filing an amicus brief that supports the state's efforts to regulate prediction market operators. They claim that Kalshi's operations have detrimental effects on the financial health of tribal casinos. The brief highlights the tribes' belief that prediction markets like Kalshi are siphoning off money that would otherwise be spent in casinos.

The tribes emphasize the importance of casino revenue for their communities, which often rely on these funds for economic stability and development. By drawing attention to the financial implications of prediction markets, the tribes aim to underscore the need for regulatory measures that protect their economic interests.

The amicus brief serves as a formal statement of support for the state's crackdown on prediction market operators, positioning the tribes as advocates for their economic wellbeing. The assertion that Kalshi siphons away revenue suggests that the tribes view the platform as a competitor that undermines the traditional gaming industry, which is a significant source of income for them.
