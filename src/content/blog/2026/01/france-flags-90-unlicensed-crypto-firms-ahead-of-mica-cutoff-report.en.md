## Overview

France has identified 90 crypto firms that are operating without the necessary license under the Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) framework. A significant portion of these firms, 40%, are not pursuing the required licensing, while 30% have not responded to regulatory inquiries. This situation raises concerns as regulators signal potential shutdowns by July for non-compliant entities.

## What Happened

In a recent development, French regulators have flagged 90 cryptocurrency firms that lack a MiCA license. The MiCA legislation is designed to establish a comprehensive regulatory framework for digital assets within the European Union. This framework aims to enhance consumer protection and ensure market integrity in the rapidly evolving crypto space.

Among the flagged firms, a notable 40% have chosen not to seek the MiCA license, indicating a reluctance or inability to comply with the new regulatory standards. Additionally, 30% of these firms have not responded to the communications from regulators, further complicating the situation. The MiCA regulations are set to impose stricter oversight and operational requirements on crypto businesses, making compliance essential for continued operation.

The French regulatory authorities have issued warnings that firms failing to secure the necessary licenses by the upcoming deadline in July could face shutdowns. This ultimatum underscores the urgency for these companies to either align themselves with the regulatory framework or risk losing their ability to operate legally within the market. The MiCA regulations are seen as a pivotal step in establishing a more secure and transparent crypto environment, and the response from these unlicensed firms will be closely monitored by both regulators and market participants.

## From author

The situation in France reflects a broader trend within the European Union as it seeks to regulate the burgeoning crypto market. The MiCA framework represents a significant regulatory shift aimed at addressing the challenges posed by the rapid growth of digital assets. The response from the flagged firms could serve as an indicator of the industry's willingness to adapt to regulatory requirements. The mixed responses—some firms not seeking licensing and others remaining unresponsive—raise questions about the overall compliance landscape in the crypto market.

As regulatory frameworks evolve, the actions taken by both regulators and firms will likely influence the future of cryptocurrency operations in Europe. The potential shutdown of non-compliant firms could lead to a consolidation within the industry, where only those companies willing to adhere to regulations survive. This could reshape the competitive landscape and impact the way consumers engage with crypto services in France and beyond.
