## Overview

Jake Claver, a prominent advocate for XRP and CEO of Digital Ascension Group, has reaffirmed his belief in the potential of XRP despite facing criticism for his previous price predictions. Claver's recent statements emphasize institutional adoption and the significance of non-disclosure agreements in the context of XRP's future.

## Claver's Position on XRP

In a series of posts from late December to early January, Claver reiterated his stance that XRP is poised for significant institutional adoption. He responded to analyst Zach Rector's recent criticism regarding his prediction of a $100 XRP target by the end of 2025. Claver acknowledged the need for patience, stating that "timelines always get extended." He highlighted his experience over the past three years, working with various partners and regulators in the cryptocurrency space.

Claver's messaging centers around the notion of “domino” catalysts, suggesting that real-world events will lead to XRP becoming a foundational element of future financial markets. He specifically pointed out that Ripple, the company behind XRP, has signed over 1,700 non-disclosure agreements, which he interprets as a sign that major institutions, including governments, global banks, payment networks, universities, and Fortune 500 companies, are preparing to incorporate XRP into their operations.

In his posts, Claver expressed optimism about the future of XRP, stating that major institutions are accumulating XRP behind the scenes while maintaining a low profile in public discussions. He characterized the current price of XRP as merely a shadow of its potential, arguing that the cryptocurrency will eventually serve as the backbone of international finance. Claver believes that the hesitation seen in the market today will translate into regret for those who fail to recognize XRP's value.

## Criticism from Analysts

The criticism from Zach Rector focused not on the boldness of Claver's predictions but rather on the manner in which they are presented. Rector contended that the forecast of a 5,000% price increase within the given timeframe lacks a plausible scenario and suggested that Claver's messaging could imply privileged insights rather than a probabilistic view of XRP's future.

Rector also raised concerns about discrepancies between Claver's public statements and the feedback he has received from investors in the XRP community. He suggested that there may be a significant gap between the optimistic narrative promoted by Claver and the private sentiments of XRP investors.
