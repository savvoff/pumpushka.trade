## Overview

XRP's price has recently experienced a notable decline, falling below the $2.10 mark. The cryptocurrency is currently consolidating its losses, and there are signs that it may decline further if it does not hold above a key support level.

## Price Movement and Technical Analysis

XRP began a downward trend, breaking below the $2.120 zone, which led to a series of losses. The price dipped below $2.10 and the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average, indicating a shift into a bearish zone. Following this decline, XRP formed a low at $2.052 before attempting to stabilize.

Despite attempts to regain momentum, the price faced significant resistance. A key bearish trend line has formed, with resistance identified at $2.0850 on the hourly chart of the XRP/USD pair. This trend line suggests that the price could continue its downward trajectory if it does not reclaim the $2.10 level.

XRP's struggle to maintain levels above $2.150 reflects broader trends observed in the cryptocurrency market, particularly with Bitcoin and Ethereum also experiencing similar downward movements. The price of XRP even briefly fell below $2.080, highlighting the prevailing bearish sentiment.

The technical indicators paint a concerning picture for XRP. The hourly MACD is gaining momentum in the bearish zone, while the Relative Strength Index (RSI) has dipped below the neutral 50 level, further suggesting a lack of buying pressure.

If XRP fails to breach the $2.120 resistance zone, it may face further declines. The immediate support levels to watch are $2.050 and $2.020. A break below the latter could lead to a continued downward trend, with potential targets at $1.950 and $1.920.
