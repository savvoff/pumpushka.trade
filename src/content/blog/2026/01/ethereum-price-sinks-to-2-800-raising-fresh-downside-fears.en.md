## Overview

Ethereum has experienced a significant decline in its price, falling below the $2,865 level and testing the $2,800 mark. This downward trend raises concerns about further losses as the cryptocurrency struggles to maintain stability. The current market conditions indicate a bearish sentiment surrounding Ethereum's performance.

## Recent Price Movements

Ethereum's price has extended its losses, failing to remain stable above the $2,950 threshold. Following a decline below the $2,880 and $2,865 levels, the cryptocurrency entered a bearish zone. The selling pressure intensified, pushing the price below $2,840 and eventually testing the critical support level of $2,800. Currently, Ethereum is consolidating its losses while trading below both the $2,900 mark and the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average.

The bearish trend is further illustrated by the formation of a trend line with resistance at $2,920 on the hourly chart of ETH/USD. In order for Ethereum to initiate a recovery, it must first clear this resistance level. The immediate resistance is identified near the $2,920 region, while a more significant resistance is found near $2,960. The potential for a price increase exists if Ethereum can remain above the $2,800 support level.

## Market Dynamics

Ethereum's current price movements are reflective of broader market trends, as it follows a similar pattern to Bitcoin. The cryptocurrency faced challenges in maintaining its value as it dipped below key support levels. The price's failure to hold above $2,950 signals a bearish outlook, and the bears have firmly established control over the market.

A minor upside was noted, with Ethereum briefly surpassing the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level of the downward wave from the recent swing high to swing low. However, this did not lead to a sustained recovery, and the price remains under pressure. If the bearish trend continues and Ethereum fails to overcome the $2,920 resistance, it could face a fresh decline.
