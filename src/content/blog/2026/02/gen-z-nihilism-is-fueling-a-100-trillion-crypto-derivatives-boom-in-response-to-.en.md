## Overview

The rising costs associated with housing have rendered home ownership unattainable for many individuals of Generation Z. This financial landscape has contributed to a sentiment of nihilism among this demographic, as highlighted by David Pakman from CoinFund. This shift in mentality is linked to a burgeoning interest in crypto derivatives, which some analysts estimate could contribute to a $100 trillion market.

## The Rise of Financial Nihilism Among Gen Z

As housing prices continue to climb, many Gen Z individuals find themselves facing a harsh reality where traditional pathways to financial stability, such as home ownership, are increasingly out of reach. This situation has led to feelings of disillusionment and despair regarding their financial futures. David Pakman, a representative from CoinFund, emphasizes that this "financial nihilism" is a significant factor influencing the behavior and investment choices of younger generations.

In this context, the interest in crypto derivatives is surging. These financial instruments provide new avenues for speculation and investment, allowing individuals to engage with the financial market outside of conventional assets like real estate. The potential for high returns, coupled with the accessibility of crypto trading platforms, makes these derivatives an attractive option for those who feel disenfranchised by the traditional economic system.

Pakman’s observations suggest that as Gen Z grapples with the realities of a broken financial system, their engagement with crypto derivatives may reflect a broader desire to find alternative ways to achieve financial security and independence. This shift not only signifies a change in investment strategies but also highlights a generational response to economic challenges that many feel are insurmountable.
