## Overview

Ethereum has recently experienced a significant decline, falling below the critical $2,100 mark, which has intensified short-term bearish sentiment. Despite this downturn, a historical fractal pattern suggests that a potential reversal could occur, offering a sliver of hope for traders and investors.

## Current Market Situation

Ethereum's technical structure has deteriorated as it decisively broke below the $2,100 level. This decline has heightened bearish pressure in the short term. The loss of this key support zone, previously established between $2,300 and $2,100, indicates a structural breakdown, as noted by Crypto Candy. This area was once a strong demand region, effectively absorbing selling pressure. The failure to maintain this support level marks a significant shift in market dynamics, indicating a weakening broader market structure.

Following this breakdown, the former support zone has transformed into a considerable resistance area, complicating any attempts by Ethereum to reclaim the $2,100 to $2,300 range. Recent attempts to regain acceptance above this threshold have been unsuccessful, reinforcing the notion that sellers are actively defending this level. Consequently, the short-term momentum remains tilted to the downside.

If the bearish momentum persists, traders should keep an eye on the next major support region, which lies between $1,700 and $1,500. A decline into this range would align with typical market behavior following a failed reclaim of previously established support. Presently, the market bias remains bearish as long as Ethereum continues trading below the $2,300 to $2,100 zone. A strong reclaim accompanied by sustained consolidation above this range would be necessary to invalidate the current downside scenario.

## Fractal Analysis

Trader Tardigrade provided an intriguing update on Ethereum's weekly performance, highlighting a fractal comparison that suggests a familiar price structure may be developing. The analysis points to the formation of a rectangular consolidation box, akin to the price behavior observed prior to Ethereum’s notable rally in late 2025.

In that previous cycle, Ethereum spent several weeks consolidating within a clearly defined horizontal range, building momentum before eventually breaking out with significant force. The current chart displays a similar box pattern, situated within a broader ascending channel. The resemblance between these two structures implies that the current consolidation phase may not be coincidental but rather indicative of a recurring cyclical pattern.

If the fractal pattern continues to evolve as it did in the past, a decisive breakout above the current range could lead to a substantial upward movement. Just like in 2025, the longer Ethereum's price remains compressed within this box, the more aggressive the eventual move could be. A confirmed break and sustained acceptance above this range would signal a transition from accumulation to markup, potentially revitalizing bullish sentiment.
