## Overview

Polymarket has announced a significant change in its operational structure by transitioning from using bridged USDC on the Polygon network to utilizing Circle-issued native USDC. This strategic move aims to decrease the platform's dependence on cross-chain bridges as it continues to expand its prediction market services.

## Migration to Native USDC

The decision to migrate to native USDC from bridged USDC is a critical step for Polymarket. By adopting Circle-issued USDC, the platform is positioning itself to enhance its operational efficiency and security within the growing landscape of prediction markets. Cross-chain bridges, while useful for transferring assets across different blockchain networks, can introduce vulnerabilities and complexities. By reducing reliance on these bridges, Polymarket aims to streamline its processes and mitigate potential risks associated with cross-chain transactions.

The shift to native USDC is particularly significant as it reflects a broader trend in the cryptocurrency market where platforms are increasingly prioritizing security and reliability. Native assets, like USDC issued directly by Circle, often provide a more stable and trustworthy medium for transactions compared to bridged assets, which can be subject to additional risks.

This migration is not just a technical adjustment; it holds substantial implications for the user experience on Polymarket. Users can expect improved transaction speeds and potentially lower fees as the platform moves to a more efficient settlement method. Additionally, this change may enhance the overall liquidity on Polymarket, attracting more users to participate in its prediction markets.
