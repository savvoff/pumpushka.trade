## Overview

Bitcoin's recent price action has shifted into a bearish trend after falling below a crucial support level that had sustained its rally for several months. Currently, Bitcoin is trading at a significantly lower price, with analysts indicating that this shift marks a pivotal change in its technical structure.

## Recent Developments

Bitcoin's price has dropped below an important previous low, which was instrumental in supporting its upward movement for an extended period. The price fell to a low of $77,082 in the past 24 hours, and Bitcoin is currently trading at $78,560. This decline has prompted analysis from XForceGlobal, a crypto analyst, who claims that the recent movements represent a substantial change in Bitcoin's technical framework.

XForceGlobal's analysis, based on Elliott Wave principles, suggests that the invalidation of the bullish structure many traders relied upon has now occurred. As a result, lower price levels are becoming increasingly probable in the coming weeks and months. The analysis indicates that Bitcoin had been navigating a complex sideways structure, characterized as a WXY combination, which was expected to resolve through distribution rather than a direct breakdown. 

Despite bulls managing to complete three of the five components necessary for this triangle-like structure, the inability to maintain the previous low has led to a significant structural shift. The prior low in question was the $82,000 mark seen in November 2025. The failure to defend this level during the recent drop below $80,000 has rendered the primary wave count unsustainable. 

In terms of Elliott Wave analysis, this lower low implies that the price action from Bitcoin's all-time high should now be regarded as distinct and corrective, rather than indicative of a healthy continuation. This change in structure allows for a more extensive decline from a Fibonacci extension perspective and necessitates a reevaluation of both minimum and maximum downside targets.
