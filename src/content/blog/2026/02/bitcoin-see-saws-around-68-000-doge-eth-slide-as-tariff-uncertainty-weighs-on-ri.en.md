Bitcoin has been fluctuating around $68,000, while both DOGE and ETH have experienced declines. This volatility comes in the context of heightened uncertainty in global markets, primarily due to recent trade policy developments.

## Recent Developments

President Donald Trump has enacted a global tariff increase to 15%, which has significant implications for international trade. This decision follows a Supreme Court ruling that opposed earlier emergency trade measures, indicating a complicated legal landscape surrounding trade policies. The increase in tariffs is expected to exacerbate tensions between the United States and China, among other trade partners. 

The increase in tariffs adds pressure to an already strained economic environment, leading to heightened uncertainty among investors. As risk assets react to these developments, Bitcoin's price stability has been challenged, causing fluctuations around the $68,000 mark. Meanwhile, the declines in both DOGE and ETH reflect broader market sentiment, as investors reassess their positions in light of potential economic impacts stemming from the tariff changes.

## Why It Matters

The decision to raise tariffs can have far-reaching implications for the global economy. Tariffs generally lead to increased costs for imported goods, which can contribute to inflationary pressures. This situation may lead to decreased consumer spending and a slowdown in economic growth. For the cryptocurrency market, which is often viewed as a risk asset, these economic indicators can influence investor behavior and market dynamics.

As Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies are increasingly seen as alternative investments, their performance can be directly affected by shifts in investor sentiment driven by global economic conditions. The uncertainty surrounding tariffs and trade relations may lead to increased volatility in crypto markets, as traders react to news and adjust their strategies accordingly.
