## Overview

Bitcoin experienced a significant decline, falling below the $61,000 mark. This drop can be attributed to the unwinding of leveraged bets alongside a broader market selloff, which has erased all gains that had accumulated since the election of President Donald Trump.

## What Happened

The recent downturn in Bitcoin's price has been driven by a combination of factors. The unwinding of leveraged positions has played a crucial role in amplifying the selloff. Leveraged trading allows investors to borrow funds to increase their exposure to an asset, and when markets begin to decline, these positions can be liquidated, leading to further price declines.

In addition to the unwinding of these leveraged bets, broader market turbulence has contributed to the decrease in Bitcoin's value. Market fluctuations can lead to increased volatility, and when investors react to uncertain conditions by selling off assets, it can create a ripple effect that impacts various sectors, including cryptocurrencies.

The timing of this decline is notable as it coincides with the aftermath of President Donald Trump's election. The election initially sparked a surge in speculative interest in cryptocurrencies, with many investors believing that a Trump presidency would lead to favorable conditions for digital assets. However, the recent price action suggests a stark reversal of that sentiment, as all gains accrued since the election have now been wiped out.
