## Overview

Standard Chartered has revised its price targets for Bitcoin and Ether, predicting that Bitcoin may drop to $50,000 and Ether to $1,400 before experiencing a recovery. This adjustment follows the bank's assessment of the current market conditions, which are influenced by ETF outflows and broader macroeconomic challenges.

## Price Target Adjustments

In a recent announcement, Standard Chartered indicated a downward revision of its 2026 price targets for major cryptocurrencies. The bank's outlook reflects concerns regarding ongoing market dynamics that could lead to further near-term capitulation in the digital asset space. The decision to lower these targets is primarily attributed to the impact of ETF outflows, which are contributing to a bearish sentiment in the cryptocurrency market. Additionally, macroeconomic headwinds are compounding the challenges faced by digital assets, leading to increased volatility and uncertainty.

The implications of this revised outlook are significant, as it underscores the current struggles within the cryptocurrency sector. Investors and market participants may need to brace for potential price declines in the short term, as the factors influencing these changes appear to be deeply rooted in both market sentiment and external economic conditions.
