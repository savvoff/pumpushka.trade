## Overview

Recent developments surrounding artificial intelligence have triggered a notable selloff in professional-services stocks. This shift arises from growing apprehensions regarding the potential disruption AI agents may cause to conventional software pricing structures.

## What Happened

The professional-services sector experienced a significant decline, primarily driven by concerns that advancements in AI technology could fundamentally alter the pricing models of traditional software. As AI agents become increasingly capable, businesses and consumers may begin to favor these innovative solutions over established software offerings. This shift in preference raises questions about the sustainability of existing software pricing strategies.

The implications of this selloff extend beyond mere stock performance; they signal a broader reevaluation of the entire software industry. Investors are now forced to consider how AI developments will affect the competitive landscape and the financial health of companies that rely heavily on traditional software sales. The uncertainty surrounding AI's impact on pricing has led to a reassessment of valuations within the sector, as market participants grapple with the potential for reduced revenues among companies unwilling or unable to adapt to these changes.
