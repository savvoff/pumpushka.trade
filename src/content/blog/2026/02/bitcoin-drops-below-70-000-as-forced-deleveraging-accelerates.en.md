## Overview

Bitcoin experienced a significant decline, falling below the $70,000 mark on Thursday. This drop highlights the ongoing negative momentum in the cryptocurrency market, which is being exacerbated by diminishing marginal demand and volatility in other asset classes.

## What Happened

On Thursday, Bitcoin's value decreased, marking a notable threshold as it fell below $70,000. This decline is indicative of a broader trend affecting the cryptocurrency market, which has been characterized by increasing negativity. The drop in Bitcoin's price is attributed to a combination of factors, including a lack of marginal demand and recent fluctuations in technology equities and precious metals. 

The term "forced deleveraging" has been used to describe the current market conditions, suggesting that investors may be compelled to sell off assets to meet margin calls or to reduce leverage in their portfolios. This phenomenon often leads to further selling pressure, creating a cycle that can exacerbate price declines across various asset classes, including cryptocurrencies.

The relationship between Bitcoin and other financial markets, particularly tech equities and precious metals, is also significant. As these markets experience volatility, it can lead to shifts in investor sentiment and behavior, impacting demand for Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies. The interconnectivity of these markets suggests that movements in one area can have ripple effects, influencing the overall market dynamics of cryptocurrencies.
