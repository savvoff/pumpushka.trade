## Overview

Startale and SBI have collaborated to launch a new blockchain initiative aimed at enhancing institutional foreign exchange (FX) and real-world asset (RWA) trading. This initiative, named Strium, is set to begin with synthetic US and Japanese stocks and commodities, paving the way for future developments in real tokenized shares and asset-backed products.

## Details of the Initiative

The Strium blockchain is designed to cater specifically to institutional trading needs, focusing on the FX and RWA markets. By starting with synthetic assets, Strium aims to provide a platform that can handle complex trading scenarios while leveraging the benefits of blockchain technology. 

The initial offerings will include synthetic stocks and commodities tied to major markets, specifically those of the United States and Japan. This approach allows for a more flexible trading environment and serves as a preliminary step toward more advanced offerings. 

As the platform evolves, there are plans to introduce real tokenized shares and asset-backed products. This transition will incorporate identity verification measures to ensure compliance and security in trading practices. The emphasis on identity verification is crucial, as it reflects the growing importance of regulatory compliance in the financial sector, particularly within the blockchain space.

The collaboration between Startale and SBI signifies a strategic move to enhance the efficiency and transparency of institutional trading. By utilizing blockchain technology, the initiative aims to streamline processes and reduce costs associated with traditional trading methods.
