## Overview

Ethereum's price has recently dropped below the critical $2,000 support level, continuing a broader decline in the cryptocurrency market. With the price now hovering around $1,750, Ethereum faces significant resistance as it attempts to recover. This situation is concerning for investors as it highlights the ongoing bearish trend affecting the crypto market.

## Price Movement and Current Status

Ethereum has extended its decline, falling below both the $2,000 and $1,950 thresholds. Following this drop, ETH is currently trying to recover from a low of $1,744. However, the recovery efforts face substantial hurdles, particularly around the $2,200 resistance level. The price is trading below both the $2,000 mark and the 100-hourly Simple Moving Average, indicating a bearish trend.

The recent price action mirrors a similar decline seen in Bitcoin, with Ethereum's price dropping more than 15% from its previous levels. The bears have driven the price below $1,880, solidifying a bearish sentiment in the market. Despite a brief rally above $1,850, the price remains entrenched in a bearish zone.

Ethereum's failure to maintain stability above $2,200 has led to this downward trajectory. The current resistance levels are critical, with immediate resistance noted around $1,950. If Ethereum can manage to stay above $1,800, there may be a chance for further price increases. However, the key resistance levels lie at $2,050 and $2,200, where a significant bearish trend line is forming.

Should the price surpass the $2,200 resistance, it may lead to further upward momentum toward the $2,350 resistance area. Conversely, if Ethereum fails to break through the $2,050 resistance, it could initiate another round of declines. Initial support levels are observed around $1,850, with major support sitting near the $1,800 zone. A clear move below this support could push the price toward $1,750 and potentially lower.
