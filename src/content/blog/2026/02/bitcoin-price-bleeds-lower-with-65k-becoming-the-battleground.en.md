## Overview

Bitcoin's price has recently encountered significant downward pressure, failing to maintain stability above key levels. The cryptocurrency is now trading below critical support zones, raising concerns about further declines in the near term.

## Current Price Movement

Bitcoin's price has struggled to stay above the $68,800 mark, initiating a fresh decline. Currently, BTC is trading below $67,500 and is moving lower beneath both the $68,000 and $67,000 levels. The price has dipped below the 100 hourly simple moving average, indicating a bearish trend. 

A bearish trend line is forming with resistance at the $67,500 level on the hourly chart of the BTC/USD pair. The recent price action has seen Bitcoin fall below the 50% Fibonacci retracement level of its upward move, which originated from a swing low of $60,500 and reached a high of $72,255. This decline has pushed the price below $65,500, further confirming the bearish sentiment in the market.

## Technical Analysis

As Bitcoin continues to trade below $67,000, it faces immediate resistance near the $67,500 level and the trend line. A close above the $68,000 resistance could indicate a potential for recovery, with targets possibly extending toward $69,200 and $70,500. However, if Bitcoin fails to breach the $68,000 resistance zone, it risks entering another phase of decline.

Immediate support for Bitcoin is located around the $65,500 level, with the first major support identified near $65,000. This level coincides with the 61.8% Fibonacci retracement level of the upward move from the previous swing low. Should the price decline further, the next support area is near $62,750, with significant support at $60,500. A breach below this level could pose challenges for any potential recovery in the near term.

The technical indicators are also reflecting bearish momentum. The hourly MACD is gaining pace in the bearish zone, while the Relative Strength Index (RSI) for BTC/USD is currently positioned below the 50 level, further confirming the bearish outlook.
