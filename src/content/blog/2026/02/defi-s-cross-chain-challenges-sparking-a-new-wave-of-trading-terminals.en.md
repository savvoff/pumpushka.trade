## Overview

The decentralized finance (DeFi) landscape is undergoing significant transformations as liquidity becomes increasingly fragmented across various blockchain networks. This fragmentation is prompting the development of unified trading terminals aimed at re-aggregating liquidity, which could potentially mediate a substantial portion of DeFi trading volume in the coming years.

## The Fragmentation of Liquidity in DeFi

The current state of DeFi is characterized by a growing division of liquidity across multiple blockchain networks. As more projects and protocols emerge, the distribution of assets becomes more dispersed, making it challenging for traders to access the liquidity they need on a single platform. This fragmentation can lead to inefficiencies in trading, increased slippage, and a less optimal experience for users navigating the DeFi ecosystem.

In response to these challenges, there is a push for the creation of unified trading terminals. These terminals aim to streamline the trading process by aggregating liquidity from various chains, allowing users to access a broader range of assets and trading pairs without having to switch between different platforms. This consolidation could enhance the overall trading experience, making it easier for users to execute trades swiftly and efficiently.

The potential impact of these unified terminals is significant. Analysts suggest that by 2030, they could mediate a substantial portion of DeFi trading volume, indicating a shift in how traders interact with decentralized platforms. This move towards a more integrated trading environment could attract more users to DeFi, as it addresses some of the key pain points associated with liquidity fragmentation.
