## Overview

Bitcoin has shown signs of recovery after a lackluster week, gaining momentum on February 13th. This uptick is significant as it approaches the psychologically important $70,000 mark. Recent on-chain data suggests that the cryptocurrency might be on the verge of a short-term rally, potentially leading to a short squeeze.

## Bitcoin's Recent Performance

Following a challenging week, Bitcoin's price rebounded on February 13th, creating a sense of optimism among traders as it neared the $70,000 threshold. This movement is noteworthy because it reflects a shift in market sentiment, which could have implications for future price movements.

### Funding Rate Insights

A critical factor contributing to this bullish sentiment is the Bitcoin Funding Rate on Binance, the largest cryptocurrency exchange by trading volume. Market analyst CryptoOnchain highlighted that this funding rate has dropped to a critically low level, not seen in over a year. Specifically, the 14-day Simple Moving Average (SMA-14) of the Bitcoin Funding Rate has fallen to -0.002. 

The Funding Rate is a metric that indicates the periodic fee traders pay in a derivatives market for a specific cryptocurrency. A positive funding rate typically means that long traders are compensating short traders, while a negative funding rate indicates the opposite. In the current scenario, the negative funding rate suggests that short traders are paying long traders, which is a significant shift in market dynamics.

CryptoOnchain pointed out that this deeply negative funding rate, especially over a sustained period, indicates a growing number of bears betting against Bitcoin. Historically, such conditions are often associated with the bottom of severe downward trends. 

### Contrarian Signals

From an analytical standpoint, a deeply negative funding rate can serve as a strong contrarian signal. The market appears to be heavily skewed towards short positions, which could mean that a minor price rebound might trigger a wave of liquidations of these short positions. This phenomenon, known as a short squeeze, can lead to accelerated price increases as traders rush to close their positions.
