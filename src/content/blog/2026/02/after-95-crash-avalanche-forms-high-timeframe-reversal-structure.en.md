## Overview

Avalanche has experienced a significant price decline, falling over 95% from its peak in 2021. Recently, signs have emerged suggesting a potential reversal in its long-term trend, as the asset stabilizes at macro support levels and begins to form an Elliott Wave structure on the weekly chart.

## Current Situation

Avalanche is exhibiting early indications of a high-timeframe reversal. The asset is currently operating within a descending channel that has persisted since its all-time high in 2021. The broader structure indicates that the asset remains within a long-term corrective phase, yet recent technical signals hint at a possible inflection point in this broader cycle.

According to analysis, Avalanche appears to have completed Wave 1 of the Elliott Wave structure, with a macro low identified near a specific price point. The asset is now entering the early stages of a Wave 2 recovery phase. This phase is crucial, as it often determines whether a sustainable expansion can follow.

On the weekly chart, several developments are noteworthy. Wave 1 seems to have concluded within a designated macro bottoming zone, potentially establishing a base of support. Despite the ongoing long-term downtrend, the price action has shown a classic deviation setup, characterized by a bearish breakdown followed by a retest of the lower trendline.

Additionally, Avalanche has executed a liquidity sweep into a specified demand zone, indicating increased buying interest. The fractal structure also resembles the compression phase observed in the previous cycle before a significant expansion occurred. For further confirmation of the bullish outlook, sustained strength and price expansion toward mid-channel resistance are required. A decisive movement in this direction would reinforce the bullish Wave 2 narrative and indicate that a larger recovery structure is beginning to take shape.
