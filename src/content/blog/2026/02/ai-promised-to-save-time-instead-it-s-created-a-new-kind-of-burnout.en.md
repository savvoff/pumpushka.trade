## Overview

Recent research conducted by scholars from UC-Berkeley and Yale has revealed that artificial intelligence (AI) tools, rather than alleviating workloads, are contributing to an increase in work intensity and burnout among workers. This finding raises significant concerns about the implications of AI integration in the workplace, particularly regarding employee well-being and productivity.

## Research Findings

The study from UC-Berkeley and Yale highlights a critical issue in the modern workplace: the promise of AI to save time and enhance efficiency has not materialized as expected. Instead, researchers found that the implementation of AI tools has led to what they term "workload creep." This phenomenon refers to the gradual increase in work demands that employees experience, which can result in overwhelming stress and burnout.

The researchers observed that AI tools often create additional tasks rather than streamlining existing ones. For example, while AI can automate certain processes, it can also generate new responsibilities that employees must manage. This paradoxical effect means that instead of reducing the overall workload, AI can lead to a scenario where employees find themselves with an expanded range of tasks to complete, thereby intensifying their overall work experience.

The findings underscore the psychological toll that AI tools can impose on employees. Burnout, characterized by emotional exhaustion, cynicism, and reduced professional efficacy, has become an increasingly prevalent issue in various industries. The study emphasizes that the introduction of AI into the workplace should be approached with caution, as the tools that are intended to enhance productivity may inadvertently contribute to a decrease in employee satisfaction and mental health.

## From author

The implications of this research are profound. As organizations increasingly adopt AI technologies to improve efficiency, it is essential to consider the human element and the potential consequences on employee morale. The expectation that AI will simplify workflows needs to be balanced with an understanding of how these tools can inadvertently complicate work processes. Organizations must prioritize employee well-being and consider strategies to mitigate the risk of burnout as they integrate AI into their operations.

Furthermore, this research serves as a reminder that technology alone cannot solve workplace challenges. The human experience in the workplace must remain at the forefront of any technological adoption. Employers should focus on creating supportive environments that help employees manage their workloads effectively, regardless of the tools they use.
