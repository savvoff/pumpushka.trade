## Overview

Recent developments regarding Solana's decentralized application (dApp) revenues and the interest from both institutional and retail investors have raised concerns about the stability of the cryptocurrency. The current state of the market suggests a potential vulnerability for Solana's support level at $78.

## Declining dApp Revenues

Solana has experienced a noticeable decline in its dApp revenues. This downturn is significant as dApp revenues are often seen as a critical indicator of a blockchain's ecosystem health and user engagement. When dApp revenues drop, it may reflect a decrease in user activity and interest, which can have cascading effects on the overall perception of the network's viability.

## Lack of Investor Interest

In addition to the drop in dApp revenues, there is a marked lack of interest from both institutional and retail investors in Solana. This limited engagement can exacerbate the situation, as low investor interest typically leads to reduced market liquidity and can hinder price stability. When investors are hesitant or unwilling to participate, it creates an environment where price support levels, such as the current $78 for SOL, come under increasing pressure.

## Vulnerability of Support Levels

The combination of declining dApp revenues and limited investor interest contributes to the vulnerability of Solana's support level. A support level is a price point where buying interest is thought to be strong enough to prevent the price from falling further. As SOL hovers around this critical level, the lack of bullish sentiment raises questions about its ability to hold firm. If the support fails, it could lead to further declines, impacting investor confidence.
