## Overview

On Friday, cryptocurrency prices experienced a notable uptick, as Bitcoin approached the $68,000 mark. This price movement occurred amidst a backdrop of tariff-related developments, specifically following a ruling by the U.S. Supreme Court that deemed former President Trump's tariffs illegal. Despite the potential implications of this ruling, Bitcoin demonstrated resilience, while altcoins also contributed to a modest market rebound.

## What Happened

Cryptocurrency prices showed a positive trend on Friday, with Bitcoin nearing the $68,000 threshold. The increase in Bitcoin's value coincided with an important legal decision from the U.S. Supreme Court, which ruled that tariffs imposed by former President Trump were illegal. This ruling has significant implications for trade and economic policy in the United States, as it challenges the legitimacy of the tariffs that were established during his administration.

The Supreme Court's decision is noteworthy because it could potentially alter the landscape of U.S. trade relations and economic strategies. Tariffs, which are taxes imposed on imported goods, can influence market behavior and investor sentiment. In this case, the ruling may have created a sense of uncertainty in traditional markets, pushing investors to seek refuge in cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin.

Despite the turbulent backdrop of tariff discussions, Bitcoin's price movement suggests a degree of confidence among investors in the cryptocurrency space. The resilience of Bitcoin, especially in the face of legal and economic challenges, highlights its perceived value as a store of wealth and a hedge against traditional market volatility. Additionally, the positive performance of altcoins during this period indicates a broader recovery in the cryptocurrency market.
