Ethereum co-founder Vitalik Buterin has expressed significant concerns regarding the current trajectory of layer-2 (L2) networks within the Ethereum ecosystem. In a recent discussion, Buterin emphasized that some L2 solutions have compromised their decentralization principles, suggesting that these networks should not be labeled as mere extensions of Ethereum. His stance indicates a call for a reevaluation of how these networks are integrated and perceived within the broader Ethereum framework.

## Concerns About Decentralization

Buterin's remarks highlight a growing apprehension about the decentralization of certain layer-2 networks. He pointed out that the concessions made by some of these networks in the name of efficiency or scalability could undermine the core values that Ethereum was built upon. The fundamental ethos of Ethereum revolves around decentralization, and Buterin’s critique raises questions about the long-term implications of prioritizing speed and cost-effectiveness over the foundational principle of a decentralized network.

## The Importance of Identity in Layer-2 Solutions

In his discussion, Buterin made it clear that the identity of layer-2 solutions is crucial. He argued that if these networks deviate from the decentralized model, they should not be considered as part of the Ethereum ecosystem. This distinction is important not just for the integrity of Ethereum but also for users and developers who rely on the principles of decentralization when building and engaging with DApps (decentralized applications). By asserting that these networks should not be “branded” as extensions of Ethereum, Buterin is advocating for a more nuanced understanding of what it means to be part of the Ethereum network.

## From author

Vitalik Buterin's remarks serve as a critical reminder of the ongoing discussions surrounding decentralization in the blockchain space. His insights reflect a broader concern within the cryptocurrency community about the balance between innovation and the core principles that define decentralized networks. As layer-2 networks continue to evolve, the challenge will be to maintain a commitment to decentralization while also addressing the practical needs of scalability and performance. Buterin's call for a reevaluation of the L2-focused roadmap underscores the need for a collaborative effort to ensure that the Ethereum ecosystem remains true to its foundational values.
