## Overview

Bitcoin has recently experienced significant volatility, with its price retreating toward its 2026 low. This decline is attributed to several factors, including a lack of new capital inflows, weak investor sentiment, and increasing selling pressure in spot markets.

## Bitcoin's Price Decline

Bitcoin's price has faced downward pressure, falling back toward its 2026 low. This movement is indicative of the challenges the cryptocurrency is currently facing. Notably, the price struggled to maintain the $66,000 level, which is a crucial resistance point for many traders and investors. The inability to hold this level suggests a weakening bullish sentiment among market participants.

One of the primary contributors to this decline is the lack of fresh capital inflows into the Bitcoin market. Without new investments, the demand for Bitcoin diminishes, leading to increased selling pressure. Additionally, the overall sentiment among investors appears to be weak. This combination of factors has contributed to a decline in price, as traders may be more inclined to sell in a downtrend rather than buy.

Moreover, the rising selling activity in spot markets has compounded these issues. As more investors choose to sell their holdings, it creates a further imbalance between supply and demand. This selling pressure is especially pronounced when there is a lack of confidence in the market, which can lead to a rapid decline in prices.
