## Overview

Recent trading data indicates a significant shift in market sentiment, particularly concerning Bitcoin. The cryptocurrency has experienced a rebound, yet it has encountered resistance at a price level of $71,000. This development is notable as it coincides with a heightened sense of fear among investors, marking the most fearful sentiment observed since 2022.

## Market Sentiment and Trading Activity

The current trading environment reflects a broad risk-off unwind, as evidenced by the decline in spot volumes on major exchanges. Specifically, these volumes have decreased by approximately 30% since late 2025. This downturn suggests that traders are becoming increasingly cautious, opting to reduce their exposure to riskier assets like Bitcoin.

The fading participation from retail investors further underscores the shift in market dynamics. Retail trading is often a significant driver of price movements in the cryptocurrency market, and a decline in this segment can indicate a lack of confidence among individual investors. As fear permeates the market, traders may be more inclined to sell off their holdings rather than take on new positions.

The resistance at the $71,000 level for Bitcoin is particularly notable, as it represents a psychological barrier for traders. When prices reach such levels, it is common for investors to reassess their positions, leading to increased selling pressure if sentiment is negative. The combination of reduced trading volumes and heightened fear suggests that a sustained move above this resistance level may be challenging to achieve in the current environment.
