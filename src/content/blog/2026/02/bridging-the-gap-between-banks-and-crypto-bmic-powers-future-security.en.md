## Overview

The integration of traditional banking and cryptocurrency is undergoing a significant transformation, shifting its focus from mere regulation to long-term security infrastructure. The emergence of the $BMIC project is addressing critical vulnerabilities that threaten institutions holding digital assets over extended periods, particularly in light of advancements in quantum computing.

## The Current Landscape

Recent developments highlight a shift in the relationship between banks and cryptocurrencies. The concept of "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks presents a profound threat to institutions that plan to hold digital assets for long durations. These attacks involve bad actors scraping encrypted data, biding their time until they possess the computational power necessary to decrypt it. This looming threat underscores the necessity for robust security measures to protect assets in a rapidly evolving digital landscape.

$BMIC is leveraging post-quantum cryptography and a framework that ensures zero public-key exposure, thereby safeguarding assets against potential future computing threats. Early traction for the project indicates a market demand for preventative security solutions, as evidenced by over $433K raised during its presale phase. This is indicative of a growing recognition of the importance of security in the integration of traditional finance and the digital asset economy.

The traditional rivalry between banks and cryptocurrencies appears to be diminishing, with recent approvals for ETFs and a softened stance from regulatory bodies in Washington. Signals from Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent suggest that traditional banks are preparing to offer direct cryptocurrency products, signaling a crucial merging of the $130 trillion legacy finance world with the burgeoning digital asset sector. This shift fundamentally alters the industry's risk profile, moving the conversation from whether crypto is legal to whether it is secure for the long term.

As institutions migrate assets on-chain, the urgency for enhanced security measures becomes clear. The current encryption standards, which protect everything from SWIFT transfers to Ethereum wallets, are increasingly viewed as inadequate in the face of quantum computing advancements. This gap in security infrastructure is a pivotal friction point that $BMIC aims to address.
