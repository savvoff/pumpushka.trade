## Overview

Pavel Durov, the founder of Telegram, has voiced strong opposition to Spain's proposed online age verification laws. He argues that these laws are not genuinely aimed at protecting children but rather serve to establish a system of mass surveillance.

## Durov's Critique of Spain's Proposal

On Wednesday, Pavel Durov publicly criticized the proposed legislation in Spain concerning online age verification. He contends that the intent behind these laws is not to safeguard minors but to construct a surveillance framework that would monitor individuals online. Durov's assertions highlight a growing concern regarding privacy and the implications of government regulations in the digital space.

Durov's remarks come amid increasing scrutiny over how nations regulate online platforms and protect vulnerable populations. The debate around age verification is particularly contentious, as it raises questions about the balance between safeguarding children from harmful content and respecting individual privacy rights. Durov's perspective suggests that the legislation could lead to unintended consequences, where the mechanisms intended to protect children could infringe upon the privacy of all users.
