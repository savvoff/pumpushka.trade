## Overview

Bitcoin is currently experiencing a range-bound state as liquidity clears on both sides of the market. After a prolonged period of weakness, demand for Bitcoin has turned positive, suggesting that selling pressure may be easing and that structural accumulation could be returning to the market.

## Bitcoin Remains Range-Bound Amid Active Liquidity Clearing

Bitcoin's price action is characterized by indecision, with the asset locked in a range-bound state. This environment is marked by ongoing liquidity clearing, where the price fluctuates but lacks a clear direction. On one hand, upward price movements are met with selling pressure, while on the other, price dips are quickly absorbed by buyers. This creates a market dynamic resembling a tug-of-war, where neither buyers nor sellers can gain a definitive upper hand.

According to analysis, market liquidity is well-defined both above and below the current price levels. This structure contributes to the ongoing choppy market environment, as price movements oscillate between established pockets of orders. Traders are advised to exercise patience, as the market’s trajectory will largely depend on its reaction after nearby liquidity is purged. 

If Bitcoin manages to find acceptance above its current range following a liquidity sweep, it may lead to a bullish expansion into higher price pockets. Conversely, if this attempt fails, the market could face further downside, potentially leading to additional sweeps of lower liquidity levels. Until a major trend is established, the market's focus remains on a technical clean-up of liquidity.

## Bitcoin Demand Turns Positive After Months of Weakness

In a notable development, Bitcoin's demand has shifted back above zero after almost three months of persistent weakness. Currently, demand stands at a positive metric, which reflects a change in investor sentiment and activity in a market influenced by heightened volatility. Previously, in December, demand had dipped to a deeply negative level, which contributed to the sluggish price action observed in the following weeks. 

Since that low point, selling activity has begun to slow down, indicating that structural accumulation is re-emerging. This shift in demand suggests that long-term holders may be absorbing new supply, which is a critical factor in determining market health. When demand is negative, the market typically struggles; conversely, a positive demand metric indicates rebuilding buying activity, which can foster a healthier market structure.

However, it is crucial to note that a single positive demand print does not confirm a trend reversal. If this recovery in demand continues, it may serve as an early indicator that the market is transitioning from a distribution phase back towards accumulation, potentially setting the stage for sustained strength in the weeks to come.
