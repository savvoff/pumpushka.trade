## Overview

Recent discussions have highlighted the performance of static options strategies, particularly in the context of Bitcoin. These strategies, which include secured puts and covered calls, may appear attractive to clients but have shown a tendency to underperform over time. 

## Performance of Static Options Strategies

Static options strategies are investment methods that involve the use of options contracts without making adjustments based on market conditions. These strategies are often marketed to clients due to their simplicity and perceived reliability. However, the underlying reality is that while they can deliver satisfactory results during certain market conditions, they typically struggle to maintain that performance over extended periods.

One of the primary reasons for the disappointing long-term performance of these strategies is their inherent rigidity. By not allowing for adjustments in response to market fluctuations, investors may find themselves at a disadvantage. For example, a secured put strategy might be profitable in a stable or rising market, but when volatility increases or the market moves unfavorably, the strategy can quickly turn unprofitable.

Similarly, covered calls—where an investor holds a long position in an asset while selling call options on the same asset—can yield returns when the market is stable. However, in fluctuating or bearish markets, the performance can decline significantly. The inability to adapt to changing market dynamics is a key factor that contributes to the overall poor performance of these strategies over time.
