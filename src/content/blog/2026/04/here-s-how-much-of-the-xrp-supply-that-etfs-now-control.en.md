## Overview

Recent developments indicate a resurgence of institutional interest in XRP as inflows into Spot XRP ETFs in the United States are gaining momentum. Despite the token's price remaining below a certain threshold, the data reveals a significant portion of XRP's circulating supply is now being held by these investment vehicles.

## ETF Accumulation of XRP Supply

In the past months, the landscape for Spot XRP ETFs has seen notable fluctuations. March was particularly challenging, with data from SoSoValue revealing a substantial net outflow of $31.16 million for that month. This decline resulted in total assets under management (AUM) for XRP dropping from a peak of $1.65 billion in January to below $1 billion. This downturn was attributed to a combination of a significant drop in XRP's price, exceeding 40%, and investor redemptions.

However, the situation has shifted as Spot XRP ETFs have begun to experience net inflows once again. On April 10, these ETFs attracted $9.1 million in net inflows, marking the strongest single-day intake since February 6. This resurgence suggests that new capital is entering the XRP ecosystem through institutional investors after a period of stagnation. Cumulatively, Spot XRP ETFs have received $1.22 billion in net inflows since their inception, indicating that the accumulation of XRP in these investment vehicles is becoming increasingly significant.

As of April 14, there were seven Spot XRP ETFs trading in the United States, collectively holding 771.7 million XRP tokens and an AUM of approximately $959.40 million. This accumulation now represents around 1.16% of XRP’s overall market capitalization, highlighting the growing influence of these ETFs in the market.

## Importance of ETF Accumulation for XRP’s Market Structure

The inflows into Spot XRP ETFs are emerging as a critical factor impacting XRP's market structure. When inflows increase, these funds are required to acquire XRP from the market, establishing a consistent source of demand. Additionally, XRP tokens that are allocated to ETFs are typically held for extended periods, contrasting with the more fluid trading behavior of retail investors. This creates a supply sink that could significantly influence price dynamics, particularly if inflows continue to rise.

For context, the amount of XRP held on exchanges has decreased by 45%, from 3.95 billion to 2.6 billion, reaching its lowest level since 2018. This reduction has left the order book relatively thin, making it sensitive to any uptick in demand.

A survey conducted by Coinbase and EY-Parthenon involving 351 institutional investors revealed that 25% plan to incorporate XRP into their portfolios by 2026, while 18% already hold it. However, 65% of these respondents cited regulatory clarity as the primary barrier to increasing their exposure to cryptocurrencies. The advancement of the CLARITY Act is currently viewed as a pivotal regulatory element for the future of XRP.

If the legislation successfully passes through the Senate Banking Committee, there is potential for Spot XRP ETFs to grow to about $5 billion in AUM. Such growth would effectively lock up approximately 2.5 billion XRP tokens, exceeding the total amount held by all crypto exchanges combined at present.
