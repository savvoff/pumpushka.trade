## Overview

Crypto market analyst Marmot has raised concerns regarding Bitcoin's recent price surge above $70,000. He characterizes this rally as a negative indicator, warning investors not to misinterpret the rebound as a sign of a genuine recovery in the market.

## The Current Situation

Marmot has labeled Bitcoin’s price rebound above $74,000 as a potential trap for investors. In a post on X, he expressed that the current circumstances surrounding Bitcoin are precarious, suggesting that the market may be poised for a deeper pullback to new lows once the upward trend reverses. He emphasizes that the recent surge above $72,000 was orchestrated, describing it as a carefully designed whale trap aimed at attracting retail buyers before a broader sell-off occurs.

Marmot cautions against viewing this relief rally as the onset of a new bull market. He notes that similar price movements in the past have often led traders into poorly timed entry points, resulting in significant losses. The analyst points out that historically, 90% of Bitcoin traders have faced liquidation during November 2026, a period associated with previous bear market bottoms.

During bear markets, Marmot highlights the occurrence of bull traps, where sudden price increases create the false impression that the downtrend has concluded. This phenomenon tends to generate optimism and trigger fear of missing out (FOMO) among investors, leading many to buy into the rebound. However, once the buying frenzy subsides, Bitcoin's price often reverses sharply, returning to levels seen prior to the rally and causing substantial liquidations.

Marmot further explains that underlying the recent price strength is a decline in global liquidity, as institutions appear to be quietly exiting the market to mitigate downside risk. He argues that weaker demand, compounded by ongoing geopolitical tensions, continues to exert pressure on market sentiment, indicating that Bitcoin's bear market bottom remains distant.

## From author

Marmot's analysis provides a sobering perspective on the current state of Bitcoin, especially in light of the recent price surge. His observations about market behavior during bear cycles serve as a reminder of the volatility inherent in cryptocurrency trading. While many may be tempted to celebrate short-term gains, the warning signs of a potential downturn cannot be ignored. As the market continues to evolve, both seasoned and novice investors should remain vigilant and critically assess the broader market dynamics at play.
