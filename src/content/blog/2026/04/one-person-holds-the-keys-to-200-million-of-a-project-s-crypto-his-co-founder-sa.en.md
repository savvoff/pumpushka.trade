## Overview

In a significant development within a prominent cryptocurrency project, co-founders Da Hongfei and Erik Zhang are at odds over the future governance and management structure of their venture, which has approximately $200 million in crypto assets at stake. Their differing visions could have profound implications for the project's governance and operational integrity.

## Conflicting Governance Plans

The disagreement between Da Hongfei and Erik Zhang centers on the restructuring of the project's governance model. Da Hongfei advocates for a governance system that emphasizes independence and the implementation of multi-signature (multi-sig) protection. This approach aims to decentralize control and enhance security by requiring multiple signatures for transactions, thus reducing the risk of unilateral decision-making and potential mismanagement of funds.

On the other hand, Erik Zhang proposes a different strategy. He calls for accountability investigations into the project's management and insists on remaining on the board. This perspective suggests a desire for oversight and possibly a more traditional governance framework, where the existing leadership maintains a level of control and responsibility for decision-making processes.

The stark contrast between these two proposals not only highlights the internal divisions within the project but also raises questions about the future direction of the venture. Da's push for independent governance could attract stakeholders who are concerned about centralization and seek greater transparency in how their assets are managed. Conversely, Zhang's focus on accountability may resonate with those who prioritize stability and oversight in the wake of potential controversies.
