## Overview

The Rave Token (RAVE) experienced a dramatic collapse, losing nearly $6 billion in market value within 48 hours. This sudden downturn, primarily attributed to allegations of market manipulation, has raised significant concerns among investors and analysts alike.

## The Collapse of Rave Token

RAVE, which had seen an astronomical rise from approximately $0.25 to $27.30 in just nine days, plummeted to nearly $1 in a mere 24-hour timeframe. This 95% drop caught the attention of many in the crypto community, particularly due to the stark contrast between the market value loss and the $52 million in liquidations that could be directly accounted for. Analysts were puzzled by this discrepancy, signaling potential underlying issues within the market dynamics.

The immediate trigger for the collapse was a public accusation made by on-chain investigator ZachXBT. He claimed that RAVE was involved in a coordinated pump-and-dump scheme and offered a $25,000 bounty for credible information from whistleblowers. His findings indicated that nine wallets associated with the project’s early distribution phase controlled nearly 95% of the total token supply. This concentrated ownership allowed a small number of insiders to manipulate prices at will, raising alarms about the integrity of the trading environment.

In response to the allegations, major exchanges such as Binance and Bitget announced investigations into the trading activities surrounding RAVE. However, rather than providing reassurance, these announcements intensified the sell-off as investors interpreted the probes as confirmation of wrongdoing. This led to a rapid acceleration in selling activity.

Adding to the chaos, large transfers to exchanges were noted before the price rally, which is a pattern often linked to coordinated sell-offs once retail interest peaks. The trading frenzy had previously forced short sellers out of their positions, resulting in over $40 million in liquidations. The absence of genuine buyer demand meant that once the pressure from short squeezes subsided, the market rapidly declined.

In the aftermath, RaveDAO released a statement asserting that the team was not responsible for the recent price fluctuations. They indicated that the token unlocks were conducted to cover operational costs, a practice they deemed standard. However, the statement did not address the specific claims made by ZachXBT regarding wallet control.
