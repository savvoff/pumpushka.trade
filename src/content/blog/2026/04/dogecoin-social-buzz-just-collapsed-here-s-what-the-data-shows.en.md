## Overview

Dogecoin's social momentum has experienced a significant decline, indicating a shift in the dynamics of its market activity. The latest data suggests that the current phase of Dogecoin is influenced more by derivatives positioning rather than a genuine recovery in network demand.

## Dogecoin's Social and On-Chain Activity

Joao Wedson, the founder and CEO of Alphractal, reported a drastic decrease in social media interactions surrounding Dogecoin. He noted that the current interest in Dogecoin is limited compared to other altcoins, with heightened engagement typically observed during bull markets. This decline in social buzz correlates with a broader reduction in on-chain activity.

According to Alphractal's data, Dogecoin's daily active addresses have dropped to 37,197, reflecting a decline of 38.35% in just one day and 44.88% over the past week. Similarly, daily transactions have fallen sharply to 26,189, which is a decrease of 64.30% day-over-day and 51.27% week-over-week. The adjusted on-chain transfer volume also registered a significant drop, now at $118.12 million, down 41.94% in a single day and 41.25% over the week.

These figures reveal a concerning trend of diminished participation within the Dogecoin network, challenging the notion of a demand-driven recovery. Alphractal's AI analysis frames the current market behavior as being more influenced by sentiment and positioning rather than actual usage.

## Derivatives Positioning and Market Sentiment

On a different note, Alphractal AI has indicated that Dogecoin's derivatives market is exhibiting a "risk-on bullish regime." Open interest in derivatives has expanded to $1.099 billion, with the long/short ratio increasing to 2.6433, suggesting a strong appetite for leveraged upside. However, this scenario is not without its risks. The elevated long/short ratio points to a potential imbalance, indicating that many traders are positioned on the long side, which could create vulnerabilities if market sentiment shifts.

Despite the bullish sentiment in derivatives, valuation metrics for Dogecoin appear subdued. The asset is trading approximately at $0.096, compared to a realized price of $0.1383, resulting in a Market Value to Realized Value (MVRV) ratio of 0.686. The Net Unrealized Profit/Loss stands at -0.459, placing it within a capitulation zone. This indicates that the average holder is currently at a loss, and the network is in a loss-heavy environment typically associated with late-stage market drawdowns or early recovery phases.

## Short-Term Market Indicators

In the short term, momentum indicators show signs of stabilization but not a breakout. Alphractal's AI reports that the Relative Strength Index (RSI) is nearing neutral territory, while the Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) has turned bullish, suggesting a reduction in downside pressure. However, Dogecoin continues to trade below its long-term averages and remains under the 200-day baseline, which keeps the broader market structure constrained.

Supply dynamics also present cautionary signs. The circulating supply of Dogecoin is 153.95 billion, with exchange reserves increasing to 27.19 billion DOGE, valued at approximately $2.66 billion. The rise in exchange reserves often signals that coins are moving to exchanges for potential selling, rather than indicating a tightening supply.

## From author

The current state of Dogecoin presents a complex picture, balancing between bearish indicators of social and on-chain activity and bullish sentiment in the derivatives market. The lack of strong engagement and participation raises questions about the sustainability of any upward price movements. Observing how these dynamics evolve will be critical for investors and market participants.
