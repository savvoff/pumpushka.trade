## Overview

The ongoing situation related to the Iran war is anticipated to have significant implications for global markets throughout 2026, according to insights from analyst Nic Puckrin. This development casts uncertainty on monetary policy, particularly in relation to interest rate cuts, which are now not expected until at least the third quarter of the year.

## Market Implications of the Iran War

Analyst Nic Puckrin has indicated that the consequences of the Iran war will likely exert considerable pressure on markets for a substantial part of 2026. This situation is critical as it suggests a prolonged period of instability, which could hinder economic recovery and growth.

The expected fallout from the conflict relates not only to geopolitical tensions but also to their broader economic ramifications. Markets often react adversely to prolonged conflicts, and the Iran war is no exception. The uncertainty surrounding the war can lead to volatility in various sectors, affecting investor sentiment and market confidence.

In light of these developments, hopes for interest rate cuts have been dashed, with Puckrin stating that such cuts are now unlikely to occur until the third quarter of 2026 at the earliest. This delay in monetary easing could have significant implications for borrowing costs, consumer spending, and overall economic activity. 

The expectation of a prolonged period without rate cuts may lead to a more cautious approach from investors, as they navigate the complexities of a turbulent market environment. The lack of rate cuts could also hinder the recovery of cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin, which often reacts to changes in monetary policy and investor sentiment.
