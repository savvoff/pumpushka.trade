## Overview

World Liberty Financial's WLFI token experienced a significant decline on Friday, falling approximately 13% to reach new all-time lows. This drop has raised concerns regarding the company's leverage and collateral management, leading to fears of potential liquidations.

## WLFI's Recent Decline

The selloff of the WLFI token has been attributed to mounting apprehensions surrounding World Liberty Financial's financial practices. Reports circulating on social media platform X have indicated that the company allegedly posted a substantial amount of WLFI tokens as collateral, estimated at 5 billion tokens, while borrowing around $75 million in stablecoins from the decentralized lender Dolomite. It has also been reported that more than $40 million of this borrowed amount was subsequently transferred to Coinbase Prime.

Concerns about the liquidity of the company's USD1 stablecoin pools have emerged as well, with users reportedly facing withdrawal pressures. Critics have pointed out that WLFI's dominance—claiming more than 50% of Dolomite's liquidity—could exacerbate market stress during rapid price fluctuations. A declining WLFI price could diminish collateral safety, potentially increasing the likelihood of forced liquidations, despite initial strategies aimed at preventing such a scenario.

## Response from World Liberty Financial

In response to the backlash and growing fears of liquidation, World Liberty Financial issued a statement asserting its position in the market. The company confirmed its role as a significant supplier and borrower on WLFI Markets, acknowledging the use of WLFI as collateral and the borrowing of stablecoins. However, it emphasized that it was "nowhere near liquidation" and outlined plans to supply additional collateral if market conditions turned against its position.

World Liberty Financial described its borrowing activities as part of a broader strategy to serve as an anchor borrower, which would help generate yield and enhance the attractiveness of WLFI Markets for other participants in the ecosystem. The company also announced its intention to involve early token holders in governance decisions, stating that a governance proposal to unlock locked tokens would be shared for community feedback in the coming week, followed by a formal vote.

Additionally, World Liberty Financial highlighted the compliance-grade controls within its USD1 stablecoin, aimed at ensuring fund safety in light of future regulatory environments. The company assured stakeholders that any upgrades to the system would be seamless, maintaining balances, approvals, and integrations without disruption.
