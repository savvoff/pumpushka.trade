## Proposed Changes to Preferred Stock

The proposed changes are designed to address several key factors affecting the performance of the preferred stock. According to the Executive Chairman, the primary goals include stabilizing the price of the stock, reducing cyclicality, increasing liquidity, and fostering demand among investors. 

By introducing semi-monthly dividends, the strategy aims to provide more consistent returns to shareholders, which can lead to greater investor confidence. This approach could potentially mitigate the impacts of market fluctuations, thus creating a more stable trading environment for the preferred stock. 

The emphasis on liquidity is particularly noteworthy. By making the stock more attractive through regular dividend payments, the proposal seeks to encourage more trading activity. Enhanced liquidity can lead to tighter bid-ask spreads and a more efficient market overall, which benefits both buyers and sellers. 

Furthermore, the intention to grow demand indicates a proactive approach by the management to ensure the long-term viability of the preferred stock. By aligning the interests of shareholders with regular income through dividends, the company may be able to attract a wider range of investors, including those who prioritize income generation in their investment strategies.
