## Overview

James Wynn, a trader known for using high leverage, has experienced a significant series of liquidations, marking his sixth forced position closure in just two weeks. This pattern highlights the risks associated with extreme leverage in a volatile market.

## Liquidation Details

James Wynn, often recognized for his ability to achieve both remarkable gains and staggering losses through high-leverage trading, faced another liquidation as Bitcoin's price surged. Lookonchain reported that Wynn's latest forced closure occurred at around $68,000. This liquidation adds to a troubling trend, as it marks the sixth time he has been liquidated in a mere two-week span.

According to on-chain data shared by Lookonchain and Hypurrscan, Wynn’s recent failures are compounded by a history of at least 194 previous liquidations. Each of his attempts to capitalize on Bitcoin's upward movement has resulted in a full liquidation rather than a controlled exit. This pattern suggests a consistent inability to manage risk in a rapidly changing market environment.

## A History of High-Stakes Trading

Wynn's trading career has been marked by extreme highs and lows. At the peak of his success in 2025, he reportedly held over $80 million in profit, driven by a series of high-risk bets on Bitcoin and popular memecoins. His involvement with $PEPE is particularly notable, as it reached a valuation in the billions. However, a critical turning point in his trading strategy occurred when he executed a high-leverage Bitcoin long, which ultimately led to significant losses.

In late May and early June of 2025, Wynn's trading approach resulted in at least nine liquidations on a single wallet, with cumulative losses nearing $22 million. By the end of 2025, his frequent liquidations had become a subject of analysis, illustrating the dangers of hyper-leverage in trading.

Since mid-March 2026, Wynn has continued to engage in high-leverage Bitcoin shorts, often utilizing leverage as high as 40x. His positions have been characterized by notional sizes ranging from $44,000 to $190,000. Despite the modest price fluctuations in Bitcoin, these high levels of leverage have led to multiple wipeouts for Wynn, emphasizing the risks inherent in such trading strategies.
