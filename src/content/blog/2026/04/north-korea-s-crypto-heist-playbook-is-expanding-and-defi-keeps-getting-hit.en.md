## Overview

Recent exploits in the decentralized finance (DeFi) sector have revealed an alarming trend, with over $500 million reportedly stolen across two significant incidents involving Drift and Kelp. These breaches, which initially appeared to be isolated events, are now indicative of a larger, sustained campaign likely motivated by the financial needs of a sanctioned state.

## What Happened

In a span of just over two weeks, the crypto space witnessed a staggering loss of funds due to the exploits targeting Drift and Kelp. These incidents have raised serious concerns about the security vulnerabilities within the DeFi ecosystem. The significant amount siphoned off suggests that these attacks were not merely opportunistic but rather part of a coordinated effort to exploit weaknesses in decentralized platforms.

The implications of these events extend beyond the immediate financial losses. They highlight the ongoing risks associated with DeFi protocols, which have been increasingly targeted by malicious actors. The sophistication and scale of these exploits suggest that they may be part of a broader strategy employed by entities seeking to finance activities in the face of international sanctions. This situation underscores the pressing need for enhanced security measures and regulatory frameworks within the crypto industry to protect against such threats.
