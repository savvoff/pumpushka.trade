## What Happened

In a recent statement, Justin Sun expressed his discontent with the WLFI platform, highlighting two major issues that he believes compromise the integrity of the platform. First, he pointed out the extended periods during which tokens are locked, a practice that can potentially limit liquidity and negatively impact investors' ability to access their funds. This concern is significant in the cryptocurrency market, where liquidity is often a key factor for traders and investors looking to capitalize on price movements.

Secondly, Sun accused WLFI of incorporating blacklist functions directly into its smart contracts. Such functions can restrict access to tokens, effectively barring certain users from participating in transactions or accessing their assets. The implications of this feature raise questions about fairness and transparency within the platform, as it suggests a level of control that could be exercised over users' funds without their consent.

In response to Sun's criticisms, WLFI threatened to pursue legal action against him.
