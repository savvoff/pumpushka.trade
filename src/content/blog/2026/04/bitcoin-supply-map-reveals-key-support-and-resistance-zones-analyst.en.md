## Overview

Recent insights from market analyst Darkfost reveal significant implications for Bitcoin's price trajectory based on its supply distribution. This analysis highlights critical support and resistance zones that could shape market behavior in the near term, particularly following a positive price trend observed in early April.

## Bitcoin’s Supply Distribution Insights

In a recent post, Darkfost shared findings regarding Bitcoin’s price structure tied to its supply distribution. The analysis indicates that approximately 61% of Bitcoin's circulating supply was acquired at prices below the current market level. This leaves 39% of the supply purchased at higher prices. Such a distribution suggests that a majority of market participants are currently in profit, which is often associated with a healthier market structure. 

Darkfost’s analysis further identifies a significant concentration of investor activity within the $65,000 to $70,000 range. Although this range reflects both buying and selling activities, it is not purely an accumulation zone. Nevertheless, it represents a crucial area where a considerable volume of Bitcoin changed hands. As this range lies below the current price, it is interpreted as a potential support floor, making it an attractive accumulation zone for savvy investors.

On the upper end, Darkfost points out a cluster of activity between $90,000 and $95,000, which could serve as a strong resistance level. Investors who acquired Bitcoin in this price range may be inclined to exit their positions when prices return to their initial purchase levels. This behavior could create a barrier to further upward price movement.

However, not all clusters of activity are viewed with the same significance. Darkfost notes that the $85,000 region lacks a sequential relationship from a technical perspective, despite exhibiting high transaction volumes. This is attributed to the impact of exchange-related transfers, particularly a notable transfer of nearly 800,000 Bitcoin from Coinbase, which skews the data and does not accurately reflect genuine investor sentiment.

## Air Pocket Above $75,000

Another critical insight from the supply map is the identification of a low-activity zone, often referred to as an “air pocket,” situated above $75,000. Historically, Bitcoin has experienced limited trading activity in this range, suggesting fewer obstacles to price movement. If Bitcoin were to enter this zone with sufficient momentum, it could either pass through it rapidly or enter a brief consolidation phase before continuing its ascent.

At the time of analysis, Bitcoin was trading at $71,535, reflecting a 6.45% increase over the previous week.
