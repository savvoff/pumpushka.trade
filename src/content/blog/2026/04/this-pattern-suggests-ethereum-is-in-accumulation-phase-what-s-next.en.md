## Overview

The price of Ethereum experienced a slowdown over the weekend following a breakdown in negotiations between the United States and Iran. Despite this uncertainty, on-chain data indicates that the market structure for Ethereum remains largely intact, suggesting potential for a bullish shift in the near future.

## Current Market Dynamics

Ethereum's price has seen a modest decline, attributed to geopolitical tensions. However, a prominent analyst has pointed out that significant movements in Ether tokens, particularly outflows from Binance, are indicative of an ongoing accumulation phase. The analysis is based on the 365-day Simple Moving Average of Ethereum Exchange Netflow on Binance, which has recently hit its lowest level since May 2024.

This decline in netflow is historically considered a bullish sign, as it suggests that investors are moving their assets off exchanges and into self-custody. This behavior often indicates a long-term holding strategy, which reduces immediate sell pressure in the market. The analyst highlighted that similar patterns in the past have led to upward reversals and significant bull rallies for Ethereum.

The current trend, as outlined by CryptoOnchain, points to a potential turning point for Ethereum. If historical patterns hold true, this accumulation phase could set the stage for the next major upward trend in Ethereum's price. Market participants are advised to monitor this netflow metric closely for signs of a decisive upward pivot, which would confirm the onset of a new bullish cycle.
