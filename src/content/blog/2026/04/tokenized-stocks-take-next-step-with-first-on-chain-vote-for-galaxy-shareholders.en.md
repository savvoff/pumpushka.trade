## Overview

Holders of tokenized shares in Galaxy (GLXY) are set to engage in a significant advancement in shareholder participation. They will soon be able to utilize on-chain proxy voting through Broadridge, marking a pivotal moment for tokenized stocks.

## What Happened

The announcement of on-chain voting capabilities for Galaxy shareholders signifies a progressive step in the integration of blockchain technology within traditional finance. This move allows holders of tokenized shares to participate directly in corporate governance through proxy voting. The implementation of this system is facilitated by Broadridge, a prominent provider of investor communications and technology solutions. 

This development is particularly noteworthy as it represents a convergence of digital assets and established investment practices. Tokenized stocks have been gaining traction in the financial markets, and the ability to vote on corporate matters aligns with the growing demand for greater shareholder engagement and transparency. 

By enabling on-chain voting, Galaxy is positioning itself at the forefront of innovation in the realm of tokenized assets. This capability not only enhances the voting process for shareholders, making it more accessible and efficient, but it also underscores the potential for blockchain technology to disrupt traditional voting mechanisms.

The involvement of Broadridge is significant, given the company's established presence in the financial technology sector. Their expertise in proxy voting and investor communications is expected to provide a robust framework for Galaxy's tokenized shareholders, ensuring that the voting process is secure, transparent, and compliant with regulatory standards.
