## Overview

A recent observation by crypto expert Chad has highlighted a significant connection between Ripple, XRP, and SWIFT. This development comes as Ripple enhances its payment services and integrates its digital assets into traditional finance, marking a pivotal moment for the firm and its offerings.

## Ripple's Connection to SWIFT

Chad's insights reveal that Ripple Treasury and XRP are now directly linked to SWIFT, a major player in global financial transactions. He pointed out that Ripple has officially listed SWIFT as one of its connectivity partners for payment solutions. This connection is part of Ripple's involvement in the SWIFT Certified Partner Program, which enables global bank connectivity and provides hosting options for SWIFT's Alliance Lite2 platform.

Furthermore, Ripple Treasury has established a partnership to provide SWIFTRef data, facilitating IBAN and ABA lookups directly within its operational workflow. This partnership enhances the functionality and accessibility of Ripple's services, demonstrating a commitment to integrating traditional banking systems with cryptocurrency solutions.

Additionally, Ripple Treasury has collaborated with Fides, a firm that closely aligns with SWIFT's operations. This partnership allows Ripple Treasury to extend multi-bank connectivity to its customers worldwide, thereby increasing the reach and utility of Ripple's payment solutions.

Chad emphasized that Ripple and XRP are effectively becoming integrated into the broader financial ecosystem. This integration occurs through Ripple Treasury’s ClearConnect connectivity layer, which provides banks with the ability to connect globally. Notably, for banks that are not yet connected to Ripple, the installation of the necessary API can be completed in just seven days.

Currently, Ripple Treasury is connected to several major enterprise resource planning (ERP) systems, including NetSuite, Oracle, SAP, Infor, Workday, and MS Dynamics. This connectivity allows customers holding crypto assets across multiple platforms to manage their entire portfolio through a single treasury management system, eliminating the need for multiple separate systems.

## Acquiring GTreasury: A Strategic Move

Chad also highlighted Ripple's acquisition of GTreasury as a significant milestone. He described it as Ripple's "single biggest move" to date, as it enables the launch of the first management system with native on-chain capabilities. This development allows Ripple to integrate XRP and RLUSD within the Ripple Treasury, facilitating the use of these digital assets alongside traditional fiat currencies.

Chad noted that Ripple's operations do not rely on the CLARITY Act for functionality, as the firm continues to weave XRP into mainstream financial practices. Furthermore, Ripple is on the verge of becoming a national trust bank, which could enhance its access to the U.S. banking system.

Additionally, Ripple has applied for a Fed Master account, a move that would enable the firm to utilize the Federal Reserve’s payment rails for its stablecoin operations. This step is crucial for Ripple as it seeks to solidify its role in the financial landscape.
