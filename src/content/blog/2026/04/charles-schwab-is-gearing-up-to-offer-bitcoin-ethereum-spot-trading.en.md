## Overview

Financial giant Charles Schwab has announced plans to introduce spot trading for Bitcoin and Ethereum, with the launch expected by the end of the current quarter. This development marks a significant step for the firm, as it expands its offerings in the cryptocurrency space.

## What Happened

On Friday, Charles Schwab confirmed its intention to begin spot buying of Bitcoin and Ethereum. This move indicates the firm's commitment to engaging with the growing demand for cryptocurrency trading among its client base. By venturing into the spot trading market for these prominent digital assets, Charles Schwab aims to cater to investors looking to diversify their portfolios with cryptocurrencies.

Spot trading allows investors to buy and sell assets for immediate delivery, which contrasts with futures trading, where contracts are agreed upon for future delivery. The decision to offer spot trading for Bitcoin and Ethereum reflects a broader trend among traditional financial institutions recognizing the importance of cryptocurrencies in the modern financial landscape.

This initiative is particularly relevant considering the increasing interest from both retail and institutional investors in cryptocurrencies. By providing access to spot trading, Charles Schwab is positioning itself as a more competitive player in the financial services market, appealing to clients who wish to invest in digital currencies alongside traditional assets.
