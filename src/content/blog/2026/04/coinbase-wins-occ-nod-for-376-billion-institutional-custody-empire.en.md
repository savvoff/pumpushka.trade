## Overview

Coinbase has received conditional approval from the Office of the Comptroller of the Currency (OCC) to operate under a federal national trust charter. This significant development is poised to enhance Coinbase's institutional crypto custody services, which currently manage a substantial amount of assets.

## Conditional OCC Approval

The approval granted to Coinbase by the OCC marks a pivotal moment in the evolution of institutional cryptocurrency custody. With this new federal national trust charter, Coinbase is set to streamline its operations and offer a robust framework for the management of digital assets. The charter enables Coinbase to operate with a uniform federal oversight, which is crucial for building trust among institutional investors.

Coinbase already oversees a substantial amount of assets, with reported assets under management (AUM) totaling $376 billion. This figure underscores the company's significant presence in the cryptocurrency market, particularly in the realm of institutional custody. Furthermore, Coinbase holds more than 80% of the assets related to Bitcoin and Ethereum exchange-traded funds (ETFs) in the United States. This dominant position not only highlights the company's influence but also signals growing institutional interest in cryptocurrencies.

The approval from the OCC is especially important as it provides a regulatory framework that can facilitate the safe and secure management of digital assets. This move is expected to enhance confidence among institutional investors, who have historically been cautious about entering the crypto space due to regulatory uncertainties. By obtaining this charter, Coinbase positions itself as a leader in the institutional custody market, potentially attracting more clients looking for reliable custodial solutions.
