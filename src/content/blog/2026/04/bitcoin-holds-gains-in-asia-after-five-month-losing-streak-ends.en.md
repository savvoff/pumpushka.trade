## Overview

Bitcoin is maintaining its recent gains in Asia following the conclusion of a five-month losing streak that ended in March. This positive momentum has also been seen in other risk assets, largely driven by geopolitical developments, specifically US President Donald Trump's announcement regarding the intention to end the war on Iran within weeks.

## What Happened

In early Wednesday trading in Asia, Bitcoin demonstrated resilience by holding onto its gains after a notable recovery in March. This recovery marked the end of a prolonged five-month period during which the cryptocurrency faced consistent declines. The turnaround in Bitcoin's performance coincided with broader market trends, as other risk assets also experienced upward movement.

The catalyst for this shift appears to be linked to US President Donald Trump's announcement that he plans to conclude the ongoing conflict with Iran in the near future. Such geopolitical developments often have significant implications for market sentiment, particularly in the realm of risk assets, which include cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin. The prospect of reduced geopolitical tension can lead to increased investor confidence, prompting a shift towards assets perceived as higher risk.

This change in sentiment is noteworthy as it highlights the interconnectedness of global events and market dynamics. Investors often react to news that suggests stability or peace, as these conditions can foster a more favorable environment for investment. The announcement from President Trump may have provided a sense of optimism that contributed to the recent gains in Bitcoin and other risk assets.
