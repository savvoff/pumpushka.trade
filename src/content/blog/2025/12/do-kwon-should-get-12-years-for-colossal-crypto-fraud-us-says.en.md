## Overview

US prosecutors have recommended a 12-year prison sentence for Do Kwon, co-founder of Terraform Labs Pte., in connection with the fraud that resulted in the significant collapse of the TerraUSD stablecoin in 2022. This incident led to a substantial loss, amounting to $40 billion.

## What Happened

The recommendation for a lengthy prison sentence comes as part of the legal proceedings against Kwon, who has been accused of orchestrating a fraudulent scheme linked to the TerraUSD stablecoin. The collapse of this stablecoin was a pivotal event in the cryptocurrency market, leading to widespread scrutiny and concern over the stability and regulatory oversight of digital currencies. 

The prosecutors’ assertion highlights the severity of the alleged fraud and its consequences, particularly the financial impact on investors and the broader crypto ecosystem. The case against Kwon underscores the ongoing challenges faced by regulators and law enforcement in addressing fraudulent activities within the rapidly evolving cryptocurrency space.
