## Overview

Bitcoin experienced a decline during the Asian trading hours, falling below the $90,000 mark. This movement occurred despite a rally in other risk assets, which gained traction following the US Federal Reserve's decision to cut interest rates and its optimistic outlook on the economy.

## What Happened

In recent trading sessions, Bitcoin saw a notable drop, slipping below the significant threshold of $90,000. This decline took place during the Asian trading hours, a period when many traders and investors globally assess market movements. Interestingly, while Bitcoin faced downward pressure, other risk assets were on the rise. 

The US Federal Reserve's recent decision to cut interest rates has been a pivotal factor influencing market dynamics. The Fed's action is often viewed as a stimulus measure aimed at supporting economic growth. Accompanying this rate cut, the Federal Reserve expressed an optimistic view regarding the economy, suggesting a potentially favorable environment for growth and investment. 

Despite the positive sentiment in the broader market, Bitcoin's decline raises questions about its correlation with traditional financial instruments and risk assets. Typically, a decrease in interest rates can lead to increased investment in riskier assets, as lower borrowing costs can stimulate economic activity. However, Bitcoin's reaction appears contrary to what some might expect, indicating that it operates under its own set of dynamics.
