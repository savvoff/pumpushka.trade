## Overview

Ledger has reported a significant vulnerability in the chip commonly used in Solana phones. The company claims to have successfully gained "full and absolute control" over a smartphone by utilizing electromagnetic pulses to exploit this weakness.

## What Happened

Ledger's findings reveal that the chip found in Solana phones is susceptible to electromagnetic pulse attacks. By leveraging this vulnerability, Ledger demonstrated the ability to take over a smartphone completely. This incident raises concerns about the security of devices that utilize this specific chip, particularly regarding the potential for unauthorized access and control.

## Why It Matters

The implications of this vulnerability are significant for users and developers within the Solana ecosystem. The ability to manipulate smartphones through such a method highlights potential risks associated with hardware security in mobile devices. As reliance on smartphones for cryptocurrency transactions and interactions grows, ensuring the integrity and security of the underlying hardware becomes increasingly critical.

## Impact on the crypto market

- Heightened concerns about smartphone security in the crypto space.
- Potential implications for user trust in devices associated with cryptocurrency transactions.
- Increased scrutiny on manufacturers of hardware components used in crypto-related devices.
- Possible calls for enhanced security measures and protocols in the development of mobile technology for cryptocurrencies.
- Attention from developers and users to alternative solutions or devices that prioritize security.
