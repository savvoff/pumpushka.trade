## Overview

The Marshall Islands has introduced a groundbreaking initiative by launching the world’s first blockchain-based Universal Basic Income (UBI) on the Stellar blockchain. This innovative program, supported by U.S. Treasuries, aims to provide a new model for digital public finance and address the economic challenges faced by underserved regions.

## What Happened

The implementation of the blockchain-based UBI in the Marshall Islands represents a significant step towards modernizing public finance. By utilizing the Stellar blockchain, the initiative enables the efficient distribution of funds directly to individuals, ensuring transparency and accessibility. This move is particularly relevant for regions that face economic difficulties and limited access to traditional banking systems.

The UBI program is backed by U.S. Treasuries, which adds a layer of financial security and credibility to the initiative. By anchoring the digital currency to a stable asset, the project aims to mitigate the volatility often associated with cryptocurrencies. This strategic backing is crucial for building trust among participants and encouraging broader adoption of the digital currency.

The importance of this initiative extends beyond the Marshall Islands, as it could serve as a model for other countries exploring similar solutions. The successful implementation of blockchain technology in public finance can pave the way for innovative approaches to economic support, especially in regions that struggle with poverty and financial exclusion.
