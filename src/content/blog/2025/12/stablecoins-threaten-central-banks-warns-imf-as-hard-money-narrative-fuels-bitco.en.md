## Overview

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) has raised concerns regarding dollar-backed stablecoins potentially undermining local currencies and eroding central banks' control over domestic liquidity. This situation has intensified interest in Bitcoin and related infrastructure, particularly specialized Layer 2 solutions that enhance Bitcoin's usability in decentralized finance (DeFi) and other applications.

## IMF's Concerns About Stablecoins

The IMF's recent report highlights the risks posed by dollar stablecoins to weaker local currencies. The report questions the implications if a digital dollar becomes widely accessible on smartphones, potentially affecting currencies such as the Peruvian sol, Nigerian naira, and Turkish lira. While the report acknowledges the benefits of stablecoins—such as faster payments and improved user experience—it emphasizes a significant macroeconomic narrative: the rising demand for scarce, non-sovereign assets like Bitcoin.

This narrative positions Bitcoin not just as a speculative investment but as a potential hedge against inflationary pressures associated with fiat currencies. The growing reliance on digital dollars has shifted focus from mere price appreciation to the development of infrastructure that enhances Bitcoin's functionality as a neutral reserve asset.

## The Rise of Bitcoin Layer 2 Infrastructure

The IMF's warnings signal a fragmentation of monetary power, where users now have choices between local fiat, dollar-backed tokens, and non-sovereign assets like Bitcoin. This shift has drawn capital toward Bitcoin, exposing limitations in its base layer for real-world applications. Current on-chain transactions face delays with long confirmation times, fluctuating fees, and limited smart contract capabilities.

To address these challenges, various Bitcoin scaling initiatives have emerged. The Lightning Network offers off-chain payment channels for instant Bitcoin transfers, while projects like Stacks and Rootstock utilize sidechains and alternative virtual machines to bring decentralized finance into the Bitcoin ecosystem.

In this competitive landscape, Bitcoin Hyper aims to transform dormant Bitcoin liquidity into programmable capital. It is positioning itself as a leading liquidity rail by leveraging the Solana Virtual Machine (SVM) technology to enhance the Bitcoin experience.

## Bitcoin Hyper: A New Approach

Bitcoin Hyper seeks to redefine the capabilities of Bitcoin by integrating a modular Layer 2 solution that enhances transaction speed and functionality. This approach allows for the creation of complex decentralized applications (dApps) similar to those found in the Solana ecosystem. Key features of Bitcoin Hyper include:

- **Rust-based Smart Contracts**: Enabling developers to build diverse applications, including gaming, NFTs, and decentralized exchanges (DEXs).
- **Latency**: Achieving sub-second transaction finality, surpassing existing benchmarks.
- **Security**: Periodically anchoring state back to Bitcoin, reinforcing its hard-money characteristics.

The presale for Bitcoin Hyper has reportedly attracted significant interest, with substantial investments being made, indicating a strong market response.
