## Overview

Recent market activity has seen Bitcoin's price dip below $86,000 amid significant selling pressure from larger entities. Despite traders attempting to capitalize on the price drop, the overwhelming volume of sales has raised questions about Bitcoin's ability to maintain its current price level.

## What Happened

Data indicates that traders were actively buying during the recent dip in Bitcoin's price. However, this buying effort was significantly overshadowed by substantial selling activity from larger market participants. Specifically, $2.78 billion worth of Bitcoin was sold by these larger entities, which created a considerable imbalance in the market dynamics. 

This selling pressure has sparked concerns among traders about Bitcoin's ability to hold above the critical price point of $86,000. The actions of larger entities often have a pronounced impact on market sentiment and can lead to increased volatility. As a result, even though there were active dip buyers, the sheer volume of selling from these larger players has dominated the market, raising doubts about the sustainability of the recent price levels.

The current situation highlights the challenges faced by retail investors in the cryptocurrency market, especially when larger players decide to liquidate significant portions of their holdings. The market's reaction to such events can be swift and dramatic, often resulting in rapid price fluctuations that can catch many traders off guard.
