## Overview

Tassat has secured a U.S. patent for its innovative 'Yield-in-Transit' on-chain settlement technology. This new intellectual property is significant as it facilitates intraday, block-by-block interest accrual during continuous settlement operations. The technology is foundational to Lynq, an institutional network that Tassat co-launched in July.

## What Happened

Tassat's recent patent acquisition marks a notable advancement in the cryptocurrency and blockchain space. The 'Yield-in-Transit' on-chain settlement technology enables financial transactions to accrue interest in real-time while facilitating settlements around the clock. The ability to manage interest accrual on an intraday basis is a crucial innovation that could enhance the efficiency of financial transactions conducted on blockchain networks.

This new technology is particularly relevant for institutional investors and traders who require timely and precise settlement processes. By allowing interest to accrue block-by-block, Tassat's system offers a more dynamic approach to handling transactions, catering to the demands of a fast-paced trading environment.

The launch of Lynq, the institutional network co-founded by Tassat, further emphasizes the importance of this patent. Lynq aims to connect institutional players with a reliable platform for executing and settling transactions, thereby enhancing the functionality and appeal of blockchain solutions in the financial sector. The patent serves as a vital component of this network, ensuring that Tassat's offerings are both innovative and competitive in the growing landscape of digital finance.
