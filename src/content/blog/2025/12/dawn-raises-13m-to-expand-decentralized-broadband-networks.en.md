## Overview

DAWN, a decentralized wireless protocol, has successfully raised $13 million in funding to support its expansion efforts. This investment aims to enhance decentralized broadband networks in the United States and facilitate new international deployments. The initiative is backed by investors who are interested in providing a user-owned alternative to traditional internet service providers.

## What Happened

The recent funding round for DAWN underscores a growing interest in decentralized internet solutions. As traditional internet providers face criticism for their pricing and service models, DAWN is positioning itself as a viable alternative. The protocol focuses on creating a broadband network that empowers users by allowing them to own and operate their internet infrastructure. This user-centric approach aims to foster a more equitable and accessible internet experience.

DAWN's plans include expanding its services within the United States while also exploring opportunities in international markets. The goal is to build a robust network that can compete with established internet service providers, which often dominate the market with limited options for consumers. By leveraging decentralized technology, DAWN seeks to disrupt the existing internet landscape and provide users with more control over their connectivity.

Investors have shown a keen interest in DAWN's vision, recognizing the potential for decentralized networks to revolutionize the way people access the internet. The funding will likely facilitate the development of technology and infrastructure necessary for DAWN to implement its expansion plans effectively.
