## Overview

Stellar has recently generated a Tom Demark (TD) Sequential buy signal, indicating a potential reversal in its price trend. This development has drawn attention due to the previous similar occurrence that led to a significant price increase.

## What Happened

A cryptocurrency analyst highlighted the emergence of a TD Sequential buy signal for Stellar, specifically observed in its weekly price chart. The TD Sequential is a technical analysis indicator designed to identify potential reversal points in an asset's price movement. This indicator operates in two distinct phases: the setup and the countdown.

During the setup phase, the TD Sequential counts candles of the same color until it reaches nine. If the candles leading up to this count are red, the signal is considered bullish, suggesting a potential upward price movement. Conversely, if the candles are green, the signal is seen as bearish. Following the setup phase, the countdown phase begins, continuing the count up to 13 candles, which can also indicate a price reversal.

For Stellar, the recent TD Sequential setup has completed the first phase, showing nine red candles. This pattern suggests that the ongoing downtrend may be nearing exhaustion, potentially paving the way for a bullish reversal. The analyst referenced a chart demonstrating this TD Sequential configuration in Stellar's weekly price.

Notably, this is not the first instance of the TD Sequential buy signal appearing at similar price levels for Stellar. A comparable signal was noted in March, and that setup ultimately resulted in a price surge of 95%. While the previous signal did not lead to an immediate rally, it is important to consider the consolidation period that followed before the breakout occurred.
