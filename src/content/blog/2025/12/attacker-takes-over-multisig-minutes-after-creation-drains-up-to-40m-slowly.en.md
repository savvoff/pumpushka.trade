## Overview

A recent forensic analysis has revealed that an attacker gained control of a whale's multisig wallet just minutes after its creation. Since then, the attacker has been methodically draining funds from the wallet, with estimates suggesting that up to $40 million may have been compromised.

## Incident Details

The incident underscores a significant vulnerability in the security of multisig wallets, which are typically designed to require multiple signatures for transactions, thereby providing an added layer of protection against unauthorized access. However, the analysis indicates that the attacker was able to bypass these security measures almost immediately after the wallet's establishment. 

This rapid takeover raises critical questions about the effectiveness of the security protocols in place for creating and managing multisig wallets. The fact that the attacker has been draining funds gradually rather than executing a single large withdrawal may suggest a strategy aimed at avoiding detection while still extracting significant value from the compromised wallet.

The implications of this incident extend beyond the immediate loss of funds. It highlights potential weaknesses in the multisig setup and the need for enhanced security practices in the cryptocurrency space. As the crypto market continues to evolve, incidents like this serve as cautionary tales for both individual investors and institutions regarding the importance of robust security measures.
