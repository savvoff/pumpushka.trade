## What Happened

In the early U.S. trading session, Bitcoin's price surged past the $90,000 threshold, marking a notable milestone in its market performance. This upward movement can be linked to a combination of external economic influences. Notably, the prices of metals have been on the rise, which often correlates with increased investor interest in alternative assets like cryptocurrencies.

Additionally, comments from Chris Waller, a leading contender for the position of Federal Reserve chair, are believed to have played a role in boosting crypto prices. Waller's remarks were characterized as dovish, which typically suggests a more accommodative monetary policy stance. Such a position can lead to greater liquidity in the market, encouraging investors to allocate funds toward riskier assets, including cryptocurrencies.

The interplay between rising commodities prices and dovish monetary policy perspectives creates an environment that is generally favorable for cryptocurrencies. Investors often look for alternative stores of value during times of inflation or economic uncertainty, and Bitcoin is frequently viewed as a hedge against such conditions.
