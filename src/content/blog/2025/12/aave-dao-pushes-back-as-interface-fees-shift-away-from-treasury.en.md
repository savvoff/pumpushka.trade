## Overview

The Aave Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) recently faced a significant change regarding its interface fees. While this shift was presented as an upgrade intended to enhance execution, it raised concerns among delegates about the implications for the Aave DAO treasury.

## What Happened

The Aave DAO, responsible for overseeing the governance and management of the Aave protocol, has implemented changes to how interface fees are handled. This modification was characterized as an upgrade, suggesting improvements in the execution of transactions on the platform. However, delegates within the DAO have expressed their apprehensions regarding a crucial aspect of this change: the fact that fees generated from swaps are no longer being directed to the Aave DAO treasury.

The treasury plays a vital role in the Aave ecosystem, as it is responsible for funding various initiatives, governance proposals, and community projects. The shift in fee allocation raises questions about the financial sustainability of the DAO, as the removal of these fees could affect its ability to support future developments and maintain operational functionality.

Concerns over the treasury's health have prompted discussions among delegates about the potential long-term implications of this change. The delegates are tasked with ensuring the Aave protocol remains robust and capable of adapting to market conditions while also safeguarding the interests of its community members. The removal of swap-related fees from the treasury could limit the DAO's financial flexibility and its capacity to respond effectively to emerging challenges.

## From author

The decision to change the fee structure within the Aave DAO highlights the ongoing debate within decentralized finance (DeFi) communities regarding the balance between innovation and financial sustainability. While upgrades that enhance execution are generally welcomed, they must be carefully weighed against their potential impact on the organization's financial health. The Aave DAO's situation serves as a reminder of the complexities involved in governance and the necessity for transparent communication among stakeholders.

The delegates' concerns reflect a broader sentiment within the DeFi space, where the sustainability of protocols is increasingly under scrutiny. As projects evolve, maintaining a clear focus on financial viability becomes crucial for fostering trust and engagement within the community.
