## Overview

The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) has released a guide focused on the fundamentals of cryptocurrency custody. This document serves as an introductory resource for understanding crypto custody, outlining best practices and potential risks associated with various wallet storage methods.

## What Happened

The SEC's newly published guide provides a comprehensive overview of crypto custody, detailing essential information for both individuals and institutions involved in cryptocurrency management. The guide is designed to inform stakeholders about the different forms of wallet storage available, ranging from hot wallets to cold storage solutions. 

In addition to explaining the types of wallets, the guide highlights common risks that users may encounter when managing cryptocurrencies. By addressing these risks, the SEC aims to foster a better understanding of the responsibilities and challenges associated with crypto custody. This initiative reflects an ongoing effort by regulatory bodies to provide clarity and direction in the rapidly evolving landscape of digital assets.

The SEC's publication comes at a time when regulatory scrutiny over the cryptocurrency market is intensifying. As more investors and institutions enter the crypto space, the need for secure and compliant custody solutions has become paramount. This guide may help to establish a baseline of knowledge regarding safe practices in managing digital assets, thereby promoting investor confidence and encouraging responsible participation in the market.
