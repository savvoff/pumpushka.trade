## Overview

Changpeng Zhao, commonly known as CZ, has recently emphasized the need for enhanced security protocols within the blockchain industry. This call to action comes in light of a significant incident where an investor suffered a loss of $50 million due to an address poisoning scheme. The proposal aims to safeguard investors and improve overall security in cryptocurrency transactions.

## Address Poisoning Incident

The incident that prompted Zhao's concerns revolves around a sophisticated scam known as address poisoning. In this scheme, malicious actors exploit vulnerabilities in blockchain systems to redirect funds from unsuspecting investors. By manipulating the information associated with a particular address, scammers can effectively deceive users into sending their assets to fraudulent addresses.

CZ's proposal for the implementation of a scam address blacklist seeks to combat such fraudulent activities. This measure would involve creating a comprehensive list of known scam addresses that can be shared across the blockchain ecosystem. By doing so, it would enable wallets and exchanges to automatically flag or block transactions linked to these addresses, thereby reducing the risk of investors falling victim to similar schemes in the future.
