## Overview

Dogecoin traders are once again facing speculation about a potential decline in price, with an analyst suggesting that the cryptocurrency could revisit the $0.05 to $0.06 range within the next year. This warning comes against the backdrop of Bitcoin's current market behavior, which is being closely monitored for signs of a bear market.

## Analyst's Warning

In a recent video analysis, YouTube analyst VisionPulsed shared his outlook on Dogecoin, stating that his "base case" is that the cryptocurrency may drop to the $0.05 to $0.06 zone over the next 12 months. At the time of his analysis, Dogecoin was trading around $0.140, while Bitcoin was near $92,300. VisionPulsed emphasized that if Bitcoin enters a bear market, Dogecoin could experience downward pressure without any additional catalysts to support its price.

The analyst pointed to several indicators that suggest Bitcoin might be in a bear regime, including an 8-day moving average near $102,000 and the Gaussian Channel. He identified a critical level for Bitcoin at approximately $103,000, stating that as long as Bitcoin remains below this threshold, the likelihood of Dogecoin trending downward is significant.

In his video, VisionPulsed discussed the potential for "chop zones," a term he uses to describe periods where traders face volatility and uncertainty, often leading to losses as they attempt to capitalize on price fluctuations. He highlighted that even during Bitcoin's relief rallies in the past, Dogecoin has often printed lower lows, indicating a weak correlation between the two assets.

VisionPulsed also mentioned that there are no guarantees Dogecoin will participate in any potential Bitcoin relief rally. He compared the current market conditions to previous cycles, noting that the timing framework often predicts a significant rally around 140 to 150 days after a major market top. He argued that once Bitcoin enters a specific channel regime, it tends to remain there until the broader downtrend has run its course.
