## Overview

Coinbase has appointed George Osborne, the former UK finance minister, as the chair of its advisory council. This decision follows Osborne's role as an advisor to the cryptocurrency exchange since 2024. His critical stance on the UK government's approach to digital asset regulations highlights the increasing focus on regulatory frameworks in the cryptocurrency sector.

## Appointment of George Osborne

George Osborne's appointment as chair of Coinbase's advisory council marks a significant move for the cryptocurrency exchange as it seeks to enhance its influence and navigate the complex landscape of digital asset regulations. Osborne has been involved with Coinbase since 2024, offering insights and advice, which suggests that the company values his experience and perspective on financial matters.

Osborne's tenure as the UK finance minister has equipped him with a deep understanding of economic policies and regulatory environments. His previous role in government allows him to bring a wealth of knowledge to Coinbase, particularly as the company aims to establish itself in a rapidly evolving market.

## Criticism of UK Digital Asset Regulations

During his advisory period with Coinbase, Osborne has been vocal about the UK government's shortcomings regarding digital asset regulations. His criticisms reflect a broader concern among industry stakeholders about the need for comprehensive and robust regulations to foster a secure environment for digital assets. The lack of clear guidelines can create uncertainty for businesses operating in the crypto space, potentially hindering innovation and investment.

Osborne's insights into the regulatory landscape are particularly relevant as governments around the world grapple with how to approach digital currencies and blockchain technology. His position at Coinbase allows him to advocate for more effective regulatory measures, which could benefit not only the exchange but also the broader cryptocurrency ecosystem.
