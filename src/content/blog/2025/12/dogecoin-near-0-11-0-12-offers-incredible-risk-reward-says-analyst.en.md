## Overview

Crypto analyst Matt Hughes, known as “The Great Mattsby,” has highlighted a specific price range for Dogecoin that he considers to offer an “incredible” risk/reward scenario. His analysis focuses on the $0.11–$0.12 zone, suggesting that this area serves as a significant support level for the cryptocurrency. This perspective has sparked debate among traders regarding the quality of Dogecoin as a trading setup.

## Analysis of the Situation

In a recent post, Hughes shared a weekly DOGE/USDT chart, emphasizing the $0.11–$0.12 area as a critical support level. He referred to this price range as a "line in the sand" on a weekly timeframe, indicating that it has historically served as a strong foundation for the asset. Currently, Dogecoin is trading around $0.1236, slightly above a defined horizontal band that extends just over $0.10. This horizontal band has acted as a significant support zone in the past, particularly during the prolonged downturn experienced from 2022 to 2023.

Hughes' analysis incorporates a linear-scale Gann Square, which includes multiple rising diagonal guides. He identifies a key upward-sloping support line that has been in place from the early history of the chart, projecting into 2026. This support line is converging with the $0.11–$0.12 horizontal band, creating a confluence of support that traders often look for when establishing risk parameters. If this confluence holds, it may indicate that the market has the potential to move back into higher price levels. However, if the support fails, traders may need to reevaluate their positions as the next visible support levels are significantly lower, within the $0.05–$0.07 range.

The debate surrounding Dogecoin's trading potential has intensified, with another trader suggesting that investors might be better off selecting a more favorable chart setup rather than relying on Dogecoin. Hughes acknowledged this critique but maintained that the clearly defined risk associated with the $0.11–$0.12 zone makes it a viable option for traders.

Moreover, Hughes pointed out a broader market sentiment regarding altcoins, suggesting that potential rotations often occur when market conviction is low and positioning is defensive. He referenced insights from another trader, indicating that significant market shifts can happen quietly and uncomfortably before confidence returns.
