## Overview

Bubblemaps has raised concerns regarding the fair launch of the PEPE token, alleging that a significant portion of the genesis supply was controlled by a single entity. This revelation could have implications for the token's perceived legitimacy and the broader market dynamics.

## Allegations of Token Bundling

According to Bubblemaps, approximately 30% of the genesis supply of the PEPE token was bundled under a single entity. This situation is noteworthy, as it suggests that a substantial chunk of the initial token distribution was not as decentralized as one might expect from a fair launch. 

The implications of such bundling are significant. When a single entity holds a large proportion of a token’s supply, it raises questions about the potential for market manipulation and the true distribution of tokens among the community. This centralized control can lead to a lack of confidence among investors and participants who are typically drawn to decentralized projects for their perceived fairness and transparency.

In addition to the bundling claims, Bubblemaps pointed out that this entity sold $2 million worth of PEPE tokens the day after the launch. Such rapid liquidation of tokens by a major holder could be interpreted as a sign of a lack of commitment to the project or as a strategy to capitalize on early price movements. This further complicates the narrative surrounding the token’s launch and raises concerns about the motivations of those behind the project.
