The prediction market on Crypto.com has become a focal point for sports betting enthusiasts. In this environment, the ability to place bets quickly can be the difference between winning and losing. Recent changes have indicated that professional bettors, commonly referred to as "the pros," now have a three-second advantage over retail bettors when it comes to placing bets on upcoming events.

The implications of this advantage are significant, as it could lead to a more challenging betting environment for casual participants who may not be able to compete on the same level.

## Why It Matters

The advantage that professionals have in the prediction market raises questions about fairness and accessibility in sports betting. For retail bettors, the three-second delay could result in missed opportunities, especially in fast-paced betting scenarios where odds can change rapidly. This situation could discourage casual bettors from participating in prediction markets, ultimately affecting the overall vibrancy and competitiveness of the betting landscape.

Additionally, the emergence of such advantages may influence how betting platforms design their systems and interfaces. If professionals continue to gain an edge, it could lead to a shift in user trust and engagement, as retail participants may feel increasingly marginalized. This development may prompt calls for regulatory scrutiny and changes to ensure a more level playing field.
