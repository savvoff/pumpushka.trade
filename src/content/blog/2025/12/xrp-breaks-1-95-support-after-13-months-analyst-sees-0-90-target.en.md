## Overview

XRP has experienced a significant decline, dropping below the $1.95 support level that had been a stabilizing factor for the cryptocurrency over the past year. This development has raised concerns among analysts, with one suggesting a potential target of $0.90. The breakdown is seen as a crucial moment for XRP's price trajectory.

## Recent Price Movement

XRP has slipped below the $1.95 level, which has acted as a crucial support point for approximately 13 months. This breach is significant as it marks the first time XRP has closed below this key price on a higher timeframe, specifically noted on the two-week chart. The analyst Guy on the Earth emphasized this point, indicating that the breach of this monthly support level could have serious implications for the asset's future price movement.

According to the analyst, the implications of this breakdown suggest a potential downside target of $0.90. He urged traders and holders to make informed decisions based on this analysis, highlighting the importance of reclaiming the $1.95 level to invalidate the bearish outlook. The $1.95 level is not just a mid-range price point, but rather the lower boundary of a broader consolidation pattern, making its loss particularly concerning.

For those holding XRP, the analyst offered a risk management strategy. He suggested that if holders are uncomfortable with the current market dynamics, they might consider selling to mitigate potential losses. Conversely, he noted that those who believe in XRP's long-term potential could continue to accumulate on the way down, with suggested buy zones at $1.61, $1.42, and the $0.90 target.

Despite the bearish framing, the analyst cautioned against assuming a swift decline to these targets. He acknowledged that while a direct path downward had occurred in recent weeks, it is unlikely that all marked levels would be reached immediately. 

The discussion around XRP's price movement has generated differing opinions among market participants. Some, like the account XRP whale, challenged the significance of the two-week chart timeframe, suggesting that it may not accurately represent a structural breakdown. In contrast, Guy maintained that the breakdown is based on higher-timeframe support, asserting that the $1.95 level had held for an extended period before the recent breach.

Furthermore, some market observers attempted to interpret the bearish target of $0.90 as a potential precursor to a bullish reversal. They argued that prices below $1 could be short-lived and may set the stage for a stronger upward movement beyond previous all-time highs.
