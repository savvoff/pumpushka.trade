## Overview

XRP has faced challenges in gaining upward momentum recently, with its price fluctuating around the $2 mark after a rejection above $2.15 earlier in the week. A technical analysis shared by a crypto analyst suggests that XRP's current price behavior mirrors a significant phase from its 2016 market cycle, which could indicate a forthcoming substantial rally.

## Current Price Action and Historical Context

According to the analysis from ChartNerd, XRP's present market structure is reminiscent of late 2016, when the cryptocurrency experienced a similar rejection from an accumulation supply block, leading to an ABC corrective move. This earlier correction resulted in a sharp 69% decline, which extended into the first quarter of 2017. During that time, XRP's price fell considerably, reaching lows of $0.00240. However, this decline was not the end of the bullish cycle; rather, it marked the conclusion of a correction phase.

The recent price action of XRP has shown consecutive monthly red candles, with closes consistently below opens. Currently, XRP is around 44% down from its all-time high of $3.65. The analysis suggests that if history is to repeat itself, an additional 69% correction could bring XRP's price below $1, potentially as low as $0.8. This anticipated decline is projected to occur into the first quarter of 2026.

## Technical Analysis Insights

The technical analysis highlights a rejection pattern forming in XRP's price, similar to what was observed in 2016. The current price of XRP is around $2.04, and a further drop below $1 would represent a 51% decrease from its current value. While such a significant pullback might seem unlikely, especially in light of the inflows into Spot XRP ETFs, the analysis frames this potential decline as a structural reset rather than a definitive bearish trend.

In 2017, following a similar correction, XRP underwent a consolidation phase that set the stage for one of its most explosive rallies, yielding gains exceeding 110,000%. Should the current sequence unfold as anticipated, a bullish opportunity could materialize later in 2026.
