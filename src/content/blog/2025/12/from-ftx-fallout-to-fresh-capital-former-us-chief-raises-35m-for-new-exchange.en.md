## Overview

Brett Harrison, the former president of FTX US, has successfully raised $35 million for his new venture, Architect Financial Technologies. The funding aims to establish an institutional trading platform that will encompass a variety of asset classes, including cryptocurrencies, equities, and futures.

## What Happened

After the collapse of FTX, a major cryptocurrency exchange, Harrison has pivoted to launch Architect Financial Technologies. The company’s objective is to create a comprehensive trading platform that serves institutional investors. The platform will not only focus on cryptocurrency trading but will also incorporate equities and futures, aiming to provide a holistic trading experience for its users.

The $35 million in funding is a significant milestone for Harrison and his team as they embark on this new venture. The capital will be utilized to develop the platform's infrastructure and technology, ensuring that it meets the demands of institutional traders. The involvement of institutional investors in the crypto space has been a topic of interest, especially following the FTX incident, which raised concerns about the regulatory environment and the safety of digital assets.

The establishment of Architect Financial Technologies and its focus on creating a multi-asset trading platform reflects a broader trend in the financial sector, where there is a growing convergence between traditional finance and cryptocurrency. By catering to institutional investors, the new platform aims to bridge the gap between these two worlds.
