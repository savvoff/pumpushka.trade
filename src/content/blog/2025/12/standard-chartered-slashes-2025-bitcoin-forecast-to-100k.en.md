## Overview

Standard Chartered has made a significant adjustment to its Bitcoin price forecast for 2025, reducing it by half. This decision is attributed to the cessation of corporate buying and a notable decline in ETF inflows.

## Price Target Reduction

Standard Chartered, a prominent banking institution, has revised its Bitcoin price target for 2025 from a previously higher figure to $100,000. This alteration reflects the bank's assessment of current market dynamics and investor behavior. The primary factors influencing this decision include the end of substantial corporate purchases of Bitcoin and a marked slowdown in the inflow of capital into exchange-traded funds (ETFs) that focus on cryptocurrency.

The cessation of corporate buying is particularly impactful, as institutional investors have played a crucial role in the cryptocurrency market in recent years. Their participation has been a driving force behind price increases and market stability. The reduction in corporate interest suggests a shift in sentiment among large-scale investors, which could have broader implications for the market as a whole.

Moreover, the slowdown in ETF inflows indicates a decrease in retail and institutional interest in Bitcoin through traditional investment vehicles. ETFs have become a popular way for investors to gain exposure to Bitcoin without directly purchasing the cryptocurrency. A decline in this area could signal a waning enthusiasm for Bitcoin as an investment, further contributing to the downward revision of the price forecast.
