## Overview

Bitcoin is currently experiencing a recovery wave, trading above $88,000 and consolidating below the $89,000 mark. The cryptocurrency's performance is under scrutiny as traders focus on potential upward movements, with specific resistance and support levels shaping the market outlook.

## Current Market Situation

Bitcoin began its recovery wave after moving above the $87,500 threshold, indicating a positive shift in momentum. The price has established a bullish trend, forming a trend line with support at $87,900 on the hourly chart. This trend line and the position above the 100 hourly Simple Moving Average suggest that Bitcoin could continue its upward trajectory if it maintains stability above certain critical levels.

As Bitcoin approached the $90,500 resistance, it encountered a high of $90,552 before entering a phase of consolidation. Following this peak, there was a minor decline, causing the price to dip below $89,000 and $88,500. This decline also saw Bitcoin fall below the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level of the upward movement from a swing low of $84,420 to the recent high of $90,552.

Currently, Bitcoin is trading above $87,800, with immediate resistance identified near the $89,100 level. The first key resistance is located at $89,500, followed by the critical $90,500 level. A successful close above $90,500 could potentially lead to further gains, with the next targets being $92,000 and $92,500. If the bullish momentum continues, traders may look to the $93,200 and $93,500 levels as additional barriers for upward movement.

Conversely, if Bitcoin fails to surpass the $89,500 resistance, it may initiate another decline. Immediate support is positioned around $88,000, with significant support levels at $87,500 and $86,750, which also aligns with the 61.8% Fibonacci retracement level from the previous upward movement. Should the price drop further, it may approach the $85,450 support level, with the main support resting at $84,500. A breach below this level could accelerate downward movement in the near term.
