## Overview

Bitcoin Treasury Company Twenty One made its debut on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) today, trading under the ticker XXI. The company, which is led by Strike CEO Jack Mallers, experienced a significant drop of 25% in its initial trading session. This decline brings the company's trading price close to the PIPE pricing of $10, which reflects the price at which private investors purchased shares during the merger process.

## What Happened

The trading session for Bitcoin Treasury Company Twenty One commenced following its merger with Cantor Equity Partners, a process commonly known as a SPAC merger. SPACs, or Special Purpose Acquisition Companies, are investment vehicles that raise capital through an initial public offering (IPO) with the intention of acquiring a private company and bringing it public. In this case, the merger allowed Bitcoin Treasury Company to enter the public market.

Despite the anticipation surrounding the company's entry into the NYSE, it faced a challenging debut. The 25% drop in share price indicates a lack of immediate investor confidence, which can be attributed to various factors, including market conditions, investor sentiment, or the overall performance of the cryptocurrency sector at the time of the listing.

The PIPE pricing of $10 serves as a benchmark for evaluating the company's market performance post-merger. PIPE, or Private Investment in Public Equity, typically involves institutional or accredited investors purchasing shares at a discount to the market price. The fact that Twenty One's share price is nearing this level could suggest that investors are cautious about the company's future prospects or that broader market dynamics are at play.
