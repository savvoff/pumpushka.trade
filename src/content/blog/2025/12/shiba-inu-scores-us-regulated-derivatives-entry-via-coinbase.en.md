## Overview

Coinbase has recently introduced regulated futures linked to Shiba Inu, allowing the token to be traded on a US derivatives platform. This launch marks a significant step in Coinbase's strategy to expand its offerings of altcoin derivatives under US regulations.

## Regulated Futures Market Launch

According to reports, on December 5, 2025, Coinbase began rolling out futures products that include perpetual-style contracts and monthly futures based on what the exchange refers to as the "1k SHIB index," which consists of 1,000 Shiba Inu tokens. These trading products are available 24/7, providing greater accessibility to traders. The perpetual contracts, while resembling offshore swaps in structure, are offered through a regulated platform and are designed with a funding-rate mechanism to align prices closely with the spot market. 

The initial phase of this rollout included monthly futures contracts, with clearing and settlement processes managed within systems that adhere to US regulatory oversight. The products have been described as compliant with the frameworks established by the Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC).

## Implications for Traders and Institutions

The introduction of regulated futures is expected to influence the trading dynamics of Shiba Inu. Market participants suggest that the availability of these futures can alter the profile of traders who engage with the token. Institutional investors and large funds often prefer regulated venues, as they provide clearer custody paths and enhance confidence in trading practices. 

The increased liquidity and continuous pricing associated with these futures contracts may attract more active traders, potentially leading to heightened trading volumes. However, it is also important to note that access to futures allows for the possibility of shorting the token, which could contribute to increased volatility in its price movements. Reports indicate that the immediate reactions in spot markets have been mixed, suggesting that the mere availability of derivatives does not guarantee an increase in the token's price.

Furthermore, with the introduction of regulated futures on Coinbase, Shiba Inu qualifies for consideration for a spot ETF under the same regulatory pathway that Bitcoin and Ethereum have utilized.
