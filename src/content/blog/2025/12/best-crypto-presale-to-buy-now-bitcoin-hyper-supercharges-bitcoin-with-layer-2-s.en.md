## Overview

Bitcoin Hyper is introducing a new Layer 2 solution designed to enhance Bitcoin's scalability and usability. By integrating Solana Virtual Machine (SVM) technology, it aims to provide faster transactions and lower fees, addressing the limitations of Bitcoin's current infrastructure.

## What Happened

Bitcoin’s Layer 1 network is known for its security; however, it faces challenges such as slow transaction throughput, high fees, and a lack of native smart contract capabilities. These limitations hinder the use of Bitcoin for payments, decentralized finance (DeFi), and other on-chain activities. As the demand for a system that combines Bitcoin's trustworthiness with improved speed has grown, Bitcoin Hyper has emerged to fill this gap.

Bitcoin Hyper plans to launch a high-performance Layer 2 solution that leverages SVM integration. This enables faster transaction execution for wrapped Bitcoin, facilitating payments, DeFi, non-fungible tokens (NFTs), and more. By offering ultra-low-latency processing and SVM smart contracts, Bitcoin Hyper seeks to transform Bitcoin from a passive asset into a productive one that can be utilized across various decentralized applications (dApps), gaming, and high-speed on-chain ecosystems.

Historically, Bitcoin has established itself as a premier reserve asset, but its practical use has lagged due to slow confirmations, variable fees, and the absence of smart contracts. This has led to a growing ecosystem around other blockchains like Solana and Ethereum, which have attracted users seeking faster, cheaper, and programmable monetary solutions.

Bitcoin Hyper is built on the premise that Bitcoin should not have to choose between being sound money and usable money. It aims to convert Bitcoin's traditional limitations into a high-throughput Layer 2 solution, allowing for near-instant transactions at minimal costs while maintaining Bitcoin's security.
