## Overview

The recent Lighter airdrop has made headlines, with a staggering $675 million worth of LIT tokens distributed. This event marks it as one of the largest airdrops in the history of cryptocurrency, according to data from Bubblemaps. Notably, around 75% of the recipients are reported to still hold their tokens, indicating a strong retention rate among those who participated in the airdrop.

## What Happened

Bubblemaps reported that the Lighter project executed an airdrop worth $675 million, making it one of the most significant distributions in the crypto space. This airdrop's scale highlights the ongoing trend of projects utilizing such strategies to engage users and expand their communities. The LIT tokens were distributed to a wide audience, emphasizing the project's intention to reach as many potential users as possible.

The data shared on X indicates that a substantial portion of the recipients, approximately 75%, have chosen to retain their LIT tokens rather than selling them immediately after the airdrop. This retention may suggest confidence in the project or the belief that the value of LIT will increase over time, reflecting a positive sentiment among the community.

Airdrops have become a common tactic for projects to distribute tokens widely, rewarding early adopters and fostering community engagement.
