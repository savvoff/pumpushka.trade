## Overview

Ethereum has faced significant challenges in its attempt to reclaim the crucial $3,000 price point, with the cryptocurrency struggling over the past 48 hours. This situation has raised concerns about the potential for further declines if the support level is not regained soon.

## Current Market Situation

As Ethereum continues to hover just above $2,940, analysts are expressing concerns about the cryptocurrency's trajectory. Market analyst Ted Pillows highlighted on social media that without a swift recovery above the $3,000 mark, Ethereum could experience additional downside pressure. This scenario suggests a possible drop toward the $2,800 range, indicating an approximate 5% retracement from its current trading price. The ongoing struggle is further underscored by a 16% decline recorded over the past month, raising alarms for the broader cryptocurrency market.

Another analyst, Columbus, examined Ethereum's performance in relation to Bitcoin. He noted that Ethereum has been trading below its Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP), which is a critical metric for assessing the asset's overall price performance. The recent price bounce observed from the $2,800 to $2,850 range appears to be more reactive than indicative of a strong rally, suggesting that while there is buying interest in Ethereum, the conviction to sustain this rally is weak.

Columbus also pointed out that there is considerable liquidity layered overhead, particularly in the $3,050 to $3,250 zone. This liquidity has effectively capped any attempts to push prices higher. Analysts suggest that unless Ethereum can reclaim this area and achieve consistent acceptance above it, any upward movements may only reflect short-term rotations into supply, rather than a genuine continuation of an upward trend. On the downside, if Ethereum fails to maintain the $2,850 mark, it could open the door to deeper losses, potentially leading to price levels between $2,400 and $2,700, where a significant concentration of liquidity exists.
