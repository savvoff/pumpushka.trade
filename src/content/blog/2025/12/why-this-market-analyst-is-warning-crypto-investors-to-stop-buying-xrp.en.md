## Overview

Recent analysis from a prominent crypto analyst has raised alarms regarding the potential for a significant downturn in the price of XRP. The analyst has identified a critical technical pattern that has historically preceded substantial price declines, advising traders and investors to refrain from purchasing the cryptocurrency at this time.

## Analysis of Current XRP Market Conditions

The warning comes from market analyst Steph is Crypto, who has publicly stated that traders and investors should "not touch XRP anymore." This caution is based on the observation of a bearish crossover forming in the monthly Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) indicator for XRP. This technical signal has only occurred twice since the cryptocurrency's inception in 2012, each time leading to dramatic price declines.

In previous instances, when the bearish crossover emerged, XRP experienced significant losses. The first crossover took place in 2019, resulting in a price crash of over 84%. The second occurrence happened in 2022, which triggered a decline of approximately 67%. Notably, both of these bearish signals followed major bull markets, indicating a trend where bullish momentum was subsequently followed by sharp corrections.

The analyst also highlighted that the current bearish crossover could signify another impending downturn. He expressed regret at having identified this pattern, as he typically holds a bullish outlook on XRP. This situation has prompted him to urge caution among traders, emphasizing the historical implications of such a setup.

In addition to the bearish indicators, XRP's price momentum appears weak. The cryptocurrency's current price is slightly above $2.00, having dropped over 15% in the current month alone. Further declines include a 2.2% decrease over the past week and a year-to-date drop of approximately 16%. The Fear and Greed Index for XRP has also fallen to 42, moving closer to the "fear" zone, which reflects growing market uncertainty.

## From author
