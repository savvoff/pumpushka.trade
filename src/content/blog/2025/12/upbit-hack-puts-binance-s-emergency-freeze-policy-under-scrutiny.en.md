## Overview

A recent incident involving the Upbit hack has brought attention to Binance's emergency freeze policy. Reports indicate that Binance froze a small fraction of the funds associated with the hack, prompting discussions about the effectiveness of major exchanges in responding to cross-border law enforcement requests.

## Incident Details

The Upbit hack, which has garnered significant media attention, involved a substantial amount of funds being stolen. South Korean police have been actively investigating the incident, seeking to recover the lost assets. As part of their investigation, they approached Binance to assist in freezing the funds that were believed to be linked to the hack.

However, it has been reported that Binance only froze approximately 17% of the funds that the South Korean police identified as connected to the Upbit hack. This limited action has raised concerns regarding how effectively major cryptocurrency exchanges cooperate with law enforcement agencies across different countries.

The situation highlights the challenges that arise in the cryptocurrency space, particularly when it comes to cross-border transactions and the enforcement of laws. Cryptocurrency exchanges often operate in a decentralized manner, which can complicate the process of tracking and recovering stolen assets. This incident underscores the necessity for clearer protocols and stronger cooperation between exchanges and law enforcement.

## From author

The limited freezing of funds raises questions not only about the efficacy of Binance's policies but also about the broader implications for security and trust in the cryptocurrency market.
