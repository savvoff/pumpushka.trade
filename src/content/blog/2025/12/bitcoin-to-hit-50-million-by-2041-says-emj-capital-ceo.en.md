## Overview

Eric Jackson, CEO of EMJ Capital, has presented an ambitious long-term projection for Bitcoin, suggesting it could reach $50 million per coin by 2041. This forecast is based on his belief that Bitcoin will transition from its current status as "digital gold" to a fundamental collateral layer within the global financial system.

## Jackson's Vision for Bitcoin

In an interview with reporter Phil Rosen, Jackson elaborated on his thesis regarding Bitcoin's future. He draws parallels between Bitcoin and his investment strategy, particularly referencing his experience with the stock of Carvana. Jackson entered the Carvana market during a significant price decline, when negative sentiment was prevalent. He noted that market behavior often reflects extreme pessimism, where investors focus solely on immediate challenges rather than the underlying strengths of the business. 

This mindset informs Jackson's long-term perspective on Bitcoin. He argues that daily price fluctuations and divided opinions on Bitcoin's value can obscure its true potential. Jackson believes that Bitcoin's role as "digital gold" is just one aspect of its future. He questions the size of the gold market and the reasons central banks and sovereign nations hold gold, proposing that Bitcoin could eventually match or surpass gold's significance.

### The Concept of the Global Collateral Layer

Jackson emphasizes that Bitcoin's current usage as a medium for daily transactions is limited. He references the historical shift from gold to the Eurodollar system in the 1960s, noting that the current financial infrastructure is heavily linked to sovereign debt. He highlights the challenges associated with this system, suggesting that Bitcoin could serve as a more effective and neutral asset for borrowing.

In his vision, Bitcoin would replace the Eurodollar, becoming the foundational collateral layer that underpins sovereign and central bank borrowing. He argues that Bitcoin's digital nature and apolitical characteristics make it a superior form of collateral compared to traditional assets. Jackson does not perceive this as a direct challenge to the dollar or Treasury securities but rather as a new layer of support for global financial transactions.

### Future Implications

Looking toward 2041, Jackson predicts that sovereign nations will increasingly rely on Bitcoin for their debt issuance and management. He argues that this transition is logical given the vast scale of sovereign debt. If Bitcoin becomes the dominant asset for collateral, its price would need to rise significantly, which underpins his $50 million target.

## From author

Eric Jackson's perspective on Bitcoin highlights an evolving narrative within the cryptocurrency space. His comparison to traditional equity investments, such as Carvana, underscores the importance of recognizing inherent value amid market volatility. By framing Bitcoin as a potential core component of the global financial system, Jackson invites a reevaluation of how cryptocurrencies are perceived and utilized.
