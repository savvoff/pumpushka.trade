## Overview

Bitcoin experienced a significant surge, moving from an intraday low close to $86,200 to surpass the $90,000 mark. This upward movement was primarily fueled by aggressive spot buying and a notable wave of short liquidations in the market.

## What Happened

On the trading day in question, Bitcoin's price demonstrated a remarkable recovery after hitting an intraday low near $86,200. The cryptocurrency quickly gained momentum, reclaiming the $90,000 level. This climb was characterized by two main factors: aggressive spot buying from market participants and a substantial wave of short liquidations.

Aggressive spot buying refers to the immediate purchasing of Bitcoin in the market, indicating strong demand from investors. This buying pressure played a crucial role in driving the price upwards. Concurrently, the wave of short liquidations indicates that many traders who had bet against Bitcoin were forced to close their positions as the price rose. This phenomenon often exacerbates price movements in the cryptocurrency market, leading to rapid increases in value as liquidations occur.

The combination of these two factors created a powerful upward momentum for Bitcoin, leading to its recovery and surpassing the $90,000 threshold. The dynamics of spot buying and short liquidations highlight the volatility and rapid movements often seen in the cryptocurrency markets, particularly with Bitcoin, which is known for its swift price fluctuations.
