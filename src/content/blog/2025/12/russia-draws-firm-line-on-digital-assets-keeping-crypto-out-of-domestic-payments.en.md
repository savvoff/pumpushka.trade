## Overview

Russia has reaffirmed its strict stance on cryptocurrencies, maintaining a clear divide between digital assets and traditional fiat currency. Despite the growing global interest in cryptocurrencies, Russian lawmakers have reiterated that domestic payments must exclusively utilize the ruble, highlighting the country's ongoing regulatory approach to digital assets.

## Russia's Firm Stance on Cryptocurrencies

In recent statements, Russian lawmakers, led by Anatoly Aksakov, chair of the State Duma Committee on Financial Markets, have emphasized that cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin and Ethereum, are not permitted to be used as a means of payment within Russia. According to Aksakov, the law is unequivocal: all transactions for goods and services must be conducted in rubles. This position reflects provisions established in Russia's 2020 digital assets law, which explicitly excludes cryptocurrencies from being recognized as legal tender.

The rationale behind this strict regulatory framework is rooted in the belief that currency must be issued and controlled by the state. Russian officials argue that private digital currencies do not satisfy this requirement, thereby justifying their exclusion from domestic commerce. Aksakov has stated that there are no intentions to relax this stance, which applies universally across various sectors, including retail, online services, and business contracts. This policy effectively shuts down any potential for cryptocurrency payments, regardless of market trends or public adoption.

## Central Bank's Influence on Crypto Policy

The Bank of Russia plays a crucial role in shaping the country's approach to cryptocurrencies. Governor Elvira Nabiullina has consistently raised concerns regarding the risks that cryptocurrencies pose to financial stability and consumer protection. The central bank has opposed the use of digital assets as a medium of exchange and has advocated for stringent restrictions on exchanges and transactions involving cryptocurrencies.

This position has led to ongoing tensions with the Ministry of Finance, which has favored a regulatory framework that includes taxation rather than outright bans. Despite various legislative proposals emerging from this debate, no significant changes have been made to alter the fundamental prohibition on crypto payments. As of now, there seems to be a consensus among policymakers to uphold the ruble's dominance in domestic transactions.

## Cross-Border Use of Cryptocurrencies

Although the use of cryptocurrencies for internal payments is prohibited, Russian authorities recognize the increasing role of digital assets in international trade. Businesses are allowed to utilize cryptocurrencies for cross-border settlements under an experimental legal framework, which has gained popularity amid global financial sanctions. Officials have indicated that substantial trade volumes have already transitioned to these alternative channels.

Additionally, Russia has legalized cryptocurrency mining and is implementing stricter regulations in this sector. This dual approach illustrates a clear distinction in Russia's crypto policy: while digital assets can be utilized for investments or international transactions, domestic payment systems remain firmly tied to the ruble.

## From author

The ongoing developments in Russia's crypto regulatory landscape highlight a significant divergence between domestic and international attitudes towards digital assets. As the world continues to explore the potential of cryptocurrencies, Russia's rigid stance may have far-reaching implications for its economic interactions and the future of digital currencies within its borders.
