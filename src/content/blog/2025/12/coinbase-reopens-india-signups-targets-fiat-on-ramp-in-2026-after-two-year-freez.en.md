## Overview

Coinbase has announced the reopening of signups for users in India after a two-year suspension of its services in the country. This decision comes after the company halted operations entirely in 2023, off-boarding millions of Indian users and shutting down local access while it reassessed its regulatory exposure.

## Background on Coinbase's Withdrawal

In 2023, Coinbase made the significant decision to halt its services in India. This move led to the off-boarding of millions of users who were previously active on the platform. The company cited the need to reassess its regulatory exposure as a primary reason for this withdrawal. During this period, Coinbase completely shuttered local access to its services, leaving many Indian users without a platform to engage in cryptocurrency trading and transactions.

## Reopening of Signups

The recent announcement marks a pivotal moment for Coinbase as it re-enters the Indian market. The reopening of signups is a strategic move aimed at re-establishing its presence in a country with a rapidly growing interest in cryptocurrencies. This shift indicates that Coinbase is now positioned to navigate the regulatory landscape more effectively and cater to the needs of Indian users.

## Future Plans for Fiat On-Ramp

Coinbase has set its sights on establishing a fiat on-ramp in India by 2026. This plan is crucial as it aims to facilitate easier access for users to exchange traditional currency for cryptocurrencies. By providing a fiat on-ramp, Coinbase intends to enhance the user experience and promote wider adoption of digital assets among Indian citizens. This initiative reflects the company's commitment to expanding its services in regions with significant potential for growth in the cryptocurrency market.
