## Overview

A recent discussion among crypto experts has centered on the potential for XRP to reach a valuation of $10,000. One expert emphasizes that XRP operates in a unique space compared to other cryptocurrencies, suggesting that traditional valuation methods may not apply. This assertion raises questions about how we assess the value of digital assets and their roles in the financial ecosystem.

## The Case for XRP's High Valuation

Stern Drew, the founder and CEO of Stageyo, has contributed to the ongoing debate regarding XRP's future price potential. He argues that many predictions dismissing the possibility of a $10,000 XRP are based on flawed assumptions. According to Drew, these projections often apply inappropriate frameworks and mathematical models to evaluate XRP, which operates differently from retail-driven cryptocurrencies.

Drew highlights that XRP was designed to facilitate large-scale institutional transactions, setting it apart from other digital assets. He points out that a single partner of Ripple can transfer more value in one day using XRP than Bitcoin can process in an entire year. This significant difference in transaction volume is crucial to understanding how XRP's value might be assessed in the future.

He further emphasizes that XRP's primary function is institutional settlement rather than speculative trading. The core use case of XRP revolves around efficiently moving substantial amounts of capital across borders. Drew suggests that price expectations based solely on retail demand or historical trends in the crypto market do not accurately reflect XRP's potential. 

He argues that if XRP is utilized for high-value transactions, a higher price per token would enhance efficiency. This scenario would require fewer tokens to transfer the same amount of value, thus reducing friction and expediting transactions. Drew describes this concept as a "different kind of math" that applies to a distinct category of financial activity.

## XRP's Role in Global Banking

In a separate post, Drew referenced a recent announcement from the Bank of Japan (BOJ), which indicated that Japan and South Korea are collaborating on developing blockchain infrastructure. This development subtly hints at the involvement of XRP and Ripple. Although the discussions remain private, their potential impact on the crypto landscape is expected to be significant.

The BOJ's statement suggests that XRP holders should be vigilant and prepare for upcoming developments, as this collaboration could mark a transformative moment for Ripple. Notably, Ripple has already established relationships with major financial institutions in Japan, including SBI Holdings, and South Korea has historically been a significant investor in XRP.
