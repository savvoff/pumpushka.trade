This shift is attributed to a lack of sufficient downside liquidity, which typically compels market makers to initiate a significant downward price leg. This imbalance suggests a potential for a temporary upward relief move.

He stated his intention to exit this position only when the price reaches a defined target zone, which coincides with a convergence of heavy liquidity.

Importantly, Mr. Wall Street emphasized that holding long positions does not alter the overarching bearish thesis regarding Bitcoin. The prevailing sentiment remains negative, with expectations pointing toward a significant downside move in the future. Despite the current strong support and limited downside liquidity, which decreases the likelihood of an immediate price drop, the analyst outlined a scenario wherein market makers might engineer a bullish move to attract retail traders. 

As new buyers enter the market, they risk becoming "exit liquidity" for larger players, setting the stage for a more pronounced downward movement once adequate liquidity is established. Notably, the previously anticipated downside target of a specific price range was revised lower, with expectations that this level could be reached in a future quarter. This target represents an initial significant level rather than a final bottom.

Recent price movements exemplify these market dynamics, illustrated by Bitcoin's rapid fluctuations within a narrow range. Such volatility has resulted in widespread liquidations among traders who pursued the upward movement, only to be caught in a sharp downturn. The analyst warns that these deceptive price movements are likely to continue as liquidity builds in preparation for a more substantial downward shift.
