## Overview

Cboe is set to launch Bitcoin and Ether "perpetual-style" crypto futures on December 15. These contracts are designed to provide long-term exposure to cryptocurrencies without the need for rollovers, making them particularly appealing to institutional investors.

## Details of the Launch

The introduction of these perpetual-style futures contracts by Cboe aims to meet the needs of institutions looking for a way to engage with cryptocurrencies while minimizing offshore risks. By eliminating the rollover requirement, these contracts allow investors to maintain their positions over an extended period, which can be advantageous for managing exposure to market fluctuations.

## Importance of the Contracts

The launch of these contracts signifies a growing trend in the cryptocurrency market, where institutional interest continues to expand. Institutions often seek products that can offer stability and ease of management, and the perpetual-style futures are designed with these needs in mind. This move by Cboe could potentially enhance the overall acceptance and integration of cryptocurrencies in traditional financial markets.

## Impact on the crypto market

- The introduction of perpetual-style futures may attract more institutional investors to the cryptocurrency market.
- These contracts could lead to increased trading volume and liquidity for Bitcoin and Ether.
- The absence of rollover requirements may simplify the investment process for institutions.
- The move highlights the ongoing evolution of financial products within the cryptocurrency space.
- It may encourage other exchanges to develop similar offerings, further expanding market options for investors.
