## Overview

Bitcoin Hyper is a proposed layer-2 (L2) solution designed to enhance Bitcoin's throughput and programmability. By leveraging advanced technology, it aims to facilitate fast, low-cost transactions and decentralized finance (DeFi) applications while maintaining the security of the Bitcoin network.

## What Happened

Bitcoin's limitations in transaction throughput and programmability have hindered its mainstream utility. Bitcoin Hyper addresses these constraints by utilizing a combination of Solana-style execution speed and zero-knowledge (ZK) proofs for transaction validation on the Bitcoin network. This new architecture is designed to batch transactions efficiently and periodically commit state to Bitcoin, allowing for high-throughput processing while ensuring security.

As of now, the presale for Bitcoin Hyper has raised a significant amount of funds, indicating strong interest from investors. The project's design targets key user benefits, including speed, cost efficiency, and programmability, without compromising Bitcoin's security principles.

Bitcoin Hyper's architecture includes a canonical bridge that verifies Bitcoin block headers and transaction proofs. This bridge allows for the minting of equivalent assets on the L2 platform. The execution layer aims to keep transaction fees low while achieving high throughput, making it an attractive option for developers and users alike.

## Why It Matters

The constraints of Bitcoin have led to a lack of effective solutions for everyday payments and DeFi applications. Bitcoin Hyper seeks to fill this gap by providing a comprehensive L2 solution that can handle high-volume transactions and complex logic without sacrificing security. If successful, it could significantly expand Bitcoin's utility and attract institutional interest.

In a competitive landscape, Bitcoin Hyper distinguishes itself by offering a straightforward approach: keeping settlement on Bitcoin while using a high-throughput execution environment. This could position it favorably against existing solutions like Lightning, which focuses primarily on peer-to-peer payments.
