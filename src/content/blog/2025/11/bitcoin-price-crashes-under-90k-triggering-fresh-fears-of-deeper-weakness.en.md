## Overview

Bitcoin has experienced a significant decline, dropping below the $90,000 mark. This downturn has raised concerns about potential further weakness in the cryptocurrency's price movement.

## Recent Price Movements

Bitcoin's price has recently fallen below critical levels of $92,000 and $90,000. Currently, it is trading under $90,000 and the 100 hourly Simple Moving Average, indicating bearish momentum. A bearish trend line has formed, with resistance identified at $91,000 on the hourly chart of the BTC/USD pair.

The decline began as Bitcoin struggled to maintain a positive position above $90,000. The selling pressure from bears has intensified, driving the price down past the $88,800 level and further below $87,500. A notable low was established at $85,276, with Bitcoin now consolidating losses below the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level from the recent high of $92,872 to the low of $85,276.

If the price attempts recovery, resistance levels are expected around $87,000, $89,000, and $91,000. A close above $91,000 could potentially lead to higher prices, with possible testing of the $92,500 resistance level.

## Potential for Further Declines

Should Bitcoin fail to breach the $90,000 resistance zone, it risks initiating another decline. Immediate support is positioned near $85,500, with major support levels at $85,000 and $83,200. If losses persist, the price may approach the $82,500 support level, with significant support lying at $80,000. Falling below this threshold could accelerate further declines.

## Technical Indicators

- **Hourly MACD**: Currently gaining pace in the bearish zone.
- **Hourly RSI (Relative Strength Index)**: Below the 50 level.
