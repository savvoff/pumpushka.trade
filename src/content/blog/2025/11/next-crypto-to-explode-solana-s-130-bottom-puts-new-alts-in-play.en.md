## Solana's Recovery and Institutional Interest

Recently, Solana has experienced a notable rebound from a sharp decline, bouncing back from a support level around $130 after a 25% drawdown from a higher price point. This recovery is characterized by a V-shaped pattern, which technical analysts view positively, indicating the possibility of a return to the $180–$250 range if momentum continues. 

Institutional interest remains strong, with Solana's spot ETFs attracting approximately $420 million in cumulative inflows. This trend of inflows has persisted for 15 consecutive days, suggesting that institutional investors are actively engaging in what appears to be a 'dip-buying' strategy. The derivatives market also reflects this renewed interest, with futures open interest surpassing $7 billion and funding rates turning positive, indicating that both spot and leveraged buyers are re-entering the market.

Furthermore, on-chain metrics reveal that daily active addresses on Solana have increased by 18% over the past month. This uptick supports the notion that the recent price movements are backed by genuine network usage rather than mere speculation.

## Potential for Altcoin Rotation

As Solana stabilizes at the $130 level, the environment becomes more conducive for a rotation back into high-beta altcoins. Traders are likely to explore narratives that resonate with institutional buying patterns, focusing on scalable infrastructure, real revenue opportunities, and sectors like AI, creator platforms, and decentralized finance (DeFi) perpetuals.
